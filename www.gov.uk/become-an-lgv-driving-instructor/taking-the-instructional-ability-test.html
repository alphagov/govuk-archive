Part 7: Taking the instructional ability test

To book the instructional ability test, use the application form you’re given when you pass the driving ability test.

It costs £142.80.

What to bring to your test

On the day, you’ll need to bring:

  • both parts of your driving licence - the photocard and the paper counterpart
  • the pass letter from the driving ability test
  • a suitable vehicle

You must take your signed driving licence and a valid passport if you have an old-style paper licence.

Rules for the vehicle you use

You must bring a large goods vehicle (LGV) that:

  • is roadworthy and legal
  • is a category C1, C1+E, C or C+E vehicle (depending on the instructor certificate you want)
  • has normal performance for its type
  • is not carrying any goods or burden
  • is a right-hand drive
  • has a seat for a forward-facing passenger
  • has seatbelts fitted for the examiner’s use
  • has external nearside and offside mirrors for use by the examiner during the test
  • displays ‘L’ plates during the test

How the test works

The examiner will begin by explaining which of the 9 possible pre-set exercises will be used.

The examiner will play the role of one of the following types of pupil:

  • someone learning to drive either a category C1 or C
  • someone learning to drive either a category C1+E or C+E
  • someone who has already failed LGV test and needs extra training

The examiner will drive the vehicle and simulate faults. You should treat the examiner as though they are your pupil.

You can use lesson plans and training aids, and you can bring brief notes or subject headings along with you.

What you have to do

During the test, you have to:

  • tailor your instruction to the standard of the pupil and the time available
  • give your pupil a chance to show what you’ve taught them and correct any faults
  • keep control of the lesson, be patient and tactful and encourage the pupil

What the examiner assesses

The examiner will assess you on:

  • the method of your instruction and how clear, thorough and correct it is
  • how well you observe and correct the pupil’s driving errors
  • your general manner and attitude when dealing with the pupil

Your test result

The examiner will give you the result at the end of the test.

Failing the first or second attempt

You can take the test again if you fail the test at either your first or second attempt.

Failing the third attempt

You have to retake and pass the theory test and driving ability test again if you fail the instructional ability test 3 times.

You have to wait 1 year from when you first passed the theory test before you can take it again.
