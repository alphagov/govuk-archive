Expenses and benefits: Christmas bonuses, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer providing Christmas bonuses to your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

This depends on:

  • whether you give cash bonuses or goods (gifts) to an employee
  • if you give goods to an employee, whether or not they can be resold for cash
  • whether the employee is a director, and how much they earn

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HM Revenue & Customs or pay tax and National Insurance if:

  • you give goods that can’t be resold for cash to employees who earn at a rate of less than £8,500 a year

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the Christmas bonuses you provide aren’t exempt, you may have to report them to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC), and deduct and pay tax and National Insurance (NI) on them.

Cash bonus

Any cash you give to employees as a Christmas bonus counts as earnings, so you’ll need to:

  • add the value to your employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay Pay As You Earn (PAYE) tax and Class 1 NI through payroll

Goods

If you give goods as Christmas gifts to employees, what you have to report and pay depends on:

  • whether or not they can be resold for cash
  • whether you give them to company directors or employees who earn at a rate of more than £8,500 a year
Expense What to do What to pay
Goods with no resale value you give to employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, unless these are counted as trivial benefits Fill in form P11D, section A or M Pay Class 1A NI on the value of the benefit
Goods with no resale value you give to directors, unless these are counted as trivial benefits Fill in form P11D, section A or M Pay Class 1A NI on the value of the benefit
Goods with cash resale value you give to employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Fill in form P9D, section A(2) Nothing
Goods with cash resale value you give to employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Form P11D, section A Pay Class 1A NI on the value of the benefit
Goods with cash resale value you give to directors Form P11D, section A Pay Class 1A NI on the value of the benefit

Part 4: Work out the value

The amount you need to report depends on the type of bonus you provide.

Cash bonuses

Use the amount you pay to the employee.

Goods with no resale value

Use the amount the goods cost you.

Goods with cash resale value

For employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year, use the resale value of the goods.

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, or directors, use whichever is the higher of:

  • the resale value
  • the amount the goods cost you
