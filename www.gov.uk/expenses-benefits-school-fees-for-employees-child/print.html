Expenses and benefits: school fees for an employee's child, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer paying your employees’ children’s school fees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or pay tax and National Insurance if all of the following apply:

  • the contract is between you and the school
  • you pay the fees directly
  • your employee earns at a rate of less than £8,500 a year

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the school fees aren’t exempt, you may have to report the costs to HMRC, and you may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance.

If your employee pays and you reimburse the fees

This counts as earnings, so you’ll need to:

  • add the cost of the fees to the employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance and PAYE tax through payroll

The contract is between your employee and the school, but you pay the fees directly

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Class 1 National Insurance on the cost of the fees through payroll
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance on the cost of the fees through payroll
Directors Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance on the cost of the fees through payroll

The contract is between you and the school and you pay the fees directly

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 a year, or directors:

  • report on form P11D, section M
  • pay Class 1A National Insurance on the cost of the fees
