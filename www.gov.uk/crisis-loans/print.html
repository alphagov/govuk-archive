Benefits & credits: Crisis Loans

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You can get an interest-free Crisis Loan to help with essential costs after an emergency or disaster, eg a flood or fire in your home.

There’s no set amount you can get - it depends on your circumstances. You don’t need to be on benefits to apply.

You’ll only repay what you borrow. You need to agree how and when you’ll do this with Jobcentre Plus.

If you’re on benefits, the loan can be repaid from your benefit payments.

Crisis Loans won’t affect your other benefits.

Part 2: What you'll get

There’s no set amount for a Crisis Loan and they are interest-free. How much you can borrow depends on:

  • your personal circumstances
  • any savings you have
  • if you already owe any money to the Social Fund - eg you have a Budgeting Loan

How you’re paid

Depending on the type and urgency of the loan, the Jobcentre will either pay the money into your account, eg a bank account, or you’ll collect a payment by cheque from the Jobcentre.

Part 3: Eligibility

You can apply for a Crisis Loan if you:

  • are 16 or over
  • don’t have enough money to meet your (or your family’s) immediate needs after an emergency or disaster
  • think there will be serious damage or risk to your (or your family’s) health or safety without the loan

You might get a Community Care Grant if you’re not eligible for a loan.

What can a loan pay for

Crisis Loans can pay for:

  • daily living expenses
  • rent in advance
  • board/lodging charges
  • residential charges for a hostel
  • pre-paid meter fuel debt
  • travel expenses if you’re stranded away from home

Part 4: How to claim

Living expenses and urgent rent in advance

To apply for living expenses and urgent rent in advance contact Jobcentre Plus.

Decisions are usually made on the day you apply. Payment is made within a few hours, depending on whether you’re collecting it from the Jobcentre or if it’s being paid into your bank account.

Reviewing a decision on your application

If you’re unhappy with the decision on your Crisis Loan application you’ve got the right to ask for a review. You must write to Jobcentre Plus within 28 days of the decision, explaining why you think it was wrong.

If, after the review, you still think the decision was wrong you can ask for a further review by a Social Fund Inspector. This review should take no longer than 12 days. You can download a leaflet and claim form to ask for an independent review.
