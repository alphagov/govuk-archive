Latest news

On 14 December 2012, Universities and Science Minister David Willetts announced a further investment in open data through an expansion of the Technology Strategy Board’s Innovation Voucher programme. This new development will make Innovation Vouchers available to SMEs who want to use open data to help commercialise their ideas and develop new products and prototypes.

The vouchers will form part of the 2 round of the Technology Strategy Board’s Innovation Voucher programme and will compliment the 3 open data investments announced on 12 December.

Overview

In December 2012 ministers Matthew Hancock and Francis Maude announced 3 new complementary investments in open data designed to increase the flow of open data and to support companies working to create innovative new products from the re-use of public data.

See the press notice New funding to accelerate benefits of open data. There will be:

  • a new Data Strategy Board Breakthrough Fund
  • a new Open Data Immersion Programme
  • upgrades to the format in which Ordnance Survey data can be downloaded to make it easier, more accurate and more flexible for companies to use

All of the measures announced are expected to be in place by early April 2013.

The announcement demonstrates a real commitment from government to open up public data to stimulate economic growth, unlock new commercial opportunities for business and develop more transparent and efficient public services. Further details are available in the frequently asked questions below.

The Data Strategy Board (DSB) held its inaugural meeting on 11 July 2012. The Board is chaired by Stephan Shakespeare. The purpose of this Board is to raise the UK economic competitiveness, increase accountability and improve public services through greater access to public data for people and businesses.

The remit of the board will look to maximise value of data from the Public Data Group of trading funds and beyond. The membership of the board includes data users from outside the public sector, with representatives of commercial re-users and the open data community.

Purpose of the Data Strategy Board

The DSB’s remit is to create maximum value, for businesses and people across the UK, from data held by the Public Data Group Trading Funds (Ordnance Survey, Met Office, Land Registry, Companies House) and beyond:

On behalf of the public sector the DSB will:

  • steer the management of contracts for weather and geographic information
  • consider how data from the DSB might generate economic growth
  • work to ensure that ministers have appropriate advice to enable:
  • delivery of open data from the Public Data Group
  • provision of data and services to the public sector UK general public, including services which contribute to the protection of life, property and UK national infrastructure
  • making access to data easier for all users
  • increasing public access to free data over time, subject to affordability and value for money

FAQs on the three new complementary investments

DSB Breakthrough Fund

Q. What is the DSB Breakthrough Fund?

A. The fund will assist open data proposals where government departments, government agencies and local authorities have identified constraints on data release. It will help the government’s “presumption to publish” agenda.

Q. Who is eligible?

A. Government departments, government agencies and local authorities are eligible to apply. In the case of local authorities, collaborative bids will be encouraged (especially from district councils) based on geographic, economic, community of interest, shared services or other local or regional partnership groupings.

Q. What will the fund cover?

A. The fund will cover a number of sub-themes of activity which could include funding for:

  • IT/ tool development
  • standardising data
  • making data linkable

Q. What are the criteria for applications?

A. In order to qualify for funding organisations must:

  • demonstrate that they have not been able to identify appropriate funding through existing budgets
  • commit to making the data available for free under the Open Government License, to high quality, with appropriate frequent updates on an ongoing basis
  • show that there is a demand for the data to be released/ standardised/ linked and that there is a longer term economic, social or environmental benefit to making it more accessible
  • commit to ways to track the use of their data and the benefit which has been realised from its release
  • make the data available through the government’s Data.gov.uk website

Open Data Immersion Programme

including competition prizes

Q. What is the Open Data Immersion Programme?

A. The programme will comprise of a series of events and competitions, run by the Open Data Institute and NESTA (National Endowment for Science Technology & the Arts). It will involve a range of data owners – such as Trading Funds, Departments, agencies and local authorities - and data users.

The events will be aligned to both specific sector challenges and industry themes.

Q. Who is eligible for the programme?

A. The programme will be targeted at small to medium enterprises (SMEs) and start-ups. Key participants will be developers who can code the products and services, data owners/ domain experts who understand and can help source the appropriate information and product/ industry business leaders who have an explicit understanding of the real world industry challenges.

Q. What will participants get from the events?

A. Participants who are selected to partake in an Innovation Camp event will get the opportunity to work closely with data owners/ domain experts to help develop their products, and also the chance to partner with other SMEs/ start-ups.

The winning projects at each event will be eligible to take their concepts into early product development with pre-seed funding from the Technology Strategy Board (TSB) Innovation Vouchers or competition prizes run by NESTA.

ODI anticipates engaging the private sector to in some way match the commitment of the Data Strategy Board funding. This could be in the form of NESTA prizes, support and or resources to support the winning projects.

Q. Where can I find more information about the Open Data Immersion Programme?

A. More information on the programme will be published shortly on the Open Data Institute website at www.theodi.org

Ordnance Survey (OS) Open Data updates

Q. What are OS Open Data updates?

A. The OS Open Data updates include new product releases; release of OS VectorMap District Version 1, and OS Terrain 50. New styling is being released for Meridian2 and Strategi. These updates will increase the quality, accuracy and currency of a number of products, whilst the styling will make them easier to use and interpret.

Q. Who is eligible?

A. The new products will be available to everybody through the OS OpenData website from April 2013.

Q. Where can I find more information about the new products?

A. Further information is available on the Ordnance Survey website

FAQ Innovation Vouchers

Q. How does this relate to the existing Innovation Vouchers programme?

A. The Technology Strategy Board (TSB) will deliver the new vouchers as part of the existing Innovation Voucher programme. The vouchers will also adopt the format and structure of the vouchers available to other sectors.

Q. How much is each voucher worth?

A. The vouchers will have a value of £5,000 each.

Q. How can I apply for a voucher?

A. Application for the vouchers will be online through the existing TSB Vouchers portal at https://vouchers.innovateuk.org

Q. What are Innovation Vouchers?

A. The purpose of Innovation Vouchers is to help stimulate and promote innovation in targeted sectors where we want to encourage greater levels of innovation. Vouchers can be used to incentivise SMEs to engage with the knowledge base and other forms of innovation advice to help them to grow by developing new ideas and potential new commercial products. Start-ups and small and medium sized businesses and sole traders from across the UK can apply for an Innovation Voucher.

Q. Who is eligible?

A. Innovation Vouchers for Open Data Business Innovators and Inventors are aimed at SMEs and sole traders. The vouchers will help them access specialist and technical advice from knowledge suppliers to help develop new or improve existing data products that make use of open data.

Q. Where can I find more information?

A. Further details can be found on the TSB website

Overview of the Board

The DSB is a Ministerial Advisory Board that advises David Willetts, Minister for Universities and Science, and Francis Maude, Minister of Cabinet Office. It is led by an independent chair, and includes data users from outside the public sector, including representatives of commercial re-users and the Open Data community.

The Board also incorporates existing public sector customer groups, who will continue to provide sector expertise around the commissioning of weather services and geographic information for the public sector. The Public Weather Service Customer Group, the Geographic Information Group and the Open Data User Group will become sub-committees of the DSB.

There are 2 to 3 public sector seats for the chair to appoint.
