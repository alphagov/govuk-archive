Expenses and benefits: subscriptions and professional fees, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer covering the cost of your employees’ subscriptions and professional fees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or pay tax and National Insurance if all of the following apply:

  • you pay the organisation yourself directly
  • the organisation is on ‘List 3’, HMRC’s list of approved professional bodies
  • you have a dispensation covering the fees or subscription

Part 3: What to report and pay

If any of the subscriptions or fees you cover aren’t exempt, you may have to report the costs to HMRC, and you may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance on them.

If you pay a ‘List 3’ organisation directly

If you cover subscriptions or fees for an organisation included in ‘List 3’ by paying them directly, you can apply for a dispensation. This means you won’t have to include them on your end-of-year reports.

The following table shows what you have to do if you don’t have a dispensation.

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of up to £8,500 a year Fill in form P9D, section A(2) Nothing
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Fill in form P11D, section M Nothing
Directors Fill in form P11D, section M Nothing

If you pay another organisation directly

If the organisation isn’t included in ‘List 3’ add the cost of the fee or subscription to your employee’s earnings when deducting Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) through your payroll.

If your employee pays the fee or subscription and you pay them back

If the organisation is included in ‘List 3’:

  • add the cost of the fee or subscription to your employee’s earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax (but not Class 1 National Insurance) through your payroll

If the organisation isn’t included in ‘List 3’:

  • add the cost of the fee or subscription to your employee’s earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance using through your payroll
