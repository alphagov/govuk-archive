UK export control legislation for strategic goods: military and dual-use

A summary of the current UK export control legislation for military or dual-use goods.

Introduction

If you are planning to export or trade in military or dual-use goods then you should be aware of export restrictions or ‘export controls’ administered by the Export Control Organisation (ECO). These restrictions cover a broad range of goods and products. They also equally affect organisations and individuals.

This guide summarises the current UK export control legislation which is operated by the ECO, part of the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS). It outlines amending orders to current legislation and links to further information.

Controls on export of strategic goods and licensable trade activities

In the UK, the Export Control Organisation (ECO) is responsible for administering controls on:

  • the export of strategic goods - including military equipment, security and paramilitary goods
  • the transfer of technology and provision of technical assistance
  • embargoed destinations - arms embargoes and sanctions
  • transit and transhipment
  • trade controls - trafficking and brokering

These come from both EU and national legislation.

The Export Control Order 2008 (SI 2008/3231)

This is the UK government’s main legislation on export control (applicable under the Export Control Act 2002), outlining the duties and responsibilities of companies when exporting controlled goods.

Council Regulation (EC) No 428/2009 (‘the re-cast Dual-Use Regulation’)

This Regulation is the main legal basis for controls on dual-use goods. It is directly applicable in all EU countries, including the UK.

Download Council Regulation (EC) No.428/2009 from the Europa website (PDF, 174K).

Council Regulation (EC) No.1236/2005 (‘the 2005 Regulation’)

This Regulation concerns trade in certain equipment and products which could be used for capital punishment, torture or other cruel, inhuman or degrading treatment, or punishment.

The Regulation was adopted on 30 July 2005 and came into force 12 months later on 30 July 2006.

The Export of Radioactive Sources (Control) Order 2006 (SI 2008/1846)

This UK legislation controls the exports of certain high-activity radioactive sources as defined under the International Atomic Energy Agency Code of Conduct on the Safety and Security of Radioactive Sources.

The Control Lists

These are the main reference documents which list goods that require an export control licence. The Control Lists form an integral part of the legislation.

Amending orders

Export control legislation can be amended from time to time to reflect the addition or removal of controls or editorial corrections and drafting changes. See changes to both the Export Control Order 2008 and the EU Dual-Use Regulation (Council Regulation (EC) No 428/2009).

The original legislation has been amended by the following amending orders.

Amendments to Council Regulation (EC) No 428/2009 (‘the re-cast Dual-Use Regulation’)

This Regulation is the main legal basis for controls on dual-use goods. It is directly applicable in all European Union (EU) countries, including the UK.

Download Council Regulation (EC) No.428/2009 from the Europa website (PDF, 173K).

The re-issued legislation entirely replaced the previous EU Dual-Use Regulation (Council Regulation (EC) No 1334/2000), as well as all its associated amendments.

This regulation has subsequently been amended:

  • by Council Regulation (EU) No 1232/2011 published on 8 December 2011 which issues new EU General Export Authorisations
  • by Council Regulation (EU) No 388/2012 published on 16 May 2012 and which came into force 30 days later on 15 June 2012. This amendment includes the revised version of the EU Dual-Use List

For more details (including links to the regulations) see the guides on controls on dual-use goods (EU Dual-Use Regulation) and European Union General Export Authorisations  (EU GEAs). 

Controls on destination countries and on End-Use

Export restrictions and End-Use controls may apply only to specific destination countries.

Sanctions and embargoes

Sanctions vary widely in scope. They may prohibit, for example, all trade placing restrictions upon exports, imports, financial transactions, communications and transportation, with the exception of certain humanitarian goods. But sanctions can be more limited and may restrict only the export of certain goods, such as military goods and armaments.

Embargoed destinations are listed in:

  • UK legislation imposing European Union (EU) Regulations, as outlined in Schedule 4, Part 1 and Part 2 of the Export Control Order 2008 - see the guide on the Export Control Order 2008 
  • UN legislation and relevant orders which are directly applicable in the UK

You can also find more information at the following sources:

  • The Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) is responsible for overall UK policy on international sanctions. You can find an A-Z index of export controls and sanctions by country on the FCO website. This listing provides wider, more comprehensive information relating to countries where there are other restrictions in place (such as visa and financial sanctions)
  • HM Treasury’s Asset Freezing Unit is responsible for the implementation and administration of international financial sanctions in the UK. Read guidance on financial sanctions on the HM Treasury website
  • The European Commission website has an up-to-date list of EU Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP) sanctions in force. This includes details of financial and other restrictions, as well as arms embargoes and sanctions. You can find a list of CFSP sanctions in force on the Europa website

End-Use controls

If your goods are not listed on the Control Lists, you may still need a licence under so-called ‘End-Use controls’. This aspect of export controls covers licensing of items that might potentially be used in a Weapons of Mass Destruction programme or in terms of military goods.

These controls are outlined in Article 4 of the EC Regulation and in the Export Control Order 2008.
