Part 6: Discretionary payments and grants

You might be entitled to a grant from a dissolved company’s assets or a deceased person’s estate.

Grants from dissolved companies

If you were a member of a dissolved company, you can apply to the Treasury Solicitor for a discretionary grant. Use the dissolved company notification form and confirm the:

  • full name of the company
  • company registration number
  • name and address of your bank
  • your bank account number and sort code

The Treasury Solicitor has published guidelines to help you:

  • discretionary grants - where the company can be restored
  • discretionary grants - where the company can’t be restored

Grants from deceased estates

You can apply to the Treasury Solicitor for a grant from a deceased person’s estate if you could have expected to benefit from it. This could be where:

  • you provided the person with free services like washing, cleaning, cooking, shopping, home repairs or care where they might otherwise have had to pay
  • you lived together with the person (as their partner or as a friend) but were not married
  • you represent a charity or other body that cared for the person at considerable expense

You don’t have to be related to the person to make a claim.

How to apply

The Treasury Solicitor has published guidelines on grants from estates to help you apply.

There isn’t a form to fill out - contact the Treasury Solicitor, including as much information as possible, at:
