Licence Export control licence

Apply for this licence

Get started on the Export Control Organisation's online export licensing system

Overview

You will need either an export or trade control licence issued by the Export Control Organisation (ECO) if you are exporting specific ‘controlled’ goods. This means items that are listed on the UK Strategic Export Control Lists or on Sanctions Lists or that fall under ‘end-use controls’ require a licence. 

The controls include the licensing of dual-use and military goods, as well as components, software and ‘intangible’ technology.

Depending on the nature of your export (such as export destination and type of goods being exported) you might need to register or apply for one of the following licence types:

  • Open General Export Licence (OGEL) including the Community General Export Authorisation (CGEA)
  • Standard Individual Export Licence (SIEL)
  • Open Individual Export Licence (OIEL)
  • Global Project Licence (GPL)
  • Open General Transhipment Licence (OGTL)
  • Standard Individual Transhipment Licence (SITL)
  • Open General Trade Control Licence (OGTCL)
  • Standard Individual Trade Control Licence (SITCL)
  • Open Individual Trade Control Licence (OITCL)

In applying or registering to use a particular licence, you should:

  • know the ‘rating’ (Control List classification entry) of your products on the UK Strategic Export Control Lists
  • understand your responsibilities, such as keeping accurate records
  • keep updated via the ECO’s Notices to Exporters email service and regular training programme
  • be prepared for ECO Compliance Audits if required

Open General Licences are designed to cover the export of controlled goods that still require a licence but are of a less restricted nature or being exported to less sensitive destinations. Before using an OGEL you need to:

  • register to use the licence
  • read and understand the licence and its specified conditions carefully
  • ensure that you can meet all the terms and conditions specified, eg record keeping and end-user requirements
  • be prepared for ECO Compliance Audits

If you can’t use an OGEL and the goods are still controlled you will need to apply for a SIEL.

How to apply

All applications should be made via the ECO’s SPIRE export licensing system.

Fines and penalties

Breaching export controls is a criminal offence. Penalties can vary depending on the nature of the offence. They range from:

  • revocation of a licence
  • seizure of goods 
  • issuing of compound penalty fine 
  • imprisonment for up to ten years
