ERDF regional guidance: North West

Introduction

The ERDF is a funding stream which is allocated to regions by the European Union to stimulate regional economies.

The North West of England is one of 9 regions to qualify for Competitiveness and Employment funding.

Under the 2007 to 2013 programme, the region can receive €755.5 million of ERDF investment.

Calls for projects

The North West ERDF programme currently has live calls for projects under Priority 1, 1.3 and 4 of its investment framework.

Priorities

The North West operational programme document (see ‘Programme documents’ below) outlines the investment priorities for the region. It focuses on 4 main priorities:

Priority 1

Stimulating enterprise and supporting growth in target and markets, such as developing high-value new enterprise, developing higher added value activity in target regional sectors, increasing sustainable consumption and production.

Priority 2

Exploiting innovation and knowledge, such as exploiting the science and research and development base of the region, encouraging innovation to improve productivity in all companies and developing better management and leadership.

Priority 3

Creating the conditions for sustainable growth, such as exploiting the economic potential of major gateways in Merseyside, developing high quality sites and premises of regional importance and supporting the improvement of the region’s visitor offer and image.

Priority 4

Growing and accessing employment, such as stimulating enterprise in disadvantaged communities and under-represented groups, supporting linkages to key employment areas and supporting employment creation for areas of regeneration need.

Local ERDF guidance

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the ERDF programmes across England. This includes the introduction of a new suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects approved to receive funding after 1 April 2012 will need to use and follow.

For projects required to use the new documentation see the guide on ERDF: national guidance.

All forms and guidance in existence before 1 April 2012 that ERDF-supported projects have used or are currently using are available from your local programme delivery team.

Please ensure that you are using the correct forms and guidance for your project. If you have any questions, please contact the North West ERDF Programme Delivery Team on 0303 444 6520 or email nw.erdfenquiries@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Local management

In the North West, DCLG and its local delivery team is responsible for managing the ERDF programme, which is known as the Northwest Operational Programme (NWOP).

The local team allocates NWOP funding through a single appraisal process to different ‘action areas’ within its 4 priority areas.

Local management committee

A local management committee has been formed to monitor, oversee and advise on the delivery of NWOP. The role and function of the local management committee is laid down by European Union legislation.

The local management committee is responsible for:

  • setting the direction for NWOP
  • driving NWOP forward
  • ensuring NWOP meets agreed objectives and targets
  • overseeing the strategic review of NWOP

The local management committee comprises senior representatives from across the region from:

  • DCLG
  • 4NW
  • sub-regional partnerships
  • local authorities
  • higher education
  • trade unions
  • the private and voluntary sectors

Local management sub-committees

The local management committee is assisted by 2 sub-committees:

  • the performance and monitoring sub-committee
  • the merseyside phasing-in sub-committee

The performance and monitoring sub-committee assists the local management committee in its duties in reviewing the programme in more detail.

The merseyside phasing-in sub-committee provides the local management committee with further resources and informed comments to focus on Merseyside during its transitional ‘phasing in’ status.
