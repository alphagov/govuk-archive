Abingdon HIVE

Serving Abingdon, Wantage, Grove and Didcot.

Abingdon is one of Britain’s oldest market towns situated on the heart of the river Thames. Although Abingdon is small with limited facilities, it is central to many other larger towns which offer a fantastic range of shopping and entertainment facilities. To the North Oxford, South East, London and Reading, West, Swindon.

There are 5 service family accommodation (SFA) areas for Abingdon with 75% of these being in the village of Shippon where Dalton Barracks is based; just a short walking distance away from Station and a mile outside Abingdon town centre. Further SFA can be found at Wantage and Grove, Southmoor and Didcot.

There are 2 Spar shops; one within the barracks and one in the SFA area. There are 3 main supermarkets in Abingdon - Tesco, Somerfield and Waitrose - and a range of high street stores. Oxford with its larger selection of stores and amenities can be reached within 20 minutes.

Bicester HIVE

Bicester is situated just north of the City of Oxford, and is home to HQ Bicester Garrison, 23 Pioneer Regiment, DSDC, DCTA, and DSDA. It is surrounded mainly by small villages its local amenities are limited, a wider range of shopping and entertainment facilities can easily accessed in Oxford, Banbury, Aylesbury, Milton Keynes. London is also within an hour travelling either by car, train or coach.

Unit facilities

15 Army Education Centre is located at Bicester; which has an Army Learning Centre and Library. There are many courses on offer. This facility is available for all Serving Personnel and their dependents to use, as well as Civil Servants working in the Garrison area. The Garrison has a Gym which has a large indoor gym which is use for Football, Badminton, Circuit training, and aerobics; the Gym also has various Aerobic machines and free weights. There is also an indoor heated swimming pool, this is open for lane swimming during the day and family swim times after school. Future plans are to open a swim school and aqua-aerobics classes. The Community centre hosts Mums and Tots, Youth Club, Music Train and Tumble Tots.

Contact house

There is one fully furnished contact house available for hire at Bicester; this is to provide short term accommodation, on a priority need basis. Bookings can be made via the Unit Welfare Office (telephone: 1869 256457). Booking can also be made through Mrs Ellen Hawkins MBE - Garrison Welfare Facilities Manager on telephone: 01869 256411

Service Families Accommodation

There is just one main SFA area for Bicester for both Soldiers and Officers. This is in the village of Ambrosden. This is located approx 2.5 miles from 23 Pioneer Regt and HQ Bicester Garrison. There are 298 married quarters.

The allocations office for Bicester is based at High Wycombe and can be contacted on telephone: 01494 495511.

The local DE (HD) office can be reached on telephone: 01869 256329.

Single Living Accommodation

At St George’s Barracks, HQ Bicester Garrison, there are 5 blocks of accommodation behind the wire for Junior Ranks; 3 for personnel who are on Bowman courses and 1 Block for transit accommodation; contact: WO2 Brotherton - Sgt Maj of the Bowman Training Centre on telephone: 01869 257880.

There is 1 block for accommodation for those who are posted to HQ Bicester Garrison; contact the QM’s Dept on telephone: 01869 257738.

For SNCO’s posted to HQ Bicester Garrison the Sgt’ Mess can be contacted on telephone: 01869 257711.

For Officers posted to HQ Bicester Garrison the Officer’s Mess can be contacted on telephone: 01869 256520.

At 23 Pioneer Regt, St David’s Barracks, there are 8 blocks in total behind the wire; 7 of these have been slammed providing 139 en-suite rooms, in 1 block, 1 wing is female accommodation. Junior Ranks posted in, please contact Sgt Dunning Accommodation SNCO on telephone: 01869 259358. The Sgt’s Mess is behind the wire and can be contacted on telephone: 01869 257081. The Officer’s Mess is also on camp and can be contacted on telephone: 01869 257089.

Schools

There are 2 pre-schools and one local Foundation/Primary school for Families living in the Ambrosden Married Quarter area. There are also 2 Secondary Schools in Bicester.

Transport

Bicester has 2 local train stations. Bicester Town provides trains into Oxford. Bicester North is on a main line to London and Banbury. Bus and Train timetables are available from the HIVE. Oxford also has a very good Park and Ride scheme due to being very close to the M40. Many airports - London Heathrow, Luton; Stansted, Gatwick and Birmingham International are also easily reached, most within an hour.

Shopping

The quartering area of Ambrosden has a local Spar shop, as well as a Post Office and small shop. The main supermarkets in Bicester are Tesco and Somerfield. Bicester town centre has a few “big name” stores: Argos, Woolworths; Dorothy Perkins; Homebase. Larger stores are available within Aylesbury, Banbury, Oxford and Milton Keynes.

Eating out

Bicester has a good selection of places to eat out, ranging from Pub Food to restaurants offering a wide selection of menus.

Healthcare

There is a Medical and Dental Centre located at St Georges Barracks for all serving personnel. For families living in the Ambrosden Quarter area, Ambrosden Surgery is the local Doctors and is attached to a larger practice in Bicester. There are various Dental Practices in Bicester however few are taking on new patients. Oxford and Banbury have hospitals have hospitals both with A&E departments.

Bramcote HIVE

Serving Bramcote, Middlemarch Rise, Camp Hill and Nuneaton.

Bramcote HIVE is currently unmanned. Please direct enquiries to Birmingham HIVE on 01215 518775 or email birminghamhive@armymail.mod.uk

Bramcote is situated between Nuneaton and Rugby. Nuneaton Town Centre being a 5 minute drive. Coventry City centre is a 10 minute drive, and the centre of Birmingham can be reached within 30 minutes. Tamworth, Warwick and Leicester are all within easy reach. Bramcote is central to a good motorway link; M6, M69, M1, M42 and M5 are all close by. The novelist George Eliot was born in Nuneaton and for the amateur historian there are many places to visit including Arbury Hall at Nuneaton and Warwick Castle, Warwick.

There is a host of facilities within station including a well equipped gymnasium, swimming pool Londis shop, hairdressing and beauty salon, library, thrift shop, pre-school and nursery, Jungle Gym, Coffee Shop, and youth club. The Army Learning Centre and Training Wing provide learning facilities for serving personnel and dependants. There is a weekly run parent and toddler group; and the HIVE coordinates a monthly solicitors and CAB surgery. There are the usual mess facilities including the Swan Club for junior ranks, Himali Club for the Gurkha Community and the 501 Club which caters for all ranks.

Grantham HIVE

Serving Prince William of Gloucester Barracks.

Royal Logistic Corps

Prince William of Gloucester Barracks is located on a site occupying the high ground to the east of and overlooking Grantham. On what was originally a flying training school establishment for the Royal Flying Corps, it subsequently became known as RAF Spitalgate. The airfield, when taken over from the RAF, was renamed Prince of William of Gloucester Barracks by HRH Princess Alice, Duchess of Gloucester at a ceremony on the 21st May 1977. It became Headquarters The Royal Logistics Corps Territorial Army on the 5th April 1993.

Grantham is on the border with Leicestershire, a town with its roots in the old stagecoach route from London. There are still several coaching inns, including the famous Beehive Inn at Castlegate, where can be found the world’s only “living” pub sign - a real beehive perched in a tree and full of live South African bees, and the Angel Inn is where King Richard III signed the death warrant of the Duke of Buckingham in 1483. The nearby village of Woolsthorpe is where Isaac Newton supposedly saw the famous apple drop that inspired him to discover the law of gravity. He was schooled in Grantham, as was Lady Margaret Thatcher, Baroness of Kesteven, the first woman British Prime Minister. Scenes from the 1981 film Raiders of the Lost Ark were filmed in Margaret Thatcher’s old school in Grantham. Grantham Tourist Information Centre has many leaflets, maps and guidebooks for walking, cycling, leisure activities, local events and places to visit, as well as lots of souvenirs and gift ideas.

Hermitage HIVE

Serving Faircross and Curridge Place.

The Joint Aeronautical and Geospatial Organisation (JAGO) is based at Denison Barracks, Hermitage. The Unit houses 42 Engineer Regiment (Geographic) and the Royal School of Military Survey.

Hermitage and Curridge are small rural villages 1 mile from the junction of the M4 and M34. The nearest town is Newbury; 4 miles to the South. Excellent road links give easy access to Reading, London, Bristol, Oxford and the South coast. There are lovely country walks, horse trials and cycling routes locally.

The unit boasts some very good sporting facilities including a gymnasium, squash courts, tennis courts and astro turf pitch. There is a library within the unit which provides internet access; internet access is also available in the HIVE. Many clubs have sessions in and around the unit including wargamers, arts and crafts and brownies.

Innsworth HIVE

Serving Imjin Barracks, JSSu Cheltenham, Ashchurch and St Barbara’s Barracks.

Innsworth is in a semi-rural location four miles from historic Gloucester and the spa town of Cheltenham. Both offer a good range of high street stores and individual shops. Gloucester has a Designer Outlet centre, called the Quays, which is located next to the recently restored dock area and close to Gloucester Cathedral. Within Gloucester, Cheltenham and the surrounding area you have a wide range of places to eat from all the High Street names to Michelin starred restaurants. Cheltenham is famous for it’s race course and Kingsholm is home to Gloucester Rugby club; there is an excellent choice of sports and leisure activities for all abilities to watch or join in. There are good entertainment facilities with a local theatre, cinema, festivals offering a range of music and comedy. Birmingham, Bristol and Cardiff are only an hour away by train or road. There is easy access to the Cotswold villages, The Forest of Dean and the Malvern hills.

The major occupant of Imjin Barracks at Innsworth is the Allied Rapid Reaction Corps (ARRC) with the Support Battalion working along side them. There are several lodger units within the Station and we look after the staff from JSSU Cheltenham.

North Luffenham HIVE

Serving St Georges Barracks and Cottesmore.

St George’s Barracks is situated between Edith Weston and North Luffenham in Rutland, which is the smallest county in England. It is situated on the south bank of Rutland Water almost central between the towns of Stamford to the east and Oakham to the west. Edith Weston is a small village, with one public house, a small amenities shop, a Church of England Church and a primary school. Service personnel and their families form a large percentage of the local population. North Luffenham village is approximately 5 minutes drive away from the camp; it is also a small village with 2 public houses, a shop, a Church of England Church and a primary school.

At St George’s Barracks there is a gymnasium and swimming pool, which is available for use not only by the service personnel but their dependants too. There is also a new Community Centre on camp with a large newly refurbished child’s soft play area and internet suite, which also has an e fax-bluey and webcam facility. The Community Development Worker and HIVE Information Officer are located in this building.
