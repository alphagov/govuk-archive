If you’ve changed your gender abroad

If you legally changed your gender in the European Union (EU) or the European Economic Area (EEA), this is recognised in the UK.

If the country you changed it in is an ‘approved country’ outside the EU or EEA (check the list below), it’s partially recognised in the UK but you’ll need to re-apply using a simplified process.

If the country isn’t approved, you’ll need to follow the full application process using the standard form above.

List of approved countries (PDF, 147KB) Overseas application form (PDF, 1.1MB) Help with the overseas application form (PDF, 1.1MB)

How the Gender Recognition Panel decides

  1. The panel may ask you for more information or evidence, eg medical reports.

  2. If you’re successful, the panel will contact the General Register Office to make an entry in the Gender Recognition Register.

  3. You’ll get a new short birth certificate from the General Register Office (listing name, sex, date of birth, district and sub-district of birth). The office will explain how to apply to them for a full birth certificate.

  4. If you’re unsuccessful, the panel will write to you with their reasons.

  5. You have the right to appeal - the panel will explain how.
