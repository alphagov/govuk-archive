Part 3: Help from energy companies

Some energy companies might pay for all or part of the cost of installing things like:

  • loft, solid wall or cavity wall insulation
  • boiler repairs or replacements (if you qualify)

You don’t have to be a customer of the energy company but you must own your home or rent privately and have permission from the owner for the work. Your home should be suitable for the work, eg not all homes are suitable for all types of improvements.

How to apply

Contact the Energy Saving Advice Service to see what help you can get.

Energy Saving Advice Service

0300 123 1234
Monday to Friday, 9am to 8pm
Saturday, 10am to 2pm

You can also contact any of these companies:

  • British Gas
  • E.ON
  • EDF Energy
  • RWE npower
  • Scottish Power
  • Scottish And Southern Energy

Eligibility for extra help

You might get help with boiler repairs or replacements and other energy-saving products if you get one of these benefits:

  • Pension Credit
  • Child Tax Credit - and your income is £15,860 or less
  • Working Tax Credit (with a household income of less than £15,860) - and one of the conditions below
  • Income Support - and one of the conditions below
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance - and one of the conditions below
  • income-based Employment and Support Allowance - and one of the conditions below

For certain benefits, one of the following must also apply:

  • you’re responsible for a child under 16 (or under 20 if they’re in education or training)
  • you get the disability or severe disability element of Child Tax Credit
  • you get Disabled Child Premium
  • you get the severe or enhanced element of Disability Premium
  • you get the higher or enhanced premium of Pensioner Premium

The scheme for this kind of help will be called the Energy Company Obligation scheme from January 2013.
