Investing in aquaculture: accessing grants

How to access European Fisheries Fund grants for aquaculture projects, eligibility rules and how to apply.

Introduction

If you want to invest in an aquaculture project (the farming of aquatic organisms such as fish, crustaceans, molluscs and aquatic plants), you may be eligible for a grant from the European Fisheries Fund (EFF).

Micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and projects which deliver very high environmental contribution are eligible for a grant of up to 40% of the total project costs. Other applicants and projects may receive up to 20%.

Who is eligible for an aquaculture grant

Grants from the EFF for investments in aquaculture are available to enterprises in the private sector. Grants can only be awarded to micro or SMEs according to these definitions:

  • medium-sized enterprises have up to 250 employees and a turnover of less than or equal to €50 million (or a balance sheet of less than or equal to €43 million)
  • small enterprises have up to 50 employees and a turnover of less than or equal to €10 million (or a balance sheet total of less than or equal to €10 million)
  • micro enterprises have up to 10 employees and a turnover of less than or equal to €2 million (or a balance sheet of less than or equal to €2 million)

If you are unsure whether or not you are a micro, small or medium-sized enterprise, you can download guidance on business size definitions from the Europa website (PDF, 1.16MB).

When you can apply for a grant

You may apply for a grant for investment in aquaculture at any time. Depending on the amount of your grant and how complicated it is, your application form and business case might have to go to a selection panel. These selection panel reviews (PDF, 84K) are normally held 3 times a year.

The MMO reserves the right to close the programme earlier if programme funds have been used up. You should also note that the eligibility criteria may be reviewed from time to time during the lifetime of the programme.

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Types of project that are eligible for a grant

Grants from the EFF are available for support in investments for the construction, extension, equipping and modernisation of aquaculture production installations.

Key outcomes: * improve working conditions, hygiene, health and product quality * reduce negative impact * enhance positive effects on the environment

Grants can be paid for commercial and near-commercial investments. These include:

  • investments for the construction, extension, modernisation or equipping of installations for rearing fish, crustaceans, or molluscs
  • projects designed to demonstrate the technical and economic viability of unexploited species or innovative farming techniques

Details of applications will be treated as commercially confidential throughout the assessment stage. Once your project has been approved the MMO will make public some details, in particular the name of the successful applicant, the amount of grant approved and the nature of the project. In accepting an offer you will be considered to have given your consent to this. In any publicity for the project you should acknowledge that you have obtained EU and MMO grant on it.

How to change a project after accepting a grant offer

You can apply for modifications to your project once you have signed the offer letter. However, you should secure approval in writing before going ahead with any change to your project. If you do not do this any resulting costs could become ineligible for grant aid.

You should note that if you make any changes to an approved project without the MMO’s prior approval, they may revise or cancel the grant award.

Download the full conditions of EFF grants (PDF, 84K).

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Writing your business case

When applying for a grant, you should provide a detailed business case and attach it to your application for an aquaculture grant. You can get help with your business case from your local area facilitator

Your business case should show that your project:

  • has quantified outputs and clear and attainable targets
  • will provide an additional and sustainable benefit to the fishing industry
  • has a justified need for grant
  • will provide good value for money

It should include:

  • a full description of the project, including how it will operate and be carried out
  • a prospective market analysis of aquaculture species showing the economic viability of the project
  • any relevant consents
  • the benefits of your project
  • financial benefits which directly result from the project
  • any other benefits which the project will provide
  • a financial appraisal of your project comparing benefits with costs
  • at least one single quote for items worth up to £1,500, and more on a sliding scale for items worth more than this
  • an environmental impact assessment
  • the expected results of your project and likely timescales
  • how you will publicise the project
  • how you will monitor the project
  • how you will manage your risks to delivery
  • information about your business - your experience of running similar projects and the results which were achieved, including company accounts for the last three years
  • a description of how your project will affect the local area

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.
