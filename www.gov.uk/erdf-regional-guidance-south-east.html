ERDF regional guidance: South East

The South East been allocated €47.4 million of European Regional Development Funding to invest in projects that will support regional businesses and create jobs.

Only applies to England

ERDF Funding Round 5

European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) Funding Round 5 has now launched. For more information please see the detailed guide on Applying for ERDF funding.

The South East is one of 9 regions to qualify for Competitiveness and Employment funding.

Under the 2007 to 2013 programme, the region can receive €23.7 million of ERDF investment.

Priorities

The South East Operational Programme Document outlines the investment priorities for the region. It focuses on one main priority:

Priority 1: Innovation, Knowledge Transfer and Sustainable Productivity

This will be achieved by focusing on 2 themes:

Theme 1

Promoting the knowledge economy to raise levels and application of ‘knowledge’ in the non-high-tech sectors and promoting information communication technology take up for commercial development among disadvantaged areas in the region.

Theme 2

Promoting sustainable production and consumption by increasing the prosperity of communities in a way which decreases the carbon footprint of the community and thus of the region as a whole and reducing the region’s carbon footprint through the greater use of energy efficient applications.

Programme documents

Operational Programme

The aim of this Operational Programme is to: ‘Promote competitiveness in South East England whilst contributing to reducing the region’s ecological footprint’.

To achieve this aim the Programme has established 3 objectives:

  • to raise levels of knowledge and innovation across all business sectors in order to support more resource efficient business practices, boosting profitability and long-term competitiveness
  • to stimulate innovation and job creation in new and emerging ecologically-driven market sectors
  • to reduce the rate of growth of the region’s ecological footprint, whilst stimulating economic growth.

These objectives will be delivered through 2 Priority Axes:

  • Promoting Sustainable Production and Consumption
  • Technical assistance

Programme targets

Outputs

  • Number of businesses assisted to improve their performance: 750
  • Number of additional firms involved in business networks: 800
  • Number of businesses within the region engaged in new collaboration with UK knowledge base: 270
  • Number of businesses in the region developing R&D links with other businesses: 70
  • Number of businesses engaged in developing sustainable mobility strategies: 240

Result

  • Number of businesses improving performance: 1,250
  • Gross new jobs created: 180
  • Number of businesses making financial savings from improved energy and resource efficienct: 550
  • Number of businesses increasing percentage of turnover attributable to new and improved products by 5 per cent: 110
  • Number of businesses tendering for public sector contracts: 75

Impact

  • Net additional employment: 110
  • Net increase in Gross Value Added: €32,000,000
  • Reduction in region’s CO2 emissions: 85,000 tonnes

Local ERDF guidance

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the European Regional Development Fund Programmes across England. This includes the introduction of a new suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects approved to receive funding after 1st April 2012 will need to use and follow.

For projects required to use the new documentation, visit the national guidance section.

All forms and guidance in existence prior to the 1st April 2012 that ERDF supported projects have used or are currently using, are available from your local Programme Delivery Team. To access this and for more information contact the Programme Delivery Team: South East by email SE.ERDFenquiries@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Please ensure that you are using the correct forms and guidance for your project. If you have any questions regarding this seek clarity from your local Programme Delivery Team through the details shown above.

Local Management Committee and Project Selection Committee

Partnership plays a key role in the delivery and management of the ERDF Programme. To reflect this, two groups have been established, each with a specific role.

The ERDF Programme for the South East is overseen by a Local Management Committee (formerly titled Programme Monitoring Committee). This is composed of senior representatives of the bodies of the regional partnership, with the membership reflecting the sectoral and sub-regional interests in the Programme.

To assist the Committee in effectively carrying out its full range of strategic and regulatory functions, it is supported by the Project Selection Committee.

Local Management Committee

The Committee is responsible for ensuring the Programme’s overall strategic direction, driving it forward so that it meets agreed targets and redirecting the work of the Programme as and when necessary over its lifetime. Its key focus is on setting Programme direction and strategic review. Details of the membership of the Local Management Committee is available below.

Project Selection Committee

The aim of the Project Selection Committee is to support the Local Management Committee in carrying out its responsibilities. Specifically, the principal role and function of the group is to appraise and commission ERDF projects to ensure that the different priority areas of the Programme may be fulfilled and activity delivered. Details of the membership of the Project Selection Committee is available below.
