Part 2: Types of change

If your employer changes any terms in your written statement with your agreement, they must confirm the change in writing within a month. You must be told exactly what has changed in the statement.

If your employer changes terms and conditions that aren’t in the written statement (eg like the right to sick leave), they should tell you where to find information about the change (eg a company handbook, noticeboard or intranet site).

Collective agreements and changes to written statements

A collective agreement may exist between your employer and trade unions or a staff association. If your employer makes agreed changes to the terms of the collective agreement, they should inform you in writing.

Negotiations under a collective agreement can lead to changes in the terms of your written statement (eg pay or working hours). If you are covered by a collective agreement, any changes will apply to you whether you are a member of a union or staff association or not.

Flexibility clauses

Flexibility clauses are terms in your contract that give your employer the right to change some of your terms and conditions. A flexibility clause could be that your job location can be changed.

Your employer isn’t allowed to make completely unreasonable changes using a flexibility clause.

Example telling you to move to another country to work at 1 day’s notice would be completely unreasonable.

Changes of employer

If your employer changes, usually you must be given a new written statement of particulars within 2 months.

However, if the name of the business changes or there’s a new employer but no other changes in terms and conditions, your employer doesn’t need to issue a new written statement. However, they should inform you in writing of the changes within a month.

Disciplinary measures

Sometimes changes to your terms and conditions like a demotion can happen as a result of a disciplinary measure.

Your employer should make sure the staff handbook or intranet site contains information about how this could happen in the section dealing with disciplinary procedures.
