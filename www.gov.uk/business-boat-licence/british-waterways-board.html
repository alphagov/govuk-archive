Licence Business boat licence (England, Scotland and Wales)

From British Waterways Board
  1. Overview
  2. How to apply
  3. How to renew
  4. How to change

Overview

You must have a licence to run a leisure boat business on Canal & River Trust (CRT) controlled waters.

You’ll need a licence to use a boat for:

  • passenger trips
  • day hire
  • holiday hire
  • floating restaurants

You must renew your licence every 12 months.

Conditions

Before applying for a licence, you must make sure your:

  • boat meets the CRT’s construction and equipment standards
  • boat has home moorings
  • insurance covers third-party liability up to at least £1 million

Download ‘The CRT’s standards for boat construction and equipment’ form (PDF 25KB)

If your boat can hold 12 passengers or less, you must complete a declaration of compliance to send with your application.

Download ‘The CRT’s declaration of compliance’ form (PDF 132KB)

If your boat can hold 13 passengers or more, you must get a passenger certificate from the Marine and Coastguard Agency (MCA) to send with your application.

Contact your local MCA office for detailed advice on how to get the right passenger certificate for your boat.

How to apply

Complete a business boat licence application and send this to the address below:

Download ‘The CRT’s business craft licence’ form (PDF 383KB)

Boat Licensing Team
PO Box 162
Leeds
LS9 1AX

You can also send this to them by email or call them on the number below:

Boat Licensing Team
customer.services@canalrivertrust.org.uk
Telephone: 0303 040 4040

You’ll have to pay an application fee, which changes based on how many passengers your boat can hold.

Displaying your licence

You must display your licence on both sides of your boat (the CRT will provide you with 2 copies of this).

Penalties and fines

If you run a leisure boat business without a licence, your boat may be confiscated and sold or destroyed.
