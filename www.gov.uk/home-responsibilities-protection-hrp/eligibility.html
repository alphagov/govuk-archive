Part 3: Eligibility

Depending on your circumstances, either you qualified automatically for Home Responsibilities Protection (HRP) or you will have to apply.

Qualifying automatically for HRP

Most people got HRP automatically if they were:

  • getting Child Benefit in their name for a child under the age of 16 and they had given the Child Benefit Office their National Insurance number
  • getting Income Support and they didn’t need to register for work because they were caring for someone who was sick or disabled

Applying for HRP

You have to apply for HRP if you :

  • were regularly spending at least 35 hours a week looking after someone getting Attendance Allowance, Disability Living Allowance at the middle or highest rate for personal care, or Constant Attendance Allowance
  • were a registered foster carer throughout a full tax year from April 2003 and were not getting Child Benefit and were not in paid work, or did not earn enough in a tax year for it to count towards the basic State Pension
  • think HRP is missing from your record

Who wasn’t eligible

In the following circumstances you would not usually have got HRP.

Carer’s Allowance

If you get Carer’s Allowance you’ll automatically get National Insurance credits and would not usually have needed HRP.

Married women or widows

If you were a married woman or a widow, you couldn’t get HRP for any complete tax year if:

  • you had chosen to pay reduced rate Class 1 National Insurance contributions as an employee (commonly known as the small stamp)
  • you had chosen not to pay Class 2 National Insurance contributions when self-employed
