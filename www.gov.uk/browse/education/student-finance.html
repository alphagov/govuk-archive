Student finance and loans

  • Student finance calculator

    Use this student finance calculator to estimate what student finance you could get for university - student loans, student grants, bursaries and other funding

  • Register or log in to student finance

    Log in to your student finance account or sign up for a new one - see when your payments are due, track your application, change your details and apply for new finance

  • 16 to 19 Bursary Fund

    If you're 16 to 19 you might be able to get £1,200 from the 16 to 19 Bursary Fund from your school, college or training provider

  • 24+ Advanced Learning Loans

    If you’re aged 24 or older, you'll be able to apply for a loan to help cover tuition fees for courses starting on or after 1 August 2013

  • Contact Student Finance England

    Contact Student Finance England, address and phone details and contact details for Student Finance Services European team

  • Access to Learning Fund

    The Access to Learning Fund helps students in financial hardship with emergency payments - what you'll get, eligibility and applying

  • Adult Dependants' Grant

    Adult Dependants' Grant is up to £2,642 for students in higher education with an adult who depends on them financially - what you'll get, eligibility and applying

  • Care to Learn

    Care to Learn can help pay for childcare costs while you're studying - what you'll get and how to claim

  • Childcare Grant

    Help with childcare costs as part of student finance - what you'll get, eligibility, how to claim

  • Disabled Students' Allowances (DSAs)

    Apply for Disabled Students' Allowances (DSAs) if you have a disability or mental health condition that affects your ability to study - who qualifies and how much you can get

  • Discretionary Learner Support

    If you're aged 19 or over, on a further education course and facing financial hardship, Discretionary Learner Support (DLS) could help with learning costs - who qualifies and how to apply

  • Education Maintenance Allowance (EMA)

    You can still apply for Education Maintenance Allowance (EMA) if you're studying in Northern Ireland, Scotland or Wales. Students in England may be able to get a 16 to 19 Bursary Fund

  • Extra money to pay for university

    Bursaries, scholarships and awards available directly from your university or college - how much you can get, eligibility, applying

  • Funding for postgraduate study

    Different types of postgraduate funding - scholarships, bursaries, grants and loans and where to apply

  • Grants and bursaries for adult learners

    Financial help for adult learners that you don't have to pay back to cover study and training costs

  • Learner Support helpline

    Contact Learner Support for advice about help with childcare costs, accommodation

  • Mature students: university and funding

    Mature students can go to university and get funding - even if they don’t have traditional qualifications

  • NHS bursaries

    If you're training for medicine, dentistry or healthcare you may get an NHS bursary

  • NHS student bursaries

    General contact details for help with study and living costs when training for a professional registration in healthcare

  • Parents' Learning Allowance

    Parents' Learning Allowance provides help with learning costs for full-time students with children, on top of the student finance package

  • Residential Support Scheme

    Help with term-time accommodation when you're studying away from home - find out about what you'll get, eligibility, and how to claim

  • Social work bursaries

    If you're training for social work you may get a bursary to help with tuition fees and living costs.

  • Student finance

    Rates for students loans and extra grants, how to apply and repay

  • Student finance if you started before 1 September 2012

    Full-time students can apply for a Tuition Fee Loan, Maintenance Loan and Maintenance Grant, Part-time students can apply for Tuition Fee Grant and Course Grant

  • Student finance: evidence needed by parents and partners

    If you’re the parent or partner of a student applying for finance you may need to give evidence of your income or circumstances

  • Student finance: evidence needed by students

    You need to send proof of identity the first time you apply for student finance

  • Teacher training funding

    Undergraduates and postgraduates can get help with the costs of their teacher training

  • Travel grants for medical and dental students (England)
