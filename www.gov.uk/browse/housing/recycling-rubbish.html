Recycling, rubbish, streets and roads

  • Find out your rubbish collection day

    Check your council's website to find out when your rubbish will be collected

  • Apply for special collection of large waste items

    Arrange a special collection for large waste items - most councils will collect things like old sofas, fridges or washing machines for a fee

  • Find out when your council cleans your street

    Your local council is responsible for sweeping streets and removing litter - report any problems to your local council

  • Recycling collections

    Find out if your council collects recycling, how to recycle household waste such as mobile phones, computers, packaging and green waste

  • Report a pothole

    Potholes and damaged roads can be dangerous for motorists and cyclists - report potholes to the local council

  • Claim compensation for injuries caused by roads or pavements

    You may be able to claim compensation from the council if you've had an accident caused by a road or pavement

  • Dispose of garden waste

    Arrange for the collection of garden waste by your council

  • Find local transport plans

    Check the transport plan for maintenance, improvements and changes in your local area with the council

  • Find out which roads your council will grit

    Plan your journey in cold weather - find out about gritting of roads and footpaths, the location of grit bins and snow clearance

  • Get a recycling bin

    Find out how to get a recycling bin from your local council

  • Local services disrupted by severe weather

    Information about local services that have been disrupted by severe weather conditions

  • Report a blocked drain

    Your local council is responsible for public drains - report a blockage.

  • Report a dangerous building or structure

    You can report a dangerous building to the local council

  • Report a derelict or abandoned building

    You can report a derelict or abandoned building to the building control section of the local council

  • Report a dog fouling problem

    The local council must keep public areas like parks, playgrounds and pavements clear of dog mes - report a dog fouling problem to the local council

  • Report a litter problem

    Report litter to your local council

  • Report a problem with 'street furniture'

    Report a problem with street furniture, which includes things like bollards, litter bins, benches and cycle racks to the council

  • Report a problem with a pavement

    Report damaged pavements to the local council - broken or missing slabs, removing weeds

  • Report a problem with a street light

    Report a street light problem to the local council - they are responsible for installation and maintenance of street lighting

  • Report a problem with a street name sign

    Report a missing or damaged street or road name sign to your local council

  • Report a problem with a traffic light

    How to report faulty or broken traffic lights depends on where you live and the type of road

  • Report a road spillage

    Report an incident of spillage on the road such as oil, mud, sand or dropped cargo to the local council

  • Report an abandoned vehicle

    You can report an abandoned vehicle like a dumped car to the local council

  • Report an obstruction on the road

    You can report obstructions on the road such as illegal signs, hedges, builders skips and scaffolding to the local council

  • Report debris and litter on motorways and main roads

    Reporting obstructions or debris on trunk roads, A roads, major routes, motorways across the UK - phone numbers

  • Report discarded syringes

    Report discarded syringes, needles and other drug related items in public places to your council

  • Report flooding and burst water mains

    Contact your council, water company or Floodline to report flooding from water mains, public drains, sewers or rivers

  • Report fly-posting

    Report fly-posting to your local council - posters, stickers or bills put up without permission of the property owner

  • Report fly-tipping

    Fly-tipping is the illegal dumping of waste or rubbish - report it to the local council

  • Report graffiti

    Your local council is usually responsible for removing graffiti from things like public buildings, monuments, benches and bins

  • Report road flooding

    If you find a flooded road or street, report it to your local council

  • Report squatters in a council property

    Report squatters in a council property to the local council

  • Reporting road obstructions

    Contact your local council or the Highways Agency to report obstructions or debris on public roads

  • Request a pedestrian crossing

    Request a pedestrian crossing like a puffin crossing, toucan crossing or zebra crossing from the local council

  • Request a speed limit change

    Local speed limits are set by the council

  • Request snow clearance from a road, path or cycleway

    Ask your local council to remove snow and spread grit on roads or pavements in cold weather
