Planning permission and building regulations

  • Apply for a dropped kerb

    A dropped kerb allows vehicles to cross the pavement from the road to a driveway - contact your local council to find out how to apply for a dropped kerb

  • Apply for building regulations approval from your council

    Check if you need building regulations approval and apply before starting a project

  • Apply for equipment for your home if you're disabled

    Apply to your local council for equipment or help adapting your home if you’re disabled

  • Apply to do work on your home if you're in a conservation area

    Check with your local council before doing building work on your home if you live in a conservation area

  • Apply to work on a tree that's protected

    You may need to make a 'works to trees' application if they’re protected by a ‘tree preservation order’ (TPO) or you live in a conservation area.

  • Building regulations approval

    Get approval before you start any building work - your builder will usually do this for you but as the owner of a building you may be held responsible

  • Feed-in Tariffs: get money for generating your own electricity

    A Feed-in Tariff is a payment you can get if you generate your own electricity (eg with solar panels or a wind turbine) and you can also sell energy back to the grid. Find out how to apply

    Councils carry out consultations to help plan, manage and deliver services. Have your say about local issues by responding to consultations run by your council
