Quick answer Manage your approved driving instructor (ADI) registration

You’re responsible for your approved driving instructor (ADI) certificate and for keeping your registration up to date.

Your ADI certificate

You must display your certificate in the passenger-side edge of the front windscreen of your car when you give driving lessons.

You can be prosecuted for using an invalid certificate or one that’s not yours.

Changing your name or contact details

You must update your ADI registration within 7 days if you change your name or address.

Renewing your registration

You must renew your ADI registration every 4 years if you want to continue being an ADI.

You’re responsible for remembering to renew your ADI registration.

Lost or stolen ADI certificates

You must tell the police straight away if your ADI certificate is lost or stolen. They will give you a crime reference number - you should give this to the Driving Standards Agency (DSA).

DSA ADI helpline

adireg@dsa.gsi.gov.uk
0300 200 1122
Monday to Friday 8am to 6pm

The DSA will send you a new certificate if you give proof that the original has been lost, stolen or destroyed.

You’ll have to either:

  • give the DSA permission to use the photo from your photocard driving licence
  • send a recent passport-style photograph to the DSA

You’ll have to pay £3 if you can’t give the DSA a crime reference number.

You must send your old certificate to the DSA if you find it after getting a new one.

Getting a caution or conviction

You must tell the DSA in writing within 7 days if you get any caution or conviction. This includes:

  • being ‘bound over’
  • having your name entered in the sex offenders’ register
  • being banned or barred from working with children

Stopping giving driving lessons

You must tell the DSA if you stop giving driving lessons because you want to leave the ADI register.

You should send your ADI certificate with a covering letter to the DSA.

You won’t get a refund if you have any time left on your registration, unless you’re leaving the register for health reasons. You’ll need to give medical proof to be considered for a refund.

There’s a different process to manage your ADI registration in Northern Ireland.
