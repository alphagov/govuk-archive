Selling your business: your responsibilities, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Self-employed sole trader

When you sell your business, you have legal responsibilities to staff you employ. You must also finalise your business’ tax affairs.

Staff

If you have anyone working for you, you must tell them:

  • when and why you’re selling the business
  • about redundancy terms or relocation packages, if necessary

Make sure you don’t breach employees’ rights when a business changes ownership.

VAT registration

If you’re registered for VAT, you may be able to transfer the VAT registration number to the new owner.

Tax return

You must send a Self Assessment tax return by the deadline. You’ll need to put the date you stopped trading on the return.

Tax relief

You may have made a Capital Gain when selling your business (eg the money you get from the sale, or assets from the business that you keep)

If this means you need to pay Capital Gains Tax, you may be able to reduce the amount by claiming Entrepreneurs’ Relief.

There are other reliefs to reduce the amount of Capital Gains Tax that you may be able to claim - HMRC has more information.

National Insurance contributions

If you’re no longer going to be self-employed, call HMRC’s National Insurance helpline to cancel your Class 2 National Insurance contributions.

Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

Part 2: Business partnership

Your responsibilities when selling a partnership depend on whether you’re selling:

  • your share of the partnership
  • the entire partnership

Check your business’ partnership agreement - it may have restrictions and conditions on the sale of the partnership.

Staff

If you have anyone working for you, you must tell them about the sale, including:

  • when and why you’re selling the partnership
  • details about the redundancy terms or relocation packages you will offer, if required

Make sure you don’t breach employees’ rights when a business changes ownership.

VAT registration

If the partnership is registered for VAT, you may be able to transfer the VAT registration number to the new owner.

Tax returns

If you’re selling your share in the partnership

You must fill out a personal Self Assessment tax return by the deadline.

If you’re selling the whole partnership

You must:

  • make sure the ‘nominated partner’ sends a Partnership Tax Return by the deadline
  • send your personal Self Assessment tax return by the deadline

Tax relief

You may have made a ‘capital gain’ when selling the partnership (eg the money you get from the sale, or assets from the partnership that you keep).

If this means you need to pay Capital Gains Tax, you may be able to reduce the amount by claiming Entrepreneurs’ Relief.

There are other reliefs to reduce the amount of Capital Gains Tax that you may be able to claim - HMRC has more information.

National Insurance

If you’re stopping self-employment when you sell the partnership, call HMRC to cancel your Class 2 National Insurance contributions.

HMRC National Insurance self-employed helpline

Telephone: 0845 915 4655
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

Part 3: Limited company

Appoint new directors or company secretaries

When you sell your limited company, you must must appoint new directors before you resign as a director yourself.

Use the Companies House WebFiling service to do this.

Staff

If your company employs staff, you must tell them about the changes, including:

  • when and why the company is being sold
  • details about the redundancy terms or relocation packages, if necessary

Make sure you don’t breach employees’ rights when a business changes ownership.

VAT registration

If the company is registered for VAT, you may be able to transfer the VAT registration number to the new owner.

Charges against your company

If you’ve secured finance for the company against your personal property (eg a mortgage on your house to secure a business loan), you must let the provider know within 21 days of the sale.

Profits you make from the sale of the company

You may need to pay Capital Gains Tax on profit you make from the sale of the company or on the value of assets you take from the company.

Claiming Entrepreneurs’ Relief may reduce the amount you have to pay.

There are other reliefs to reduce the amount of Capital Gains Tax - HM Revenue & Customs has more information.

You can work out how much Capital Gains Tax you’ll have to pay.

You must report what you’ve made from the sale on your Self Assessment tax return at the end of the tax year.
