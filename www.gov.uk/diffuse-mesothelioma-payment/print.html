Benefits & credits: Diffuse Mesothelioma Payment

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You may be able to get a one-off lump sum payment if you’re suffering from the asbestos related disease, diffuse mesothelioma.

Your exposure to asbestos must have happened in the United Kingdom.

Part 2: What you'll get

You’ll be paid a one-off lump sum payment.

The amount you’ll get depends on how old you were when your disease was diagnosed. For example, if you were 60 years old when your disease was diagnosed, and you qualify, you will get a one off payment of £38,316.

If the date of the diagnosis is not known, it will be based on your age at the date of making a claim. For example, if you are 67 years old on the date you make your claim, and you qualify, then you will get a one off payment of £17,416.

Part 3: Eligibility

You can claim a one-off lump sum payment if you:

  • can’t make a claim under the 1979 Pneumoconiosis Act
  • haven’t been given a payment for the disease from an employer, a civil claim or elsewhere
  • aren’t entitled to compensation from a Ministry of Defence scheme

Your exposure to asbestos must have happened in the United Kingdom. For example:

  • you came into contact with asbestos from a relative - eg by washing their clothes
  • you were exposed to asbestos in the environment - eg you lived near a factory using asbestos
  • your exposure to asbestos was while self-employed
  • your exposure cannot be specified but it occurred in the United Kingdom

Part 4: How to claim

You will need to complete a claim form and provide medical evidence, as no medical examination will be offered.

Time limits for claiming

You need to claim within 12 months of diagnosis.

If you’re a dependant claiming for a sufferer who is now deceased, then a claim needs to be made within 12 months from the date of death.
