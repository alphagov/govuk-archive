Gaining consent to dredge marine minerals

Regulations on dredging minerals, how to apply for permission to dredge, and the fees and relevant licenses involved.

Introduction

Dredged marine minerals are an alternative to using aggregates found on land. They are used for building new homes, offices and factories and maintaining and constructing roads. They can also be used for town and city regeneration or beach replenishment.

An advantage of using marine sources is that boats can deliver aggregates directly to harbours in urban areas, as a result reducing pollution. However, dredging for marine minerals affects the aquatic environment, so each application to dredge for marine minerals must be considered carefully.

Regulations on marine dredging

The Marine Management Organisation (MMO) enforces the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) and Natural Habitats Regulations in England and Northern Ireland.

These regulations provide a legal basis for controlling the extraction of marine minerals by dredging in British waters. They also cover the EIA Directives and apply to:

  • assessing the effects of public and private projects on the environment
  • the conservation of natural habitats and of wild fauna and flora

Ensuring sustainable development

The government aims to ensure a steady supply of minerals for infrastructure, buildings and goods that are needed by society, industry and the economy. Decisions on using minerals are made according to the principles of sustainable development.

You can read the Minerals Policy Statement: Planning and Minerals on the Communities and Local Government website and read guidance on extracting minerals by dredging from the English seabed on the Communities and Local Government website.

Dredging guidance for applicants and operators

If you are considering applying for permission to extract marine aggregate, the MMO has produced a guidance booklet. Find information about the application process for dredging permission on the MMO website.

Fees for marine mineral dredging applications

The MMO has powers to recover expenses from applicants for marine dredging.

Find guidance on the fees payable for applying for marine mineral dredging permission on the MMO website.

Applicants for dredging permission must pay the correct fees in three stages:

  • before application
  • during dredging application
  • during the monitoring phase

Other fees are also payable for additional services including:

  • providing pre-application advice - £47,000
  • processing applications for dredging permission - £29,500
  • considering monitoring reports prepared for pre- and post-dredging and operational dredging periods - £6,500 per year
  • interpreting and assessing electronic monitoring system data produced during the operational dredging period for each year in which any dredging takes place - £11,500 per year
  • processing applications for variations which constitute relevant projects or habitats projects - £21,500
  • processing applications for all other variations - £6,000

Licences awarded to dredge for marine minerals

Under the marine mineral dredging regulations, the MMO must publish screening determinations and decisions on licence applications.

The areas for which these are available online include:

  • Off Great Yarmouth
  • South east of the Isle of Wight
  • North Nab
  • Selsey Bill
  • St Catherine’s
  • East of Southwold
  • Cutline
  • Liverpool Bay
  • Culver Sand
  • West Greenwich
  • the Eastern English Channel
  • South east of the Humber Estuary
  • Inner Dowsing
  • Humber

Find screening and application decisions for dredging licence applicants on the MMO website.

Any documents not available online can be viewed in person by appointment at the MMO headquarters in Newcastle. You can arrange an appointment in writing at:

Marine Management Organisation

Newcastle upon Tyne

You can also call the MMO helpline on 030 0123 1032, or email them at marineminerals@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Screening determinations are carried out regarding:

  • new dredging applications
  • variations to existing dredging permissions
  • variations to existing dredging licences
  • amendments to conditions of dredging permissions
  • proposed prospecting and bulk sampling
