How to apply for Uplands ELS and Uplands Organic ELS (replacing the Hill Farm Allowance) for farms in Severely Disadvantaged Areas.

Introduction

The Uplands Entry Level Stewardship (Uplands ELS) is available to farmers with land in a Severely Disadvantaged Area (SDA). The agri-environment strand was launched in 2010 and replaced the Hill Farm Allowance (HFA) subsidy, for which final payments were made in 2010.

This guide explains how Uplands ELS and Uplands Organic Entry Level Stewardship (Uplands OELS) work for farmers and land managers. It also looks at how to access the Uplands Transitional Payment (UTP) if you are still farming land under an Environmentally Sensitive Area (ESA) or Countryside Stewardship Scheme (CSS) agreement, as this land is ineligible for Uplands ELS/OELS.

Finally, the guide tells you how to use and apply for ELS online and where you can find further support and advice.

Background information

The Uplands ELS is a strand of the Environmental Stewardship scheme delivered by Natural England on behalf of the Department for Environment, Food, and Rural Affairs (Defra). It is open to applications from all farmers and land managers with land within the SDA who meet scheme eligibility requirements. It is a voluntary scheme, designed to deliver environmental benefits in the widest possible areas by rewarding effective land management practices. Uplands ELS builds on the successes of ELS.

Uplands ELS

Uplands ELS provides a higher payment in return for a commitment to simple, yet effective, environmental management. It replaced the HFA subsidy which ended in 2010. Unlike HFA - which excluded dairy farmers and had other restrictions - Uplands ELS is open to all farmers and land managers in England’s SDA, providing they meet the requirements of the scheme.

Like ELS, Uplands ELS requires farmers and land managers to manage land according to specified environmental standards, in return for a set payment per hectare. Participants can choose from a wide range of more than 80 ELS/Uplands ELS management options. These cover all farming types - for example, hedgerow management, stone wall maintenance, low input grassland, buffer strips, and various arable options. For more information, see the guide on Entry Level Stewardship.

Uplands OELS

If you are farming organically or are converting to an organic system in an SDA in England, you can apply to enter Uplands OELS.

Eligibility, rates and agreements

In order to be eligible for Uplands ELS, you will need to:

  • meet a points target for your holding, depending on the size of your farm and land type
  • meet the compulsory environmental management requirements for SDA land
  • agree to deliver the additional environmental management options you have chosen

Find guidance on Uplands ELS for farmers and land managers on the Natural England website.

Under the Uplands ELS scheme, you can create and carry out your own practical environmental management programme for your farm. The range of options allows you to:

  • look after wildlife species and their habitats , eg maintaining and improving biodiversity
  • protect natural resources such as soil and water
  • improve your land management and maintain traditional landscapes
  • protect historic features of the uplands
  • conserve traditional livestock breeds
  • adapt your practices to reduce your effect on the environment, eg by providing and protecting carbon storage in blanket bogs and improving your overall water retention

Due to the difficulties of managing upland holdings, Uplands ELS payment rates are higher than for ELS. The standard Uplands ELS rate is:

  • £62 per hectare per year for SDA land below the Moorland Line and for SDA parcels smaller than 15 hectares above the Moorland Line
  • £23 per hectare per year for SDA land parcels of 15 hectares or more above the Moorland Line

Agreements are for five years.

Common land and shared grazing are eligible for Uplands ELS, with one agreement applying to an entire common or groups of entire commons. Common land agreements where there are two or more active graziers need to be supported by a commoners’ or graziers’ association. They attract an extra £5 per hectare per year because of the extra costs of operating a shared agreement.

Uplands ELS works alongside the other strands of Environmental Stewardship - ELS, OELS and Higher Level Stewardship (HLS). See the guide on Higher Level Stewardship.

If you still farm under a CSS or ESA Scheme agreement you will not be eligible for Uplands ELS until the expiry of the existing agreement, but you may be eligible for the Uplands Transitional Payment.

You can find the ELS Environmental Stewardship handbook on the Natural England website.

Uplands Transitional Payment

If your land is under an ESA scheme or CSS agreement it will not be eligible for Uplands ELS or Uplands OELS until these agreements expire.

From 2011, if you are still farming land under a CSS or ESA agreement, you may be eligible for the Uplands Transitional Payment (UTP), provided you successfully claimed the HFA in 2010 and meet the eligibility requirements of the scheme. However, if you did not submit a valid application for HFA in 2010, you will not be eligible for the UTP at any time in the future.

Eligible farmers can apply for UTP as part of their Single Payment Scheme (SPS) application form - SP5.

If you have a part-farm ESA and/or CSS agreement and are not applying for UTP, you will be able to apply for Uplands ELS or Uplands OELS on the land not already under agreement.

The UTP has a similar set of rules and payment rates to the HFA. The Rural Payments Agency (RPA) on behalf of Defra is administering the UTP which will be paid from 2011. Find an explanatory booklet on UTP on the RPA website.

It’s best to read the UTP booklet together with the SPS handbook 2010. Download the RPA’s SPS handbook for England 2010 from the RPA website.

Find frequently asked questions (FAQs) on how to claim UTP 2011 on the RPA website.

Find guidance on Uplands ELS on the Natural England website.

Uplands Organic Entry Level Stewardship

To be able to apply for Uplands Organic Entry Level Stewardship (Uplands OELS) funding, your farm needs to fit particular criteria. You should:

  • already be farming organically on land registered with an organic control body (CB)
  • have at least one parcel of eligible land within the SDA in England
  • adopt appropriate management practices on your land

You can find a list of approved CBs on the Defra website.

Uplands OELS, like Uplands ELS, is available in place of the HFA which ended in 2010.

If you’re converting to an organic system, help may also be available as a top-up to Uplands OELS payments. For free support and advice on organic conversion, you can contact the Natural England Organic Conversion Information Service helpline on 0800 980 0048.

The payments for Uplands OELS are up to £92 per hectare - higher than for Uplands ELS.

Before you can enter land into Uplands OELS, and start to receive payments, you must register or already be registered on the RPA Rural Land Register.

How to apply for Uplands ELS and Uplands OELS

If you think you may be eligible to apply for Uplands ELS or Uplands OELS, you might find it helpful for a Natural England adviser to visit your farm beforehand - and/or to attend an Uplands ELS event run by Natural England in your area. Visits and events are offered free of charge. To arrange a visit or book into an event, you can call the Natural England Visits and Events helpline on 0300 060 1695.

To find an approved Natural England agent to help you to make your application, you can search the regional register of agents on the Natural England website.

You should contact your regional Natural England office to apply for Uplands ELS. You can find contact details for Environmental Stewardship regional offices on the Natural England website.

If you manage a combination of organic and conventionally managed land, you can apply for Uplands OELS on your organic land and ELS on your conventional land, as part of one combined agreement.

To be accepted onto the Uplands OELS scheme, you will need to:

  • meet a points target for your holding, depending on the size of your farm and type of land
  • meet the compulsory environmental management requirements for SDA land
  • agree to deliver the additional environmental management options you have chosen
  • identify, map and retain your Farm Environment Record (FER) features, including specific Uplands FER features - areas of bracken, scrub and boulders and rocky outcrops

Find the OELS handbook on the Natural England website.

Natural England may be able to visit your farm to advise you about applying for Uplands OELS. You can call the Organic Entry Level Stewardship helpline on 0300 060 1113 to ask for an Uplands OELS application pack and information on applying.

Uplands OELS operates only in England. Separate schemes are available if you farm in Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland.

Entering Uplands ELS/Uplands OELS

There are three main ways to enter into Uplands ELS/Uplands OELS:

  • if you are already in ELS/OELS, you can replace it with Uplands ELS/Uplands OELS
  • most HLS agreements may be changed to incorporate Uplands ELS/ Uplands OELS
  • if you have no existing Environmental Stewardship, CSS or ESA agreement, you can apply for Uplands ELS/Uplands OELS

If you have a part-farm CSS and/or ESA agreement and are not in receipt of UTP you will be able to apply for Uplands ELS on the land not already under agreement.

If you are a new applicant, you must make sure your land is registered on the Rural Land Register (RLR) with the RPA. You will also need an RPA vendor number. For more information, see the guide Environmental Stewardship: the basics.

You can download Uplands ELS application guidance from the Natural England website (PDF, 130KB).

Issues for graziers, tenants and landlords

If you wish to apply for Uplands ELS and you have a tenancy agreement of less than five years, your landlord will need to countersign your application. Find guidance on how the ELS affects tenancies and grazing arrangements on the Natural England website.

Applying with common land or shared grazing

If you want to apply for Uplands ELS with common land or shared grazing, you will need to apply as a commoners’ or graziers’ association, registered as a single customer with the RPA under a Single Business Identifier (SBI). You can find and download RPA customer registration forms and guidance on the RPA website.

If you participate in an Uplands ELS agreement for common land or shared grazing, you must register against the SBI. You also need to make sure that the common or shared grazing is mapped onto the RLR and you can then apply to Natural England for an application pack. Download Uplands ELS step by step guidance for commons from the Natural England website (PDF,250KB).

You will need to complete a supplementary Uplands ELS application form for common land and shared grazing (NE-CLA3), which will be sent to you in your application pack. You can also download the common land and shared grazing supplementary application form from the Natural England website (PDF, 61KB).

ELS Online

You can apply for Uplands ELS online if you or your agent use the ELS Online secure software package. ELS Online enables you to prepare and submit your Uplands ELS application electronically. You will need a computer with the required specifications and broadband access. The software is not available for Mac computers.

Find Uplands ELS Online information on the Defra Whole Farm Approach website.

You can download Uplands ELS and ELS Online software guidance from the Defra website (PDF, 1.03MB). You will need to use the most up-to-date version of the software.

What to do before using ELS Online

Before you use the ELS/Uplands ELS software, you should read the ELS handbook - or the OELS handbook if you are farming organically. You can find the ELS handbook on the Natural England website. You can find the OELS handbook on the Natural England website.

You will also need to make sure that all the land you wish to enter into Uplands ELS is registered with the RPA on the RLR. See the guide on registering your land.

How to use ELS Online

You will need to contact Natural England’s ELS Online helpline on 0300 060 3934, or by email at els.online@naturalengland.org.uk, to let them know you want to apply online and to ask for your PIN number.

For information on how to use ELS Online, see the ELS Online step-by-step guide on the Defra Whole Farm Approach website.

The benefits of applying via ELS Online include:

  • easier calculation of your points total
  • faster completion of the application form
  • faster processing of your application
  • customer support from a dedicated online team
  • user friendly and time-saving software
  • that an agent can make multiple online applications
  • that you can alter your applications before submission

Organisations that can help

Further information on Uplands ELS is available from the following organisations:

Natural England works to ensure sustainable use and management of the natural environment across England. It delivers the Environmental Stewardship scheme on behalf of Defra. It hosts events around the country, including cross compliance farm walks and farming drop-in clinics. You can read information on Environmental Stewardship on the Natural England website., or contact the Natural England Enquiry Service on 0845 600 3078

One of Defra’s major roles is to help the farming industry to operate as efficiently as possible. Defra administers European support policies that provide around £3 billion to UK agriculture annually. They also oversee several agencies that work with farmers and land managers (eg Natural England, the Environment Agency and Animal Health), the import and export of agricultural/horticultural products and the implementation of pest and disease controls. You can call the Defra helpline on 08459 33 55 77 for more information.

The RPA is responsible for licences and schemes for growers as well as for running the SPS. For more information about SPS and how it can help your farming business, you can call the RPA helpline on 0845 603 7777. You can also see the guide on the SPS.

In England, the Farm Advisory System advises farmers about cross compliance. For further information, call the Cross Compliance helpline on 0845 345 1302. Alternatively, find information on cross compliance requirements on the Cross Compliance website.

The National Farmers’ Union (NFU) represents the farmers and growers of England and Wales. It aims to promote successful and socially responsible agriculture and horticulture, while ensuring the long-term viability of rural communities. You can read about the work of the NFU on their website.

The Country Land and Business Association (CLA) works on behalf of its members to influence policy on rural land payments and schemes, as well as organising networking events and providing free specialist advice. Find out about joining the CLA on their website or call the CLA Publications Department helpline on 020 7235 0511.

The Tenant Farmers’ Association (TFA) lobbies on behalf of its members at all levels of government and provides advice, information and services for tenant farmers. Find out about joining the TFA on their website, or call the TFA helpline on 0118 930 6130.

The National Sheep Association (NSA) represents sheep farmers across the UK. They organise events and conferences and send out a bimonthly newsletter, Sheep Farmer, to their members. Find out about becoming a member of the NSA on their website.

The National Beef Association (NBA) is a membership body for anyone with an interest in the beef industry. They represent the interests of their members and promote awareness and best practice in cattle farming. Find out about NBA membership benefits on the NBA website.

Farmers are likely to come into contact with local authorities over a number of farming, land use, food standards and environmental regulations. Your local authority may also be able to provide further information or resources.

For further information on land conservation, see our section on land and conservation.
