Quick answer Usher syndrome and driving

Not what you're looking for? ↓

You must tell DVLA if you have Usher syndrome.

You can be fined up to £1,000 if you don’t tell DVLA about a medical condition that affects your driving. If you’re involved in an accident, you may be prosecuted.

Car or motorcycle licence

You must tell DVLA. Fill in form V1 and send it to DVLA. The address is on the form.

Download ‘V1 - Confidential medical information’ (PDF, 253KB)

Bus, coach or lorry licence

You must tell DVLA. Fill in forms V1V and AUD1 and send them to DVLA. The address is on each form.

Download ‘V1V - Confidential medical information’ (PDF, 261KB)

Download ‘AUD1 - Confidential medical information’ (PDF, 249KB)
