ERDF regional guidance: South West

The South West been allocated €124.7 million of ERDF to invest in projects that will support regional businesses and create jobs.

Priorities

The South West is the only English region to qualify for both Convergence funding (in Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly) and Competitveness and Employment funding across the rest of the region.

The European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) South West Competitiveness region covers the unitary authority areas of Devon, Plymouth, Torbay, Somerset, Bournemouth, Dorset, Poole, Bristol, Bath and North East Somerset, North Somerset, South Gloucestershire, Gloucestershire, Wiltshire and Swindon. The area qualifies for ERDF investment for the 2007 to 2013 Programming period of €124.7 million.

Calls for Expressions of Interest under the South West Competitiveness Programme are promoted in the detailed guide on applying for funding. There is currently an Open Call for proposals under Priority Axis 3.

The South West Competitiveness Operational Programme Document outlines the investment priorities for the Competitiveness and Employment programme in the region. It focuses on 3 main priorities:

Priority 1 - Innovation and Knowledge

The aim of this priority is to improve the regions overall performance with regard to innovation and close the intra-regional gap in innovation performance. The priority has a key role to play in maximising the economic contribution from the region’s Higher and Further Education institutions and helping companies exploit their own ideas and innovations.

Priority 2 - Enterprise and Growth

The aim of this priority is to increase the contribution that new businesses make to the economy and reduce the intra-regional disparities in enterprise rates. This priority has a key role to play in addressing the low earnings, low skilled nature of the economy.

Priority 3 - Urban Enterprise

The aim of this priority is to find new approaches to addressing the problems in the region’s most deprived neighbourhoods through enterprise creation. Resources will be focused on activities to benefit the deprived neighbourhoods of Bristol, Plymouth and Torbay.

The South West Competitiveness Operation Programme Document setting out the strategy of the Programme and levels of funding for each priority can be found with other key documents below.

Strategic Investment Frameworks

In the Competitiveness Programme area, 3 Strategic Investment Frameworks have been developed covering Bristol, Torbay and Plymouth. These frameworks have been developed via a partnership approach, and aim to help the cities unlock the economic potential in deprived areas. Individual schemes will be brought forward to deliver Strategic Investment Framework aims.

Annual Implementation Reports

Regulations set down by the European Commission (EC) governing the management of the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) programmes require the Programme partners to produce a yearly progress report known as an Annual Implementation Report. Each report covers a calendar year, and includes progress made during that period in areas such as:

  • financial progress
  • communications
  • achievements
  • monitoring and any Programme-related issues arising during the year

The Programme Monitoring Committee (Local Monitoring Committee after 2011) agrees the content of the report and it is then submitted to the EC by 30 June of the following year. Reports are scrutinised to ensure the programme is being effectively managed. Once each report has been accepted by the EC, it will be published below.

The South West Competitiveness Programme aims to increase the prosperity of the region by supporting enterprises and individuals in delivering projects which drive up productivity and competitiveness. The Operational Programme sets out the strategy, aims and targets for the programme.

ERDF partners seek to ensure that all commissioning and investment decisions are aligned to the Operational Programme priorities and contribute to achieving its targets.

Local ERDF Guidance

Project Guidance

The Programme Delivery Team provide guidance and support to project applicants and grant recipients in relation to European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) requirements throughout the course of a project’s development. Contact details for the team may be found in the Applying for Funding section.

All projects are required to fulfil a number of requirements relating to the cross-cutting themes of equality and diversity and the environment. Guidance relating to these requirements is available below.

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the European Regional Development Fund Programmes across England. This includes a suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects contracted on or after 1 April 2012 need to use and follow. To access this documentation, visit the National Guidance section.

All forms and guidance in existence prior to the 1 April 2012 that ERDF supported projects have used or are currently using, are available from your local Programme Delivery Team. To access this and for more information contact the team through the contact details provided on the Applying for Funding section.

Please ensure that you are using the correct forms and guidance for your project. If you have any questions regarding this seek clarity from your local Programme Delivery Team through the details shown above.

Equality and Diversity Guidance

All individual ERDF investments are required to carry out a screening stage equality impact assessment specific to their proposed activity, prior to approval, to show how they will positively promote equality and ensure the widest possible access to these opportunities. Guidance and all relevant documents are available below.

During development of the Operational Programme an equality impact assessment was carried out to assess potential equality impacts across the overall Programme. This document is provided under the South West Competitiveness Operational Programme section.

Local Management Committee

The South West Competitiveness European Regional Development Fund Local Management Committee is the partnership body which provides strategic leadership of the Programme. It sets direction, helps drive the Programme to achieve its targets and monitors performance and compliance.

Following a change in the delivery arrangements for European programmes in England, the Local Management Committee replaced the former Convergence Joint European Regional Development Fund and European Social Fund Programme Monitoring Committee. European Social Fund duties previously undertaken by the Joint Programme Monitoring Committee are now undertaken by a single National Competitiveness Monitoring Committee administered by the Department for Work & Pensions. The inaugural meeting of the Local Management Committee took place on 4 November 2011.

Membership of the Local Management Committee includes representatives from local authorities, the private sector, co-financing organisations, the voluntary sector, trades unions, skills, environment and equalities interest. The group is chaired by the Department for Communities and Local Government and its deputy chair is the Chairman of the West of England Local Enterprise Partnership representing the Local Enterprise Partnerships in the Competitiveness area.

Competitiveness and Employment Joint ERDF and ESF Monitoring Committee

Prior to the introduction of Local Management Committees, South West Competitiveness partners provided Programme management through the South West Competitiveness and Employment Joint ERDF and European Social Fund (ESF) Monitoring Committee. The final meeting took place on 17 June 2011.

The committee was set up in accordance with European regulations, and members were drawn from a range of partners across the public, private, and community and voluntary sectors. Alongside the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) and European Social Fund (ESF) Managing Authorities, the committee had a key strategic role in implementing the South West Competitiveness Programme by monitoring the quality and effectiveness of investments made.
