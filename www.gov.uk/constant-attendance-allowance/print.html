Benefits & credits: Constant Attendance Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you claim Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit or a War Disablement Pension, and you need daily care and attention because of a disability, you can claim Constant Attendance Allowance.

Part 2: What you'll get

You’ll get Constant Attendance Allowance at 4 different rates, depending on the extent of your disability and the amount of care you need:

Rate Weekly amount
Exceptional rate £126.60
Intermediate rate £94.95
Full day rate £63.30
Part day rate £31.65

If you get the ‘exceptional rate’ or ‘intermediate rate’, you may also be able to get Exceptionally Severe Disablement Allowance. You can contact the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency or your local Jobcentre Plus office for advice.

How Constant Attendance Allowance is paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

You must be claiming one of the following:

  • Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit
  • War Disablement Pension

If you’re claiming Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit

To claim you must:

  • need daily care and attention (for example, home nursing or home meals)
  • be 100% disabled (according to a medical examination)

You can contact your local Jobcentre Plus office to find out whether you can get Constant Attendance Allowance.

If you’re getting a War Disablement Pension

To claim you must:

  • need personal help for the same reasons that you get a war pension
  • be getting a war pension of 80% or more

Contact the Service Personnel and Veterans helpline to find out if you can claim Constant Attendance Allowance.

Part 5: Further information

What to do if your circumstances change

You should tell your local Jobcentre Plus office or the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency if your circumstances change. You can find contact details for the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency in the ‘How to claim section’.

A change of circumstances could be:

  • you go into or come out of hospital
  • you enter or leave a nursing home or residential care
  • your spouse goes into hospital (if you get extra money for them)
  • you leave the UK to live abroad permanently

Disputes and appeals

If you’re refused Constant Attendance Allowance and you want to know more about the decision or you think it is wrong, contact Jobcentre Plus office within one month of the date of the decision letter.

You can:

  • ask for an explanation
  • ask for a written statement of the reasons for the decision
  • ask Jobcentre Plus to look again at the decision to see if it can be changed, there may be some facts you think they have overlooked or you may have more information which affects the decision
  • appeal against the decision to an independent tribunal - this must be in writing

For more information download ‘If you think our decision is wrong leaflet GL24’ from the Department for Work and Pensions website.
