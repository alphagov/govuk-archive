Guide Child Benefit if a child or parent dies

  1. Part 1: A child dies
  2. Part 2: One or both parents die

Part 2: One or both parents die

If you become responsible for a child following the death of their parent or parents you need to:

  1. Tell the Child Benefit office straightway - include the exact date of death, where the child is living now and who’s looking after them.

  2. Make a Child Benefit claim for the child (you must do this within 3 months).

  3. See if you can claim Guardian’s Allowance if you’re bringing up someone else’s child or Widowed Parent’s Allowance if your partner has died.

If your partner dies

If your partner was getting Child Benefit and has died, you won’t get the payments automatically. They’ll stop on the Monday after your partner died.

If the payments were going into a bank account that was just in your partner’s name you’ll need to give the Child Benefit Office a new bank account to pay into.

You’ll need to make a Child Benefit claim even if you were already an ‘alternative payee’ (ie getting Child Benefit paid to you instead of to the person who claimed it).

If you’re the person getting Child Benefit and your partner has died, the Child Benefit Office will carry on making payments into your bank account.

If the child and your partner both die

You can normally get Child Benefit for 8 weeks after the child has died if this happens. You must have been living with your partner when they died.

Apply for Child Benefit using form CH330A - you can request this online.

Outstanding Child Benefit

The Child Benefit Office will also pay any outstanding Child Benefit due to the person who died (called ‘arrears’).

To apply for the arrears you must be 16 or over and one of the following:

  • the executor of the deceased person’s estate
  • the person who paid for the funeral
  • the deceased person’s next of kin
  • someone who’ll spend the money on the children

You need to apply for the arrears within 12 months of the date the person died.

You can apply for the Child Benefit payments and the arrears using form CH330A. You can request this online or by calling the Child Benefit Helpline.
