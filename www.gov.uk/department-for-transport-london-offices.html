Getting to GMH by bus

Please note that you cannot pay for your ticket on the buses in central London. You need either to buy a ticket in advance at the machine at the bus stop or have an Oyster card.

If you are using the local mainline and Underground stations and then catching a bus to Great Minster House (GMH):

St James’s Park

  • there is no direct bus service – it’s quicker to walk

Pimlico

  • there is no direct bus service – it’s quicker to walk

Westminster

  • there is no direct bus service – it’s quicker to walk

Victoria

  • 507 (to Waterloo) from the bus station in front of Victoria Station
  • get off opposite GMH, outside the Home Office

Vauxhall

  • 87 (to Aldwych) or 88 (to Camden Town): from the bus station outside Vauxhall station (bus stop B)
  • 87 – Get off at Lambeth Bridge and walk along Horseferry Road to the junction with Marsham Street
  • 88 – Get off at the side of GMH at the junction of Horseferry Road and Marsham Street

Charing Cross

  • there is no door-to-door bus service – it may be quicker to walk
  • 87 (to Wandsworth Plain): bus stop outside Charing Cross Station. Get off at Lambeth Bridge and walk along Horseferry Road to the junction with Marsham Street
  • 88 (to Clapham Junction): bus stop in Whitehall. Get off at junction of Marsham Street and Horseferry Road

Waterloo

  • 507 (to Victoria) from the bus stop at the side of Waterloo Station
  • get off outside GMH, opposite the Home Office

Getting to GMH by bike

If you have your own bike, secure parking is available at Great Minster House (GMH) by prior arrangement with your host. There are also shower and changing facilities at this office.

Barclays Cycle Hire scheme

This scheme means you can now hire a bike for short journeys round central London.

If you use the scheme a lot, it pays to become a member but the bikes are also available for casual hire.

For full information, see the relevant pages on the Transport for London website.

Car parking at GMH

There is no staff or visitor car parking at DfT’s London offices. However, limited parking can be arranged for mobility impaired drivers: please speak to the person hosting your meeting.

Is your journey necessary?

While the department welcome visitors, DfT is working to reduce its carbon footprint for business travel in line with government targets and has developed a series of measures to help achieve this:

  • video conferencing
  • promotion of public transport, walking and cycling

Video conferencing

An integrated video conferencing service is available within the department. If you think this might be appropriate for your meeting to avoid travelling, please contact your host at DfT for further information.
