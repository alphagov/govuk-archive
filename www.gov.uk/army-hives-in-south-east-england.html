This is a list of army HIVES in south-east England, with contact details (including phone numbers) and opening times. HIVES are information centres for forces personnel and their families.

Aldershot HIVE serving Aldershot and St Omer Barracks, Farnborough, Church Crookham and Yateley in Hampshire and Pirbright in Surrey

Aldershot is referred to as the ‘Home of the British Army’ and is located in North Hampshire. It is just a 10 minute drive from both the M3 and the A3 providing easy access to the M25, Central London, the South Coast and the West Country. It is close to traditional towns such as Farnborough and Fleet and the market town of Farnham. Larger towns such as Guildford, Woking, Basingstoke and Reading all have excellent shopping centres, restaurants and leisure facilities and are easily accessed by road and rail. Aldershot train station has direct trains to London, Guildford and Woking and you can get a connecting service to many major destinations in the South and throughout the rail network. Regular bus services operate throughout Aldershot and there is a set down and pick up coach service to London at Aldershot bus station.

There is a wide range of local services and facilities in Aldershot Garrison. These include a leisure centre, a bowling alley, 2 theatres, a golf club and the Wellington Shopping Centre and all are close to service families’ accommodation areas. There are 2 community centres in Aldershot and one in Church Crookham and all offer many activities for the local community including mother and toddler groups and both adult and children’s clubs and activities.

There is an excellent range of sporting facilities available in the Garrison. The Aldershot Garrison Sports Centre has an Olympic sized swimming pool, squash courts and a fully equipped fitness studio. Other facilities in the garrison include the St Omer Barracks Physical and Recreation Training Centre, the Aldershot Army Golf Club, the Hawley Lake Sailing Training Centre and the Aldershot Garrison Angling Club. Canada House houses a Combat Centre which has judo, boxing and martial art facilities as well as the Regional Rehabilitation Unit.

There are many other facilities available in Aldershot Garrison and these include the Aldershot Centre for Health which is the largest facility of its type in the South of England. Schools are plentiful for choice and adult educational facilities are located in Wellington House and the Connaught Community Centre. In addition to community libraries in the local area, there is the Prince Consort Library on Knollys Road which is of National significance. The garrison has 3 churches and additional places of worship for Nepalese and Fijian personnel.

Arborfield HIVE serving Arborfield

Arborfield Garrison is situated on the edge of Arborfield village, 3.5 miles from Wokingham and 7.5 miles from Reading. London (35 miles away) is less than one hour by train and there are plenty of sightseeing opportunities within easy reach. Reading has a lot to offer, excellent shopping, restaurants, pubs and clubs at the Oracle and a great sports stadium, ‘The Madjeski’, home to Reading FC and London Irish RUFC. There is a Showcase cinema at Winnersh and a Vue cinema at the Oracle. The Hexagon Theatre plays host to many famous names.

The garrison gym is situated in the Welfare Centre, Hazebrouck Barracks. In order to use the facilities family members will need to get both a pass and third party liability and personal accident insurance (usually found under home insurance policies). There is a Community Centre, situated outside the gates of Rowcroft Barracks, housing a hairdressers, beauty parlour, various clubs and activities as well as a Nursery, Pre-School, library and an Army Learning Centre. In Hazebrouck Barracks there is a Spar Shop, NAAFI Club, barber’s shop, regimental tailors and a Welfare Centre, comprising a WRVS Coffee Shop and lounge, Cyber Café, HIVE, games rooms and the welfare offices.

Bordon HIVE serving Bordon, Oakhanger and Longmoor

Bordon Garrison is home to the Defence School of Electronic and Mechanical Engineering (Bordon), 10 Training Battalion REME (10 Trg Bn), and the HQ of 104 FS Battalion REME (104 FS Bn). The CPU (Close Protection Unit) are based just along the road in Longmoor.

The town of Bordon lies within North East Hampshire and contains the villages of Grayshott, Headley, Kingsley, Lindford and Whitehill. It lies on the A325 which can be reached from the north near Farnham on the A31 or from the south off the A3. It is set within beautiful countryside close to many larger towns, for example Winchester, Portsmouth, Farnham and Guildford. It is also an easy commute into London by train. There are plenty of family attractions close by.There is a leisure centre within Bordon itself, which has many activities for both adults and children including a pool, well equipped gym and clubs for all ages. The Phoenix Theatre and Arts Centre in Bordon has regular productions and arts activities.

Bordon’s Forest Centre shopping arcade houses a variety of shops, including a supermarket, chemist, greengrocers, a dry cleaner’s and a cobblers and there is a large supermarket in Bordon town. Nearby towns of Alton, Farnham, Farnborough and Aldershot provide good shopping and retail parks. Further afield are Guildford, Portsmouth and Basingstoke, providing larger stores, retail parks and outlet shopping.

The camp has several gyms, and a garrison pool, which is open to all serving personnel and their families. The Bordon Learning Centre is based in Prince Philip Barracks and offers LearnDirect courses. There is a services welfare centre (WRVS) for use by 10 Training Battalion single soldiers, which has facilities such as internet access, Microsoft software, computer games, WiFi access, TV room (with SKY), pool and snooker tables. Within this complex there are also several bars. The camp also provides a church, a barber shop, a convenience shop, cashpoints, telephones and a PRI shop. Martinique House Community Centre is located off camp and houses the Welfare Office, the HIVE, and a coffee shop.

Canterbury HIVE serving Canterbury

Canterbury is a beautiful Cathedral City in south-east Kent, it boasts the oldest Cathedral in England and although steeped in tradition, it is a modern and vibrant City.

The city is just a 15 minute drive from the M2 providing easy access to Central London and Bluewater Shopping Complex. The ports of Dover and Folkestone are also only a 30 minute drive providing access to both France and the rest of the Continent.

Local facilities in Canterbury include a leisure centre, a bowling and skating centre, an Odeon cinema, The Marlowe Theatre, a golf club and Whitefriars Shopping Centre. Three Zoo and wildlife parks also lie within a 15 mile radius of Howe Barracks, which are open all year round. There is also an abundance of community centres in Canterbury including one at the Barracks, all of which offering Sure Start facilities for toddlers, as well as adult and children’s clubs and activities.

In Canterbury’s wider area the towns of Broadstairs, Sandwich and Whitstable all have excellent shopping, leisure facilities and restaurants and are easily accessed by road and rail. Canterbury has two train stations both of which offer frequent services to London and the Kent Coastline. Canterbury also has an excellent bus network and buses run to London from Canterbury bus station and locally from outside Howe Barracks.

Facilities within the barracks include a spar shop, a cash point and a PRI shop. The Twenty/20 club offers a bar and food court, Colours Restaurant is also open 7 days a week providing meals until 18.00. There is also a pre-school within the barracks offering early childcare to children from 2 years old.

Chatham HIVE serving Chatham

An outreach HIVE service is provided to Chatham each Wednesday (subject to change). The Assistant Unit Welfare Officer at Chatham is available Monday to Friday from 9am to 12am (telephone: 01634 841 546).

Chatham Garrison is situated in the centre of the Medway Towns, 20 minutes from London by train, 35 minutes away from France by Eurostar and is in the north west of Kent – The Garden of England. Within close proximity are the beautiful cities of Canterbury, Tonbridge Wells and Maidstone, the County Town.

Medway Towns have historically been renowned for their maritime history, the Dockyard here has 400 years of history. Within Medway there are castles, a cathedral, the Royal Engineers Museum, Fort Amherst and two country parks. There’s a leisure centre, a bowling centre, kart circuit, ski & snowboarding, an Ice Rink, theatres and shopping Centres, kids play zones and lots more.

Within the barracks there is a NAAFI Spar shop, internet café, WRVS, launderette, gym, squash courts and mess bars with facilities. Local amenities are ample, with leisure centres, shopping centres, cinemas and supermarkets all within minutes of the garrison. The garrison boasts a Scout group,(Beavers, Cubs, Scouts), Girl Guide Group (Rainbows, Brownies, Guides), martial arts, horse riding for military personnel and family discounts, yoga, gym and Personal Development Centre. Banks, estate agents, solicitors, opticians, launderettes, dry cleaners, pubs, vets, florists, travel agents and electrical goods suppliers are also all close by.

Deepcut HIVE serving Deepcut and Keogh

Note: Deepcut HIVE is currently unmanned. Please direct any enquiries to Aldershot HIVE. * Email: aldershothive@armymail.mod.uk * Telephone: 01252 347 650

Deepcut Garrison is situated in the Surrey Heath Borough of Surrey, a countryside area that has excellent access to surrounding towns, villages and cities and is served by the M25, M3 and M23 motorways. Coastal towns such as Portsmouth and Southampton are just over an hour away and London is within easy reach both by car or train. Local amenities are ample with family attractions, leisure centres, shopping centres, cinemas, theatres, sporting venues and supermarkets all within easy access.

The station has Officers’, WOs’ & Sgts’ and Cpls’ Messes, a recently refurbished WRVS facility for phase two soldiers, a NAAFI Spar shop, the ever popular Route 66 bar, a well equipped gym, a library, an education centre, a medical centre, a HIVE, a Church, cash points, telephones and a barber.

Marchwood HIVE serving Marchwood, Hythe and Calshot

Marchwood, Hythe and Calshot are located in an area known as the Waterside. The Waterside is situated on the edge of the New Forest, which nestles between Southampton and Bournemouth. There are several beaches, ranging from stony (Calshot), to pure sand at Boscombe, Bournemouth and Christchurch. There are so many things to do and places to visit in this area that it is impossible to list them all here. We have only detailed some facilities/amenities, but there are many more. Call into the HIVE for any further information or pick up one of our many leaflets about this beautiful area.

The Regimental Restaurant (St Georges), Sgts & WO’s Mess and Officers Mess now have the new pay-as-you-dine facility. Families are welcome to use the gymnasium, squash courts and Army Learning Centre in McMullen Barracks. The HIVE holds Information on courses in the learning centre.

Minley HIVE serving Minley and Yateley

The Royal School of Military Engineering Minley (RSME Minley) is located at Gibraltar Barracks on the A327 and is about 3 minutes from Junction 4a of the M3. The nearest train stations are Fleet where there are frequent trains to London and the South West and Blackwater which serves Gatwick and Reading. Local amenities are ample with family attractions, leisure centres, shopping centres, cinemas, theatres, sporting venues and supermarkets all within easy access. Larger towns such as Basingstoke and Reading all have excellent wide ranging choices of shopping centres, restaurants and leisure facilities and are easily accessed by road and rail.

Minley Station provides a wide range of facilities including a cinema and a Spar shop which is located next to the Regimental Dinning facility. The Junior Ranks Complex (JRC) consists of The Home Bank and Mouse Hole bars, the HIVE, WRVS (with free internet access, snooker, tea & coffee), and the barber shop. The Fitness Club has a well-equipped gym, squash courts, swimming pool and all weather tennis courts. Dependants of serving personnel can apply for membership to use fitness club facilities. There are a number of sports clubs with membership open to all Minley Station personnel including the sub-aqua club, clay pigeon club and angling club. The Station has Officers’, WOs’ & Sgts’ and Cpls’ Messes.

Minley Parish Church is located in the grounds of the Officers’ Mess whilst the welfare hub offers a multi-prayer room.

Shorncliffe HIVE serving Shorncliffe, Folkestone, Dover, Hawkinge, Ashford

Shorncliffe is located near Folkestone in south-east Kent. It is close to the Continent, with the coast of France being visible across the Straits of Dover. Landmarks include the harbour and the white horse that is carved above the Channel Tunnel terminal.
Folkestone is at the eastern end of the M20 which provides fast access to Ashford, Maidstone, London and the M25.

The Eurotunnel that links to Calais, France in just 35 minutes is within 5 minutes drive of Shorncliffe. There is easy and fast access to London (less than an hour by high speed train), Gatwick airport and Heathrow airport. Nearby towns of Ashford, Canterbury, Dover, Hawkinge are easily accessible by road or rail.

The town offers a range of sports activities with sports and leisure centres, golf courses, a racecourse, football, cricket, rugby, running and bowls clubs. Bouverie Place, a shopping centre in the heart of Folkestone, has many high street names and independent stores, and nearby towns such as Ashford and Canterbury also offer a wide range of shopping choices. The second largest shopping mall of the country, Bluewater, is just less than an hour’s drive. There are numerous primary and secondary schools and various colleges and universities nearby for further education. The HIVE can provide details on request.

Sir John Moore Barracks is well established with various amenities for the soldiers and their dependants. The Primary HealthCare Centre fulfils the healthcare needs of serving personnel. A well established education centre provides learning opportunities for the serving soldiers and their families through various courses. It is supplemented by the Army Library and Information Centre offering Learn Direct courses. There is an internet suite open to service personnel and dependants for a minimal charge. There is also a well equipped gymnasium and swimming pool that are accessible to both soldiers and their dependants. There is a temple accessible to Hindi worshippers. Other facilities include a Visitors’ Centre, PRI Shop, Spar Shop, Army Welfare Service, Welfare Contact House, British Forces Broadcasting Services (BFBS) Office, barber’s shop and a cash point. The Shorncliffe Community Centre in Mackenzie Drive houses the Unit Welfare Office (which has supporting branches at Dover and Canterbury), the HIVE, Gurkha Welfare Support Workers and the neighbourhood watch police.

Thorney Island HIVE serving Thorney Island

Thorney Island is a Peninsula in Chichester Harbour, West Sussex. As well as having a large area of easily accessible sandy beach; the Island also has various sites of Special Scientific Interest. The harbour is a bird sanctuary, and as you drive on to Thorney Island there are two small lakes which are home to many different varieties of birds.

The population of the Island is made up entirely of soldiers from 47 Regt RA & 12 Regt RA, and their families. 47 Regt comprises of 4 Batteries, 31HQ, 10, 21 and 25/170, a REME Workshops and 63 Sigs Sqn (V) TA. 12 Regt is made up of 12 (Minden) Bty, 9 (Plassey) Bty, 58 (Eyres) Bty and T(HQ) Bty. Around 250 families are quartered on the Island, with further quarters in Emsworth, Gosport and Chichester. For enquiries about the allocation of quarters on Thorney Island contact the Defence House Executive Housing Information Centre serving the south-east.

There is a Spar shop on the Island, which can satisfy many shopping needs and has a Link cash machine, and hot snacks. For requirements further afield, the nearest village is Emsworth (1 mile), and Havant (3 miles); the nearest cities are Chichester and Portsmouth (both 9 miles in opposite directions).

The amenities on Thorney Island include a Community Centre which is home to the HIVE and TJ’s Diner (serving hot drinks and snacks daily). It also has a crèche, kindergarten, primary school, stables and saddle club, St Nicholas Church, JNCO Bar, beauty salon, Youth Cadets, RA Yacht Club and water sports centre, Army Learning Centre, and a gymnasium with various facilities. The nearest contact house is at Rowner in Gopsport, 10 mins from the M27 and close to local amenities.

Worthy Down HIVE serving Worthy Down, Harestock and Barton Stacey

Worthy Down is located approximately 5 miles from the historic city of Winchester. Army Training Regiment (Winchester) is situated approximately 1 mile from Worthy Down on the Winchester road. Winchester has excellent road and rail links, it is 1 hour by rail to London Waterloo. Southampton, Basingstoke and Portsmouth are all within easy travelling distance, each has excellent shopping facilities.

Worthy Down camp is easily accessible to the M3, M4 and is located adjacent to the A34.

The following facilities are available within camp: the community centre which hosts several clubs, a playgroup and crèche, a gymnasium complex which boasts excellent facilities, shops and hairdresser/barber.
