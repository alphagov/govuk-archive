ERDF regional guidance: Yorkshire and the Humber

Yorkshire and the Humber has been allocated €583 million of European Regional Development Funding to invest in projects that will support regional businesses and create jobs.

Introduction

The European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) is a funding stream which is allocated to regions by the European Union to stimulate regional economies.

Yorkshire and the Humber is one of 9 regions to qualify for Competitiveness and Employment funding.

Under the 2007 to 2013 programme, the region can receive €583.6 million of investment.

Priorities

The Yorkshire and the Humber operational programme document (see ‘Programme documents’ below) outlines the 4 main investment priorities for the region.

Priority 1: promoting innovation and research and development

To stimulate an innovative culture and strengthen the innovation system locally by increasing technology transfer from universities, institutes and businesses to the business base. This will improve products and processes, promoting networking and setting the framework for businesses to respond to the challenges and opportunities of the knowledge economy.

Priority 2: supporting and stimulating successful enterprise

Promoting a greater shift towards the knowledge-intensive growth clusters and sectors locally by increasing the number of businesses and occupations in high growth and knowledge industries by investing in key business sectors to accelerate economic growth and encourage high value added businesses and by developing a more entrepreneurial culture.

Priority 3: sustainable communities

Targeting is based on existing community regeneration initiatives and the most deprived communities. The focus is on tackling exclusion, creating enterprise opportunities and extending the social economy.

Priority 4 (South Yorkshire phasing-in only): economic infrastructure and place-based regeneration for the development of a knowledge-based economy within South Yorkshire

Priority 4 focuses on rehabilitating the physical environment and building on natural and cultural assets that directly support the development of innovative and job creating businesses. It supports the creation of stronger cities, towns and communities to ensure they are attractive places to live, work and invest.

Programme documents

Operational programme

The Yorkshire and the Humber ERDF programme for 2007 to 2013 was approved by the European Commission on 7 December 2007.

The operational programme describes how Yorkshire and the Humber benefits from €583 million from the European Union’s Regional Competitiveness and Employment Objective. The funding aims to boost economic performance locally through investments aimed at increasing innovation, competitiveness and employment. It has been developed by a strong local partnership and outlines the broad strategic direction for investment of this European funding during the period.

The programme has adopted 2 common themes to cut across all investments in each of the priorities:

  • environmental good practice
  • equality and diversity

Technical assistance strategy

Technical assistance ensures the smooth management and administration of the programme, and the effective engagement of local partners. It finances the preparatory management, monitoring, evaluation, information and control activities of the programme together with activities to reinforce the administrative capacity for implementing ERDF in Yorkshire and the Humber. Technical assistance accounts for 4% of the programme’s financial allocation.

A technical assistance strategy outlines how this funding will be spent and managed throughout the lifetime of the programme. It was originally approved by the programme monitoring committee on 20 December 2007. The plan was revised in 2012 and approved by the local management committee to reflect the new ERDF management arrangements.

Local management committee

The local management committee is the partnership body that governs Yorkshire and the Humber’s ERDF programme. Chaired by Patrick White from the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG), the committee draws members from local representatives from across the public, private, voluntary and local authority sectors. A European Commission representative attends the meetings.

The local management committee replaced the programme monitoring committee following the change in delivery arrangements for English ERDF programmes in July 2011. The change reflects the government’s localism agenda. The government is committed to giving localities and communities greater control and greater influence over the programmes and services delivered in their areas. The committee was restructured to ensure that, within the parameters already agreed with the European Union, local people and businesses can influence the shape of the programme.

The committee gives strategic direction to the operational programme and ensures that it is delivered compliantly and that outputs are delivered.

Latest news

Progress update: August 2012

Yorkshire and the Humber’s ERDF programme 2007 to 2013 has invested ERDF with 94 separate projects. Cumulatively, these projects have been allocated £308.8 million from ERDF and have cumulative eligible expenditure (ERDF and match funding) of £668.7 million. This means that the programme has contracts for over 65% of the total amount of ERDF available to invest.

N+2 performance

The European Commission sets spending targets (in euros) for the programme to meet throughout its lifetime. These are known as N+2 targets. It is important that this spending profile is maintained as any amount unspent against target at the end of each year is decommitted from the programme value and the potential investment is lost locally. Yorkshire and the Humber’s programme has achieved all its spend targets to date. However, the 2012 target of €303 million is a considerable challenge.
