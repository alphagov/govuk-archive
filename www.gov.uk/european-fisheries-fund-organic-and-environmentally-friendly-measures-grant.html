European Fisheries Fund: organic and environmentally friendly measures

Compensation for organic and environmentally friendly aquaculture businesses and projects, who can apply and how much you could receive.

Introduction

The European Fisheries Fund (EFF) provides compensation for using aquaculture production methods that help to protect and improve the environment and to conserve nature.

Who can apply?

You, as the applicant, must sign the application form, even if you had help completing it as you will be responsible for making sure that all of the terms and conditions of the grant are met.

Compensation can be awarded to any individual, business or co-operative involved in aquaculture. Compensation is limited to micro, small or medium-sized enterprises in the private sector or those that have fewer than 750 employees and a turnover of less than €200 million (according to latest exchange rates). You can establish the size of your business by using the following table.

Table showing the criteria for establishing the size of your business

Category Number of employees Turnover Balance sheet total
Medium Up to 250 Less than or equal to €50 million Less than or equal to €43 million
Small Up to 50 Less than or equal to €10 million Less than or equal to €10 million
Micro Up to 10 Less than or equal to €2 million Less than or equal to €2 million

You must respect the number of employees threshold when you are determining the size of your enterprise but you may choose to meet either the turnover or balance sheet total maximum. You could exceed one of them without losing your status. Further information is available in ‘The new SME definition: User guide and model declaration’ on the EU website (PDF 1.2MB).

What things may be eligible for compensation?

The EFF aims to promote:

  • forms of aquaculture that protect and enhance the environment, natural resources, genetic diversity and managing the landscape and traditional features of aquaculture zones
  • participation in the European Community Eco-Management and Audit Scheme
  • organic aquaculture as defined by Council Regulation (EC) No 834/2007

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 165KB).

How much compensation will I get?

The compensation is based on the average income of the local aquaculture farms and on the basis of loss of revenue and additional costs that result from using the organic and environmentally friendly methods.

A one-off compensation can be given for:

  • forms of aquaculture that protect and enhance the environment, natural resources, genetic diversity and managing the landscape and traditional features of aquaculture zones on the basis of a maximum amount for each hectare of the area of the enterprise
  • organic aquaculture as defined by Council Regulation (EC) No 834/2007 for a maximum of 2 years during the period of conversion to organic production – more information on organic production and rules for animal production (PDF 165KB)

Compensation will be awarded to projects that demonstrate the best value for money. You will receive a percentage of the total eligible cost of your project if you are successful. The minimum grant is £400 and the maximum is £100,000.

You cannot receive money for your project from more than one European fund so you must confirm that you have not applied for funding from another scheme.

Table showing the maximum level of compensation available to different types of project

Project type Maximum level of grant available as a percentage of eligible costs
Small, micro and medium-sized enterprises and projects which deliver very high environmental contributions 100
Other applicants and projects 80

How to apply for funding

You should submit the Axis 2 application form to the MMO. Find out more about Axis 2 and download the application form from the MMO website.

Your application must include a detailed business case that shows that your project:

  • has quantified outputs and clear and attainable targets
  • will provide an additional and sustainable benefit to the fishing industry
  • has a justified need for grant will provide good value for money

You should also include:

  • a full description of your project
  • information on the economic viability of investments
  • information on any needed consents
  • the benefits of the project
  • information on how you calculated your costs
  • financial and other benefits
  • a cost-benefit analysis
  • an analysis of quotes
  • an environmental impact assessment
  • the results you expect from the project
  • how you will publicise the project
  • how you will monitor the project
  • how you will manage risks to delivery
  • information about your business
  • how your project will affect the local area

For advice on applying to the EFF, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2694 or 0191 376 2676 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

What is in the offer letter?

The offer letter sets out details of the conditions of the grant and any agreed performance targets and outputs for the project. You must comply with the conditions along with details of eligible expenditure to allow us to pay your grant claims.

More information from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

How to claim funding from the EFF

If you are eligible for funding under the EFF, you can claim your grant by submitting a claim form by the date specified in your offer letter to the MMO at the following address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
Lancaster House
Hampshire Court
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE4 7YH

Download the EFF claim form and guidance from the MMO website.

You should normally submit a single full and final claim by the date specified in your offer letter. You may be allowed to submit one or more interim claims but the final claim must still be submitted by the date specified in the offer letter.

The MMO aims to pay the grant within 8 weeks once all the necessary information to consider your claim is received. The MMO accept no liability in respect of loss attributable to any delay in the payment of claims or to any suspension, reduction or cancellation of grant.

The payment will be made electronically into your bank account.

More information on completing the claim form, supporting documentation, changing your project and how to manage your project from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.
