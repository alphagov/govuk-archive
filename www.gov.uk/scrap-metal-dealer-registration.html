Licence Scrap metal dealer registration (England, Wales and Northern Ireland)

Apply for this licence

Postcode lookup

Overview

You must register with the council as a scrap metal dealer if you buy, sell or store scrap metal in England, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Restrictions

You must keep a record of all scrap metal you receive, process or dispatch, including:

  • description and weight of the metal
  • date and time
  • name and address of the buyer or seller
  • price or value of the metal
  • registration numbers of any delivery vehicles

You must keep your records for 2 years, and allow the council to inspect your property and records if asked.

How to apply

You must tell the council:

  • your name
  • your home address or registered office
  • the address of your scrap metal store (if you have one)
  • how you carry out your business (eg if you work from home)

You’ll need to renew your registration every 3 years.

If your business changes, or if you stop dealing scrap metal, you must tell the council within 28 days.

Fines and penalties

You could be fined £1,000 if you don’t keep your records correctly, and also face the following conditions:

  • you’ll not be able to receive scrap metal between 6pm and 8am
  • you’ll have to keep any scrap metal you receive in the same place, in the form it was received, for at least 72 hours before moving it on

These conditions can last up to 2 years.

It’s illegal to receive scrap metal from anyone under 16, and to give false details when selling scrap metal.
