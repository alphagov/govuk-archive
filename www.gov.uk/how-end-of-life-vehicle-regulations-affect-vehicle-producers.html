How end-of-life vehicle regulations affect vehicle producers

Regulations implemented to reduce the environmental impact of end-of-life vehicles (ELVs), and how these affect vehicle producers.

Introduction

Each year in the UK up to two million vehicles become waste. These ELVs may have a serious impact on the environment.

Regulations have been implemented to reduce this impact by:

  • influencing vehicle design to make it easier to reuse, recover or recycle components
  • reducing the use of hazardous materials in vehicle manufacturing
  • standardising treatment of waste ELVs

In general, the regulations apply to passenger cars and light goods vehicles. This guide explains how producers of vehicles are affected by the Regulations and what steps they must take to comply.

Comply with the End-of-Life Vehicles Regulations 2003

Under the End-of-Life Vehicles Regulations 2003, vehicle producers must:

  • ensure that materials and components used in passenger cars and light goods vehicles do not contain lead, hexavalent chromium, cadmium or mercury. Spare, replacement and service parts for vehicles put on the market before 1 July 2003 are excluded from the regulations.
  • mark or code certain rubber and plastic vehicle parts (excluding tyres) weighing over 200 grams to promote dismantling, reuse, recycling or recovery.
  • provide - within six months of a new type of vehicle going on the market - information on the materials and hazardous substances that have been used and guidance on how vehicle components can be dismantled, re-used or recovered. This must be easily accessible to potential buyers and can be provided in promotional literature, written manuals, websites or on databases such as the International Dismantling Information System (IDIS). Find out about IDIS on the IDIS website.
  • compile documents to show that their vehicles, materials and components comply with the heavy metal restrictions and coding standards for plastic and rubber parts. Records must be kept for four years.

The Vehicle Certification Agency is responsible for ensuring compliance and can request to see your records or visit your premises to verify your control systems. Failure to comply with record keeping could result in prosecution.

Download advice on the End-of-life Vehicles Regulations from the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website (PDF, 155K).

Free take-back of end-of-life vehicles

The End-of-Life Vehicles (Producer Responsibility) Regulations 2005 require vehicle producers to make available a network of facilities where end-of-life vehicles (ELVs) can be taken - at no cost to the final owner - for dismantling and recycling.

These facilities are also required to ensure environmentally friendly disposal of ELVs and their components.

The regulations require producers and importers to register with the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) and state the brands of vehicles for which they are responsible for meeting recovery and recycling targets.

You can download advice on the End-of-Life Vehicles (Producer Responsibility) Regulations 2005 (PDF, 82K).

BIS must approve any take-back scheme your business would like to run before it begins operating.
