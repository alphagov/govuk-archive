European Fisheries Fund: investing on board your fishing vessel

Eligibility for grants to improve fishing vessels, the types of businesses and improvements that are supported and how to apply.

Introduction

If you want to invest in improvements to your fishing vessel, you may be eligible for a grant from the European Fisheries Fund (EFF).

Who can apply?

You, as the applicant, must sign the application form, even if you had help completing it as you will be responsible for making sure that all of the terms and conditions of the grant are met.

Grants for improvements are available to all vessel owners in the private sector.

There are some general requirements for getting a grant:

  • your vessel must have been on the EU fishing vessel register for at least 5 years
  • your vessel must be sound, sea-worthy and comply with statutory life-saving, fire-fighting, radio and collision regulations, have a current certificate of compliance and be insured
  • your home port must be in England
  • your vessel must be a commercial vessel
  • replacement gear must be more selective
  • improvements should not increase your ability to catch fish
  • you or your relatives should not have a financial interest in the companies that supply goods or services to your project
  • application costs will only be considered for equipment or work listed on the form

What happens if the improvements lead to increase tonnage?

You must agree to have your vessel re-measured after the work has been completed and get any additional licence cover for the changes by buying a licence from another vessel. You will need to provide the Marine Management Organisation (MMO) with evidence of the re-measurement and licence transaction before the claim is paid.

Your improvements may reduce the stability of your vessel. You should ask a qualified person such as a naval architect to confirm that your proposed improvements will not reduce your vessel’s stability. You should confirm this in your business case.

What things may be eligible for a grant?

Grants can be awarded for improvements to:

  • on board safety (above what is required by the Maritime and Coastguard Agency)
  • working conditions
  • hygiene
  • product quality
  • selectivity of fishing gear and methods
  • energy efficiency

You can also apply for funding to replace your engine. If you are doing this, your application must only be for this and no other improvements.

Grants are not usually available under this part of the EFF for:

  • aquaculture service vessels
  • winches that increase your ability to catch fish
  • automatic identification system (AIS)
  • VAT
  • running or operating costs
  • interest on debts
  • if work is started on the project before the starting date in the offer letter

Read more about eligible and not eligible projects in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 156KB).

How much grant will I get?

The EFF programme is competitive, and only projects that most effectively meet its aims will be awarded a grant. You will receive a percentage of the total eligible cost of your project if you are successful. The minimum grant is £500 and the maximum grant is £60,000 (except engine replacements where the maximum is £100,000).

How to apply for funding

You should submit the Axis 1 application form to the MMO. Find out more about Axis 1 and download the application form from the MMO website.

Your application must include a detailed business case that shows that your project:

  • has quantified outputs and clear and attainable targets
  • will provide an additional and sustainable benefit to the fishing industry
  • has a justified need for grant
  • will provide good value for money

You should also include:

  • a full description of your project
  • why the work is needed
  • the benefits of the project
  • financial, environmental and other benefits
  • a cost-benefit analysis
  • an analysis of quotes
  • the results you expect from the project
  • how you will monitor the project
  • how you will manage risks to delivery
  • information about your business

Smaller projects just require a simple statement of costs and benefits with the appropriate number of quotes along with copies of your company accounts will be sufficient for the projects involving:

  • improvements to working conditions
  • safety equipment additional to that required by Maritime and Coastguard Agency rules
  • facilities to maximise fish quality where the costs are less than £1,250
  • the adoption of sustainable catching methods where the costs are less than £1,250 (in this case, we will also need the details of your current and future fishing position)

You should make sure that you describe all the costs and benefits of your project without double counting.

For advice on applying to the EFF, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2694 or 0191 376 2676 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

What is in the offer letter?

The offer letter sets out details of the conditions of the grant and any agreed performance targets and outputs for the project. You must comply with the conditions along with details of eligible expenditure to allow us to pay your grant claims.

More information from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

How to claim funding from the EFF

If you are eligible for funding under the EFF, you can claim your grant by submitting a claim form by the date specified in your offer letter to the MMO at the following address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
Lancaster House
Hampshire Court
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE4 7YH

Download the EFF claim form and guidance from the MMO website.

You should normally submit a single full and final claim by the date specified in your offer letter. You may be allowed to submit one or more interim claims but the final claim must still be submitted by the date specified in the offer letter.

The MMO aims to pay the grant within 8 weeks once all the necessary information to consider your claim is received. The MMO accept no liability in respect of loss attributable to any delay in the payment of claims or to any suspension, reduction or cancellation of grant.

The payment will be made electronically into your bank account.

More information on completing the claim form, supporting documentation, changing your project and how to manage your project from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.
