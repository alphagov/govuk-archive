Quick answer Pay your VAT bill

Pay your VAT bill by Direct Debit, online with a debit or credit card, through your bank or by cheque at a bank or building society.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HMRC on time. Use the payment deadline calculator to make sure you pay on time - you may be charged a penalty if you’re payment is late.

Online or telephone banking, Bacs, CHAPS or standing order

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

You need HMRC’s bank details or you can use the CHAPS form or standing order form. Use your VAT registration number as the payment reference.

Direct Debit

Use your VAT online account to set up a Direct Debit at least 2 working days before your VAT Return and payment are due (or 3 working days, if these are due on a weekend or bank holiday).

HMRC will tell you when the Direct Debit has been set up. The payment deadline calculator tells you when the payment leaves your account. If you need to make changes to your Direct Debit, do this through your bank.

You must pay by another method until the Direct Debit is set up.

Annual Accounting Scheme

If you use the Annual Accounting Scheme, you can only set up automatic Direct Debit online for balancing payments. You can set up a Direct Debit for regular payments using form VAT623.

Download ‘Form VAT623, Direct Debit Instruction’ (PDF, 336KB)

Online with a debit or credit card

You can pay online - you’ll need your VAT registration number.

You must use a card from a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Your bank or building society

You need to order paying-in slips to pay this way. You can pay by cash or cheque made payable to ‘HM Revenue & Customs only’ followed by your VAT registration number. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

Order paying-in slips

You can order paying-in slips online or by phone (delivery can take up to 6 weeks). If you need to pay before then, use another method. For payslips in Welsh, call the Welsh orderline.

HMRC Paying-in Slip orderline
01702 366376 or 01702 366314

Welsh Paying-in Slips orderline
0845 010 0300

Post

Most VAT-registered business can’t pay VAT by post. HMRC has more details.
