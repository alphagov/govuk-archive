The National Careers Council

The National Careers Council provides advice to government on strategy for the National Careers Service and allied career support services.

The government’s vision for a better quality of information, advice and guidance on learning and work is set out in the document The right advice at the right time , published in April 2012, the month the National Careers Service was launched.

The National Careers Council is chaired by Dr Deirdre Hughes, OBE, with 10 members and 3 associate experts. The Council has been tasked to produce an annual report to government in March 2013. The council’s secretariat is provided by the Department for Business Innovation & Skills (BIS).

The Council’s work

The Council is taking forward three initial work strands:

Access, quality and professionalism

Strand Leader: David McNerney in association with Ruth Spellman, OBE, Rajay Naik and Steve Higginbotham.

Connectivity of National Careers Service to business and the wider careers market

Strand Leader: Jenny Rudge, OBE in association with Linda Taylor, OBE; Professor Tony Watts, OBE; Brian Lightman; and Nick Chambers.

Intelligence, innovation and impact

Strand Leader: Kieran Gordon in association with Chris Heaume, OBE; Simon Surtees; and Heather Jackson.

Meetings

The inaugural meeting of Council took place in London on 21 May 2012. This was followed by meetings on 12 June 2012 and 24 September 2012 with John Hayes, MP, then Minister for Further Education, Skills and Lifelong Learning (jointly with the Department for Education) and government policy-makers, to discuss and agree the proposed Council work strands for 2012 to 2013.

  • Statement by Dr Deirdre Hughes, OBE, Chair, on behalf of the National Careers Council, 20 June 2012
  • Statement by Dr Deirdre Hughes, OBE, Chair, on behalf of the National Careers Council, 3 October 2012

Evidence to Lord Heseltine Review

On 30 August 2012 members of the NCC met with the Lord Heseltine review team of how spending departments and other relevant public sector bodies interact with the private sector, and to assess their capacity to deliver pro-growth policies. The meeting covered:

  • raising the work-readiness of school leavers and those acquiring tertiary qualifications
  • remedying skills deficiencies in the current workforce
  • addressing the longstanding NEETs (not in education, employment or training) problem and encouraging apprenticeships and support for the lower skilled

A copy of the written evidence submitted to Lord Heseltine’s Review can be found here:

Summary of evidence relating to Education, Employment and Skills presented on 30 August 2012 to the Lord Heseltine Review benchmarking UK performance against a range of economic indicators

NCC work plan

The National Careers Council is currently auditing key policy themes and has identified seven major challenges that will inform its ongoing work. A work plan, formally presented to the minister on 2 July 2012, sets out key priorities and plans for 2012 to 2013.

National Careers Council: Workplan

A further update will be produced in September 2012.

Next stage plans

The next 2 Council meetings are scheduled to be held on 14 January 2013 and 18 April 2013. Dr Hughes will be meeting with interested parties to find ways of connecting organisations and individuals to feed into the Council’s work. The Council will develop a more detailed work plan for each of the three work strands and look at ways to involve others in its work.

If you would like to feed in your views, please submit them to the Council Secretariat: robert.canniff@bis.gsi.gov.uk

National Careers Council - members’ biographies

Dr Deirdre Hughes OBE - Chair

Deirdre was awarded an OBE in the 2012 New Year Honours List for her services to career guidance. She is a Commissioner at the UK Commission for Employment & Skills (July 2011). She is also an Associate Fellow at the Institute for Employment Research (IER), Warwick University, an Associate at the Centre for Educational Sociology, Edinburgh University. Deirdre is a ‘Lead Consultant’ within the European Lifelong Guidance Policy Network on quality assurance and evidence, and active member of the International Centre for Career Development and Public Policy (ICCDP). She is also Director of DMH Associates based in Derbyshire.

Linda JC Taylor OBE

Linda’s career has been in the careers guidance sector, most recently as Chief Executive of Connexions South London. She is a Fellow of ICG a past member of the National Apprenticeship Group and Secretary General of the IAEVG. Linda was awarded the OBE in 2008 for public and voluntary services to young people.

Heather Jackson

Heather Jackson has over 20 years’ experience of working closely with government on the development of career guidance for adults, including acting as External Policy Adviser to the Cross-government Review of IAG for Adults (2005-2007); as Head of Information, Advice and Guidance at the National Health Service University and involvement in the initial design and development of the current telephone and face-to-face services for adults. Heather currently chairs NAEGA (National Association of Guidance for Adults) and is a member of the CPA Executive.

Chris Heaume OBE

Since 1974 Chris has had a substantial career with young people in London as a youth worker, FE manager, Connexions CEO, Ofsted Inspector and Trustee of the Partnership for Young London, recognised in an OBE in 2007.

David McNerney

David McNerney is HR Director at Sellafield Ltd, which is responsible for safely delivering decommissioning, reprocessing and nuclear waste management activities on behalf of the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority.

David has spent the last 27 years working in Human Resources, Change and Business Services roles, both in the UK and abroad. He has worked in a variety of industries, including manufacturing, telecoms, and utility management.

Rajay Naik

Rajay Naik is Director of Government and External Affairs at The Open University. He is also Chairman of UK Programmes at the Big Lottery Fund, a Commissioner at the Department of Health and serving in his sixth year as a Governor at a major FE college. Rajay was formerly a member of the Browne Review into higher education funding, and is a former Chairman of the British Youth Council. He has served on the boards of the National Youth Agency, Learning and Skills Council and the Changemakers Foundation.

Professor Tony Watts OBE

Tony Watts is a Founding Fellow and Life President of the National Institute for Careers Education and Counselling, and Visiting Professor at the University of Derby and at Canterbury Christ Church University. A co-founder of CRAC, he has carried out many international studies of career guidance systems.

Ruth Spellman OBE

WEA’s new Chief Executive Ruth Spellman has had a career marked by a commitment to lifelong learning. She was awarded an OBE in 2007 for services to workplace learning and an Honorary Doctorate from Cranfield in 2010. Ruth was formerly Chief Executive of the Chartered Management Institute (CMI), and has previously been CEO of the Institution of Mechanical Engineers and Investors in People (IIP) UK. She has also been HR Director of the NSPCC.

Jenny Rudge OBE

Jenny Rudge is the Chief Executive of Careers South West Ltd and has 36 years experience of working in the careers sector. She has previously served on the National Taskforce for Modern Apprenticeships and as an advisor to the DFES for the Every Child Matters initiative. Jenny was awarded an OBE for services to careers education and guidance in 2002.

Kieran Gordon

Kieran has 30 years experience of working in the careers guidance sector, including latterly as Chief Executive of Greater Merseyside Connexions (GMCP). Kieran was a member of the UK Taskforce on the Careers Profession and served on the National Careers Service Advisory Group, and served on the Presidential team of the Institute of Careers Guidance (ICG) for 3 years.

Steve Higginbotham

A career guidance professional - Chief Executive of igen (a provider of careers/IAG, training and employability services); Director and Immediate Past President of Institute of Career Guidance; Director of Careers England (and Chair of Adult Task Group); Director of Careers Yorkshire & the Humber (National Careers Service prime contractor); member of the Careers Profession Alliance (CPA) Executive Group.

Simon Surtees MA DipCG - associate member

Simon qualified as a Careers Adviser in 1981 and has worked across London throughout his career. He brings to the Council experience of Careers Guidance with individuals of all ages and extensive experience of managing and training Careers Advisers and Personal Advisers. He is also a Governor of St Marks Academy in Mitcham. He is currently a Project Manager with CfBT Include working with Training Providers in West and South London.

Brian Lightman - associate member

Brian Lightman taught modern languages in SE England and had two headships in S Wales before becoming General Secretary of the Association of School and College Leaders (ASCL) in September 2010. He served on the Board of Careers Wales Cardiff and the Vale of Glamorgan from 2004-2012.

Nick Chambers - associate member

Nick Chambers is the director of the Education and Employers Taskforce. The charity aims to make it easier for education and employers to work together to help young people. It undertakes research and runs Speakers for Schools and Inspiring the Future.
