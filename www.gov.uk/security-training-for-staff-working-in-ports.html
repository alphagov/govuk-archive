Security training for staff working in ports

Find TRANSEC-approved Port Facility Security Officers and training resources, and how to host a maritime security roadshow for port staff.

Introduction

If you manage a port facility in the UK, you will need to appoint a Port Facility Security Officer (PFSO). This is a requirement under the International Ship and Port Facility Code (ISPS Code). The code is implemented in the UK through the Ship and Port Facility (Security) Regulations 2004, as amended.

PFSOs will need to be appointed to cover each port facility, although a single officer may have responsibility for more than one facility. The definition of a ‘port facility’ includes areas such as anchorages, waiting berths and approaches from seaward. This can range from a large sea freight terminal to an individual berth or wharf.

Any PFSO you appoint must complete a training course delivered by a training provider approved by the Transport Security and Contingencies (TRANSEC) directorate at the Department for Transport.

You will also need to provide training for staff carrying out security functions. You should consider providing general security awareness training to other, non-security staff working at the port facility.

This guide explains how to find TRANSEC-approved PFSO trainers. It will point you towards TRANSEC’s training resources for security staff and other employees at ports, and explain how your staff can attend its maritime security roadshows.

Port Facility Security Officer training

All PFSOs in the UK must attend a training course run by a provider approved by the Transport Security and Contingencies (TRANSEC) directorate at the DfT.

The aim of the training is to provide an appointed PFSO with the knowledge and skills needed to comply with the rules and regulations set out in the ISPS Code.

List of approved PFSO training providers.

Non-approved training

Some training companies who have not been accredited and have not sought approval from TRANSEC are currently offering PFSO course training to ports and port facilities.

TRANSEC does not recognise certification or approval from any training provider other than those listed on the DfT website. If the PFSO you have appointed has already attended a course delivered by an unapproved training provider, they may need to attend further courses from an approved provider on the DfT list.

You should contact TRANSEC if you have been approached by an unapproved training provider.

Getting approved as a training provider

If you want to deliver PFSO training courses, you can find out how to apply for TRANSEC approval by writing to:

Maritime Security - PFSO Training TRANSEC Department for Transport Zone 5/5 Southside 105 Victoria Street London SW1E 6DT

You can also contact TRANSEC Maritime Security on Tel 020 7944 2844 or email maritimesecurity@dft.gsi.gov.uk

Training material from the DfT covering port security training

If you manage staff that work in and around ports and ships, you can use training resources from the DfT as part of your security training programme.

As well as resources for training staff with specific security duties, the DfT also provides material for the general security awareness training of all staff.

Training films

You can order training films from the DfT covering the following specific maritime security topics:

  • Safe Harbours: Port Security and You - suitable for induction training of all staff working in ports
  • Maritime Freight Security - for security staff working in passenger (PAX) or container roll-on roll-off (CRR) port facilities
  • Security: Everybody’s Business - intended to assist with the security awareness training of ships’ crews

These films are supplied free of charge to UK port operators and companies operating UK-registered ships.

Other training films which are not specific to the maritime environment but cover the provision of effective security measures are also available.

Learning packages

The DfT also provides hard copy training materials for maritime security.

  • Training for port security staff - a set of voluntary training modules intended for the initial training and refresher training of security staff which cover all the requirements set out in the port facility security instructions issued by the DfT
  • Drills and exercises guidance - a document that acts as an ‘aide memoire’ and provides basic guidance to those involved in organising maritime security drills and exercises

More information about the range of maritime security training aids and how to order them.

Maritime security roadshows run by DfT

Maritime security roadshows are one-day events aimed at all staff working on ships - particularly passenger vessels - and in port environments.

The roadshows are run jointly by TRANSEC directorate at the DfT and the Security Service. They are held throughout the year, usually in port locations.

Dates are set aside each year by TRANSEC for the roadshows. Individual ports and shipping companies are then invited to submit bids in writing to host a roadshow on a particular date. These are usually accepted on a ‘first come, first served’ basis.

The roadshows are provided free of charge, but if you are hosting one you will be asked to provide certain facilities, including:

  • lecture rooms
  • TV, video and projection facilities
  • refreshments, including a buffet lunch, for delegates and presenters

What happens during the day

The day’s programme typically includes:

  • information on the threat to passenger shipping operations
  • a brief overview of the relevant legislation
  • an introduction to the devices, weapons and tactics used by terrorist groups
  • an explanation of the role of TRANSEC and how it monitors compliance

How to host or attend a roadshow

A minimum of 20 delegates is normally required for each roadshow.

If you are interested in hosting an event, you should contact TRANSEC Maritime Security on 020 7944 2844 for available dates and details of how to bid.

You should also contact TRANSEC if you have staff that would benefit from attending a roadshow, but insufficient numbers to host your own event. It may be possible for TRANSEC to make arrangements for your staff to attend somebody else’s event, provided that the hosting organisation agrees to this.
