Part 1: Overview

Benefit payments and entitlement may change or stop if you, your partner or one of your children is:

  • sent to prison or a young offenders’ institution
  • in custody awaiting trial (on remand)

You can’t claim State Pension while you’re in prison.

If you’re in prison or on remand

You can get help from a benefits advisor to suspend or close down benefits you’re no longer able to claim if you go to prison or on remand.

If your partner or child is in prison or on remand

If your partner or child has gone to prison or is on remand, you must tell whoever pays your benefits and find out:

  • if your benefit claims will be affected
  • if you can claim other benefits

If your partner has gone to prison you may still be entitled to benefits, as long as you satisfy the benefit entitlement conditions in your own right.
