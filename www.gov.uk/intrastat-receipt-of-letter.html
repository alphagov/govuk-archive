Introduction

This guide provides information and actions that may be required for any letters you have received. The guide covers the following letters:

Letter content Letter reference
Potential new Arrival traders to Intrastat 120 / 220
Only required to submit Arrivals 121
Potential new Dispatch traders to Intrastat 130 / 230
Only required to submit Dispatches 131
Potential new Arrival and Dispatch traders 140 / 240
Submission of both Arrivals and Dispatches 141
No longer required to submit Supplementary Declaration 151
Approaching the Intrastat threshold 400

For basic Intrastat information, see the introduction to Intrastat.

For intermediate information read the guide on Intrastat: reporting the value and volume of intra-EU trade.

For detailed guidance, review the guide on Supplementary Declarations for Intrastat.

Intrastat Letter Reference 120 / 220

You have received a letter because the figures entered in box 9 on your VAT return suggest that the goods you import from the European Union (EU) exceed the current Intrastat threshold for arrivals.

Check the figures on your VAT return to ensure only the value of goods is included, not related services. Do not include:

  • commission, legal and financial services
  • insurance, freight and/or carriage (unless it is included with the cost of the goods)
  • labour
  • goods bought and sold within the EU but which do not actually enter or leave the UK

If the figures on your VAT return are incorrect, please email intrastatenquiries@hmrc.gsi.gov.uk or complete the online amendment form below.

Complete the amendment form for boxes 8 and/or 9 of the VAT return on the uktradeinfo website.

Once your company’s cumulative monthly imports from the EU exceed the Intrastat threshold, your company is legally obliged to submit monthly Supplementary Declaration forms to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). The data you send to HMRC is used to produce trade statistics, an essential part of the UK Balance of Payments. It is therefore important that the information supplied is accurate. If it is not supplied correctly, penalty action could be taken.

For further information on Intrastat, read the guide: Intrastat - step by step process.

Intrastat Letter Reference 121

You have received a letter because last year’s records suggest that you either submitted Arrivals and Dispatches Supplementary Declarations (SDs), or Dispatches only.

Records indicate that from 2012 you are required only to submit Arrival SDs.

Although HMRC only requires Arrivals SDs, during 2011 you will also need to monitor your Dispatches trade within the European Union (EU). If you exceed the dispatch threshold of £250,000, then you must submit Dispatches SDs starting from the month that you first exceeded this threshold.

The Threshold is set at £600,000 for Arrivals in 2012.

If you disagree with any of this information then please email intrastatenquiries@hmrc.gsi.gov.uk or complete the amendment form below.

Complete the amendment form for boxes 8 and/or 9 of the VAT return on the uktradeinfo website.

Intrastat Letter Reference 131

You have received a letter because last year the records you submitted suggest that you either submitted Dispatches and Arrivals, or Arrivals only.

HMRC records indicate that from 2012 you are only required to submit Dispatches SDs.

Although HMRC only require Dispatches SDs, during 2012 you will also need to monitor your Arrivals trade within the EU. If you exceed the threshold of £600,000 for Arrivals, then you must submit Arrivals SDs starting from the month that you first exceeded this threshold.

Intrastat Letter Reference 140 / 240

You have received a letter because the figures entered in boxes 8 and 9 on your VAT return suggest that the goods you import from and export to the EU exceed the current Intrastat threshold of £250,000 for Dispatches and £600,000 for Arrivals.

Check the figures on your VAT return to ensure that only the value of goods is included, not related services. Do not include:

  • commission, legal and financial services

  • labour
  • goods bought and sold within the EU but which do not actually enter or leave the UK

Once your company’s cumulative monthly imports from the EU exceed the Intrastat threshold, your company is legally obliged to submit monthly Supplementary Declaration forms to HMRC. The data you send is used to produce trade statistics, an essential part of the UK Balance of Payments. It is therefore important that the information supplied is accurate. Intrastat Letter Reference 141

You have received a letter because last year you were required to submit either Arrivals or Dispatches SDs.

From the figures provided in boxes 8 and/or 9 of your 2011 VAT returns, it appears that from 2012 you are required to submit both Arrival and Dispatch SDs.

For 2012 the thresholds are set at £600,000 Arrivals and £250,000 for Dispatches.

If you disagree with this information please email intrastatenquiries@hmrc.gsi.gov.uk or complete the online amendment form below.

Complete the amendment form for boxes 8 and/or 9 of the VAT return on the uktradeinfo website.

In this example, the above company exceeded the 2011 Intrastat threshold for Arrivals in August and for Dispatches in December.

The thresholds for 2011 were set at £600,000 for Arrivals and £250,000 for Dispatches.

Arrivals SDs would have been sent in monthly from August 2011 onwards. Dispatch SDs would have been due from December. Both Arrivals and Dispatches SDs should continue to be sent in for the whole of 2012.

Intrastat Letter Reference 151

Last year you were required to submit either Arrivals and/or Dispatches SDs.

You have received a letter because from the figures provided in boxes 8 and/or 9 of your 2011 VAT returns, it appears that you have not exceeded the Intrastat threshold of £250,000 for Dispatches and £600,000 for Arrivals, and so you will not be required to submit Arrival or Dispatch SDs from the January 2012 period.

If you disagree with this information please email intrastatenquiries@hmrc.gsi.gov.uk or complete the online amendment form below.

Complete the amendment form for boxes 8 and/or 9 of the VAT return on the uktradeinfo website.

You will need to continue to monitor the value of your foreign trade within the EU from 1 January 2012, and if the cumulative monthly total of either Box 8 and/or Box 9 exceeds the threshold of £250,000 for Dispatches and £600,000 for Arrivals, you will then have to start submitting SDs again (see example below).

In the above example, we show a simplified, fictitious account sheet of a company’s business for the year 2012. Although this company did not have to submit Intrastat SDs for the beginning of 2012, by monitoring their trade they found that they exceeded the threshold for Dispatches in September 2012. SDs for Dispatches would then be required from September onwards. As the threshold was not exceeded for Arrivals, no Arrival SDs are required.

  • labour
  • goods bought and sold within the EU but which do not actually enter or leave the UK
