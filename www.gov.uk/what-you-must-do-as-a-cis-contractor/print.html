What you must do as a Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) contractor, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Register with CIS

Anyone who uses subcontractors for construction work needs to register with the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS).

CIS is administered by HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). It regulates payments from contractors to subcontractors in the building industry.

How to register

To register as a contractor, you follow the process for setting up as a new employer. You can do this by:

  • using HMRC’s new employer registration service online
  • registering as a new employer by email
  • calling the HMRC new employers’ helpline

You’ll need:

  • the name you want to register with (eg your name, business name)
  • the unique taxpayer reference number (UTR) for your business
  • a National Insurance Number

If you’re based abroad but do construction work in the UK you still need to register with CIS, but the registration process is different.

Rules you must follow

When you’re registered under CIS, you must:

  • check with HMRC that your subcontractors are registered under CIS
  • make the right CIS deductions (tax and National Insurance) to payments you make to your subcontractors
  • pay CIS deductions to HMRC
  • file monthly returns with HMRC
  • keep full CIS records
  • let HMRC know about any changes to your status

Part 2: Verifying and paying your subcontractors

Before paying your subcontractors you must check their employment status - you can do this with the employment status tool on the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) website.

You only need to make CIS deductions from payments to subcontractors - not salaried employees.

You must ‘verify’ all your subcontractors.

Verify your subcontractors

Verifying is checking to see if your subcontractors are registered with HMRC.

If your subcontractors aren’t registered under CIS, HMRC will let you know. You’ll make CIS deductions of 30% instead of 20% for unregistered subcontractors.

To verify subcontractors with HMRC, you can use:

  • the HMRC CIS online service - you can also use this to file your monthly returns
  • commercial CIS software

You can also call the CIS helpine:

0845 366 7899

To verify your subcontractors, you’ll need:

  • the name of your business or organisation
  • your Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR)
  • the reference number for your HMRC accounts office
  • your HMRC employer reference

You will also need your subcontractors’:

  • name or business name
  • UTR
  • National Insurance (NI) number if they’re a sole trader
  • partner’s name and UTR or NI number if they’re in a partnership
  • company registration number if they’re a limited company

If you have large numbers of subcontractors

You can verify subcontractors and file CIS data using the Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) system.

This allows you to transfer large amounts of information quickly and easily (you don’t have to have a certain number of subcontractors to use this system).

There’s more information about this on the HMRC website.

Pay your subcontractors

When you verify your subcontractors, HMRC will tell you the right rate to deduct from any payments you make to them - or whether to pay them gross.

You can pay your subcontractors directly or through a third party if they ask you to.

If you make deductions from subcontractors’ payments, you must give them a payment and deduction statement.

Part 3: Pay CIS deductions to HMRC

You must pay HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) any Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) deductions you make from payments to your CIS subcontractors.

The standard rate of deduction is 20%, but there is also a higher rate of 30%. You’ll take the higher rate from the earnings of some subcontractors - HMRC will tell you when to do this.

HMRC will tell you:

  • whether or not you need to make deductions
  • the rate you need to use

Payments to HMRC should be made every month - no later than the 19th day of the month - or for smaller amounts (less than £1,500), quarterly.

If you pay electronically, payment is due by the 22nd of each month.

There are instructions on how to pay CIS deductions to HMRC on the HMRC website.

Part 4: File your monthly returns

You must tell HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) about all the payments you have made to subcontractors under the CIS (Construction Industry Scheme) each month.

You can file your returns by using:

  • the HMRC CIS online service
  • commercial CIS software

Even if you don’t make payments to subcontractors every month, you must still let HMRC know by filing a ‘nil return’ for the months when you made no payments to subcontractors.

If you have large numbers of subcontractors

You can verify subcontractors and file CIS data using the Electronic Data Interchange (EDI) system.

This allows you to transfer large amounts of information quickly and easily (you don’t have to have a certain number of subcontractors to use this system).

There’s more information about this on the HMRC website.

Deadlines

Send your monthly returns to HMRC by the 19th day of the month.

Penalties for late returns

If you miss the deadline for filing returns, you’ll get a penalty, whatever the reason - even if you’re only a day late.

Penalties depend on how late your return is but they range from £100 to £3,000 or the total amount of CIS deductions shown on your late return - whichever is higher.

There’s more information about penalties for late returns on the HMRC website.

Correcting or amending returns

If you make a mistake, you might need to contact HMRC - there’s more information about this on the HMRC website.

You can also change something in your return if you need to. To correct a mistake or change something, call the CIS helpline:

Part 5: Record keeping

Under the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS), you must keep records of:

  • the gross amount of each payment made to each subcontractor (excluding VAT)
  • the amount of any deduction you made from a payment before you gave it to the subcontractor
  • if you made a deduction, the amount of any materials costs - excluding any VAT

You must keep these details for at least 3 years after the end of the tax year they relate to.

HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) could ask to see your CIS records at any time. They’ll check them from time to time like they check employers’ Pay As You Earn (PAYE) records.

If you can’t show your CIS records when asked by HMRC you could be fined up to £3,000.

Part 6: Tell HMRC about changes

Under the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS), changes you must report to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) include if:

  • you change address
  • you temporarily stop using subcontractors (because you’re only using your own employees to carry out work)
  • your business stops trading or if a contractor dies

You must also tell HMRC within 90 days if you’re a multiple contractor and you take on all or part of another contractor’s business.
