Licence Live fish and shellfish import notification - outside EU (England and Wales)

Apply for this licence

Get started on the Animal Health (AH) website

Overview

If you wish to import live fish or shellfish into England and Wales from another country outside the European Union (EU), you must notify Animal Health (AH) at least 24 hours in advance of the import taking place.

Countries outside the EU are known as ‘third countries’ for these purposes.

You must already be authorised by the Fish Health Inspectorate (FHI) to import live fish and shellfish and you can only import species covered by your authorisation.

Once you are authorised you must ensure that any imports from outside the EU are accompanied by a health certificate issued by the competent authority in the country of origin.

These imports must go through a border inspection post where the health certificate is inspected by AH.

How to notify

Notifications should be made on a Common Veterinary Entry Document (CVED) and you must provide details about your business, the consignor/exporter, species and number of fish in the consignment, place of final destination, transit points and journey details.

There is no charge for notifying AH.

Separate forms must be completed for each individual import.

Consignments of fish or shellfish from within the EU only need to be accompanied by a health certificate if they include species susceptible to, or carriers of, an EU controlled disease.

If you are importing live fish or shellfish from within the EU you should notify the FHI instead.

Fines and penalties

If you fail to notify AH in advance of an import of live fish or shellfish, you may face an unlimited fine.
