Quick answer Defer your self-employed National Insurance

Apply to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) if you want to defer (delay) paying your self-employed National Insurance contributions.

If you defer, you won’t overpay your National Insurance contributions because you’ll pay what’s due after the end of the tax year when the actual amount has been worked out.

Eligibility

You must be both self-employed and employed if you want to defer.

For the 2012 to 13 tax year, you can defer Class 2 if you’re likely to earn more than £817 a week as an employee.

You can defer Class 4 if you’re likely to pay more than £3,278.75 for your Class 1, 2 and 4 contributions.

Use the National Insurance calculator to work out your contributions for the 2012-13 tax year.

How to defer

Send form CA72B to HMRC before the deadline.

For the 2012 to 2013 tax year you must apply before 5 April 2013. If you apply after this date HMRC will only let you defer your Class 4 contributions.

Download ‘Form CA72B, Apply to defer your National Insurance contributions - 2012 to 2013 tax year’ (PDF, 95KB)

Download ‘Form CA72B, Guidance notes - 2012 to 2013 tax year ‘ (PDF, 100KB)

Previous tax years

For the 2011 to 2012 tax year - you’ve missed the deadline for Class 2 but you can still apply to defer Class 4. To do this send the 2011 to 2012 version of form CA72B to HMRC.

For any other tax year - you’ve missed the deadline, but you may be able to claim a refund from HMRC.
