Register as a waste carrier, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You usually need to register if you:

  • transport waste
  • arrange for waste to be transported, disposed of or recovered
  • buy or sell waste

Controlled waste includes waste that is any of the following:

  • hazardous
  • commercial
  • industrial
  • household

Part 2: How to apply

England and Wales

You can register online as a waste carrier.

Before you apply

You must apply for either an Upper Tier or Lower Tier registration. The one you need depends on your business and the type of waste.

Upper Tier

You need an Upper Tier registration if you transport, broker or deal in:

  • other people’s controlled waste
  • your own construction waste
  • your own demolition waste

You’ll get a certificate which must be renewed every 3 years. For a £5 fee you can get a credit card sized version.

Lower Tier

You need a Lower Tier registration if you’re a:

  • carrier, broker or dealer who only works with animal by-products, mining and quarry waste or waste from agricultural premises
  • charity or voluntary organisation
  • waste collection, disposal or regulation authority

You’ll get a letter confirming your registration. Your registration doesn’t need to be renewed.

Scotland

There is no upper or lower tier in Scotland.

Part 3: Fees

England, Scotland and Wales

The Upper Tier fees are:

  • registration - £154
  • renewal - £105
  • adding another category to your registration - £42 - eg, you’re registered as a carrier but want to include being a broker

Northern Ireland

The fees are:

  • registration - £132
  • renewal - £67
  • adding another category to your registration - £33 - eg, you’re registered as a carrier but want to include being a broker

There is no fee for Lower Tier registrations.

Part 4: Exemptions

You don’t need to register if you:

  • move waste around your own site
  • export waste by sea or air from the UK
  • import waste into the UK and the waste is ‘landed’ in its final location
  • are a ferry operator transporting waste-carrying vehicles
  • transport waste in a vehicle that has a marine licence or marine licence exemption
  • registered as a waste carrier or professional collector/transporter before 29 March 2011 (8 April 2011 in Northern Ireland) - your registration will become an Upper Tier one when it expires (this doesn’t apply in Scotland)
