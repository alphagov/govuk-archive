Benefits & credits: Reduced Earnings Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you can’t earn as much as you used to because of an accident or disease caused by your work, you could get up to £63.24 per week Reduced Earnings Allowance.

You can only get it for accidents that happened, or diseases that started, before 1 October 1990.

Effect on other benefits

Reduced Earnings Allowance may affect any income-related benefits you or your partner are getting contact your regional Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB) centre for details.

You may also be able to get IIDB.

Part 2: What you'll get

£63.24 per week is the maximum rate.

The amount you get will depend on how much you earn in your regular employment.

Contact your regional Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB) centre for more information.

Reduced Earnings Allowance will be replaced by another benefit called Retirement Allowance if both the following apply:

  • you reach State Pension age
  • you’re not in regular employment

How you’re paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

You could get Reduced Earnings Allowance if you’re suffering from an illness or disability caused by a work-related accident or disease that happened before 1 October 1990.

You must also meet all of the following:

  • your level of disability is assessed to be at least 1%
  • you can’t return to your regular occupation
  • you can’t do other work with the same level of earnings as your regular occupation

Going abroad

If you leave the UK to live permanently outside the European Economic Area (EEA), you won’t be able to get Reduced Earnings Allowance.

The EEA includes:

  • all countries in the EU
  • Norway, Iceland and Liechtenstein

For temporary visits, you can usually claim for the first 3 months of your stay.

This can be longer in special circumstances - check with the International Pensions Centre to see if you qualify.

Your circumstances change

Tell your regional Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit centre straight away - eg if you stop or start work, change your occupation or the level of your earnings changes.

Part 4: How to claim

Claim straight away or you might lose benefit.

Contact your regional Industrial Injuries Disablement Benefit (IIDB) centre for a claim form.

The form comes with notes that will help you fill it in and tell you where to send it.

You can also contact your regional IIDB Centre for a claim form.

If you want help filling in your claim form, ring the Benefit Enquiry Helpline.
