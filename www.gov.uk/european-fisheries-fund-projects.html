European Fisheries Fund projects

How to apply for grants and financial assistance to help fishermen and vessel managers make sustainable improvements to fishing vessels and fisheries practices.

Introduction

The European Fisheries Fund (EFF) provides financial assistance to help fishermen and vessel managers implement the latest reforms to the Common Fisheries Policy. It supports improvements to fishing vessels and fisheries practices to help move towards a more sustainable future.

What is the European Fisheries Fund?

The government has set aside approximately £38 million from the EFF between 2007-13 to help the fisheries industry adapt to changing requirements under the Common Fisheries Policy. This funding is broken down as follows:

  • Axis 1: measures for the adaptation of the European Community fishing fleet
  • Axis 2: measures for investments in aquaculture and processing and marketing of fishery and aquaculture products
  • Axis 3: measures of common interest
  • Axis 4: measures of the sustainable development of fisheries areas
  • Axis 5: technical assistance

You can find application forms for the different EFF Axes on the Marine Management Organisation (MMO) website.

The types of project eligible for grant aid through the EFF include:

  • improvements to non-mandatory onboard safety
  • modernising fishing vessels - for example, to reduce discards, improve gear selectivity, improve on-board hygiene and minimize environmental impact
  • improvements to product quality
  • marketing of fisheries products
  • improvements to processing and port facilities
  • support for the diversification of aquaculture

However, the EFF programme is competitive, and only projects which most effectively meet its aims will be awarded a grant. Grants are not awarded for projects that will increase fishing efforts.

Any work that starts before a decision has been made on an application is automatically not eligible for a grant.

Applying to the EFF for smaller projects

To apply for funding under the EFF of less than £100,000, you should submit the relevant application form to the MMO. The application form you should use should match the Axis of the fund you wish to apply to.

For more information on the different Axes of the EFF, see the following page in this guide on ‘What is the European Fisheries Fund?’

Unless you are applying for less than £20,000, you should also send a simple statement of the business justification for your project, covering the following five questions. Justify your answer as much as possible, with perhaps several paragraphs under each question:

  • Did you consider any alternative way of meeting your basic project needs?
  • If finance became even tighter in general, could you do anything useful by spending only part of your total project cost?
  • Have you investigated full commercial funding for your project?
  • Are you able to make a rough prediction of the effect of the project on your gross profit?
  • Apart from profit, are there other benefits of the investment, including those to your workers, the industry, the wider community or the environment?

You should make sure that you describe all the costs and benefits of your project without double counting.

For advice on applying to the EFF, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk

Applying to the EFF for larger projects

To apply for funding under the EFF for more than £100,000, you should submit the relevant application form to the MMO. The application form you should use should match the Axis of the fund you wish to apply to.

For more information on the Axis, see the following page in this guide on ‘What is the European Fisheries Fund?’

The same criteria apply as for smaller projects - you will need need to provide answers to the following five questions in your application for funding. Justify your answer as much as possible, with perhaps several paragraphs under each question:

  • Did you consider any alternative way of meeting your basic project needs?
  • If finance became even tighter in general, could you do anything useful by spending only part of your total project cost?
  • Have you investigated full commercial funding for your project?
  • Are you able to make a rough prediction of the effect of the project on your gross profit?
  • Apart from profit, are there other benefits of the investment, including those to your workers, the industry, the wider community or the environment?

As well as answering these five questions, you should also cover the following in full:

  • Rationale for the project: a statement of your general requirement for funding, independent of the actual solution you plan to adopt. This should be supported by evidence, meeting SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic and Time-bound) wherever possible
  • Options: you must submit a formal investment appraisal, including a statement of all the possible options that could satisfy at least your minimum requirements. All options should then be subject to at least a cursory cost-benefit assessment
  • Additionality: why your project would not go ahead without EFF support, and evidence that you can afford to pay for the project up front before funding can be claimed
  • Production efficiency: how your project will increase the productivity of the UK fisheries sector
  • Labour market effects: how your project will support employment in the sector
  • Other costs and benefits: for example, environmental benefits, increased health and safety or improvements to the local tourism industry
  • Risk: major risks to your project and how you plan to mitigate these. Large projects may compile a risk register attempting to quantify risks, involving pre- and post- mitigation estimates.
  • Feasibility, quotes and contract arrangements.
  • Project management: how you will plan, resource, manage and appraise the project.
  • Legal compliance: including environmental and health and safety legislation.

If you are submitting an application for funding for a project worth more than £100,000 you will also need to submit a business case with your application. See the page on ‘writing a business case and investment appraisal for European Fisheries Fund funding’

For further advice, call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Writing a business case and investment appraisal for the EFF

If you are applying to the MMO for funding through the EFF for more than £100,000, you will need to submit a brief business case (around five pages is recommended) and a simple investment appraisal, to the MMO along with the relevant application form.

For substantial applications of more than £1 million you should submit a developed business case of at least 10 pages, with separate spreadsheet calculation, and a detailed investment appraisal along with the relevant application form.

For information on the different Axes, see the following page on ‘What is the European Fisheries Fund?’

Your business case should be a summary of all the issues that have been addressed in your application. See the guides on ‘applying to the European Fisheries Fund for smaller projects’ and ‘applying to the European Fisheries Fund for larger projects’.

The case should be able to support your project on a standalone basis. Ranges or estimates should only be used where there is significant uncertainty.

Investment appraisals

An investment appraisal is a cost-benefit assessment of all the possible options.

If it has proved possible to quantify the key economic benefits in your case, you can calculate a present value of these benefits according to the Green Book convention. This can then be set against present value costs (sometimes called a discounted cash flow calculation), and a cost benefit ratio - or true net present value - can be calculated.

You should then add in any non-quantified elements and wider costs and benefits to form an investment appraisal.

If you are applying to the MMO for funding under the EFF, you should consider the following:

  • You should make the calculation over the expected life of the investment (the appraisal period) and include both initial and recurring costs - typically running costs. Running costs should be all the additional costs associated with the investment - eg labour, maintenance and utilities.
  • Depreciation, cost of capital and profit should be excluded from the present value calculation. There are no exceptions to this rule.
  • The proportion of cost that is being grant-supported is irrelevant to the value for money calculation. The assessment is for the project as a whole.
  • Capital and running costs expressed in real terms (constant or current prices) should be discounted at a cumulative 3.5% per annum to reflect the social rate of time preference (for very long-term projects a lower rate is applicable after thirty years). The same procedure is done for quantified benefits over the appraisal period.

For advice, call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Timescales for EFF applications

Once your EFF funding application form is received, the MMO Business Relations Team has 15 days to tell you whether your project is eligible, or to ask you for further information.

Depending on how complicated your project is, they might need to ask for expert advice before assessing your application. This can take up to 4 weeks. Once they have the relevant expert advice they will write to you within 2 weeks and tell you whether your project is eligible, or ask you to provide more information.

If your application is considered complex, it will normally go to the selection panel. Your local area facilitator or the MMO Business Relations Team will be able to advise you whether this is likely to happen.

You can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

How to claim funding from the EFF

If you are eligible for funding under the EFF, you can claim your grant by submitting a claim form by the date specified in your offer letter to the MMO at the following address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
PO Box 1275
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE99 5BN

Download the EFF claim form from the MMO website (PDF, 519K).

Normally this should be a single, full and final claim, although in some cases - such as larger projects - it might be possible for the MMO to make interim payments. You will need to have followed the conditions set out in your offer letter.

The MMO will aim to be able to make a payment to you within 8 weeks of receiving a claim. However, they will need to have all the necessary documents - for example, invoices - before they can make a decision. They will also need to see whether the work has been carried out in line with the original offer letter.

The payment will be made electronically into your bank account.

Completing the claim form

You or someone acting on your behalf should complete the EFF claim form and provide supporting documentation - for more guidance on this, see the guide on ‘supporting documentation for EFF claims’.

When filling in the claim form for the EFF:

  • the name of the claimant should be the person who originally applied for the grant - if different, a covering note of explanation should be attached
  • the project number can be found on the offer letter
  • the title of the project should be a brief description of the project, as set out in the application form
  • the approval date is the date of the offer letter
  • the start and completion dates should relate to the project and not the claim: for interim claims, you should enter the expected date of completion of the project
  • the funding package should set out the total grant aid available - as set out in the offer letter - and details of any grant already received, the amount you are currently claiming and any remaining funding to be claimed
  • claims made and remaining indicates how expenditure on the project is to be funded, including details of payments still to be made: all figures must normally exclude VAT unless you are not registered
  • the Work Carried Out table must show the breakdown of work as set out in the offer letter: the amounts shown in the table should be exactly as quoted on the invoice
  • changes in cost should show the differences between expected and actual costs: you should give brief reasons for any differences greater than 10%
  • progress report should show what the project has achieved compared to the targets set out in the offer letter: you should give brief reasons where targets have not been achieved
  • declaration: you, or a person authorised to sign on your behalf, must sign the form

For advice on completing the EFF claim form, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Supporting documentation for EFF claims

All claims under the EFF must be accompanied by a progress report. If the progress report for your project is not satisfactory, the MMO may not be able to pay your claim.

You must submit the originals of all invoices and other sales dockets in support of each item listed in Section 2(c)1 of the claim form. The invoices must bear either the supplier’s official receipt or similar documents. Photocopies of original documents and electronic versions of original documents will also be considered. The MMO will retain all original documents.

If you cannot, for a legitimate reason, provide originals, you must provide copies which are certified by the supplier.

Valid invoices must contain the following:

  • VAT registration number
  • company registration number
  • business name
  • business address
  • where the business is registered

Some expenses may not involve invoices. In these cases, you must provide appropriate documentation - for example, rail tickets for attendance at trade fairs.

For claims in respect of structural work, you should provide a statement from an independent architect or surveyor on letter-headed paper confirming that the work has been carried out according to the terms of the application.

For claims in respect of structural work on vessels, you must provide evidence of tonnage re-measurement where applicable to the size of the vessel.

For further advice, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Managing a EFF project

If you manage a project that is supported by the EFF, you will have to abide by these requirements:

Inspections

You must allow inspection of your project by officials authorised by the MMO, together with any person accompanying them (which may, from time to time, include an official of the European Commission). You must also provide them with access to relevant documents, and give them reasonable assistance.

All records held by you or the MMO may have to be made available for inspection by the Commission and the National Audit Office.

Progress reports

You will be asked to provide updates on the progress of your project every 6 months. You will also be required to submit a progress report on your business and performance of the project on an annual basis for 3 years from the date of the final payment of the grant.

Retention of records

The MMO will retain original copies of all documents, invoices and other evidence of payment for reference by authorities such as the Commission or European Court of Auditors. You must also keep documentary evidence of expenditure for 6 years from the date of final payment of grant.

Documentary evidence includes:

  • order forms and delivery notes for plant and equipment
  • bills of quantity
  • tenders
  • estimates and quotations
  • architects’ certificates
  • statements and invoices
  • relevant books of account
  • drawings
  • plans and technical specifications

Changing or cancelling your project

If the approved project needs to be modified, you should get the MMO’s agreement before the modification takes place. If you do not, you may lose your entitlement to the grant award.

Modifications in this context include changes in the supplier’s name, status of the beneficiary, change of location and takeover of the company as well as changes in the design and purpose of the project.

If for any reason you decide not to proceed with the project, you should notify the MMO as soon as possible that you are giving up the grant. You should send a written notification to the MMO at this address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
PO Box 1275
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE99 5BN

For further advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.
