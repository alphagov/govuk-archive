Overview

The Reuniting Europe programme supports partner countries with the reforms necessary for EU accession or closer partnership with the EU. The programme helps to build an effective and globally competitive EU and to maintain and improve the EU’s security.

Programme objectives

EU enlargement has been a major success, helping reunite a continent divided by the Cold War and helping us to respond to the opportunities and challenges of globalisation. It is strongly in our interest to maintain the momentum of the enlargement process, including the accession negotiations with Turkey and Croatia, and on bringing peace and stability to the Western Balkans. We also want to ensure that we share our prosperity and stability with our wider neighbourhood.

The programme helps European partner countries to implement the reforms necessary for EU accession and looks at stable institutions guaranteeing democracy, the rule of law, human rights and the respect for and protection of minorities. It aims to:

  • support political reforms and undertake political advocacy
  • increase political transparency and good governance
  • build capacity in public administration
  • strengthen reforms in the justice sector
  • promote human rights

Further details of the rationale behind the selection of these objectives and guidelines on the kinds of projects sought are contained in the Diplomatic Influence and Values (DIV) Fund 2011/12, which includes a full break-down of the revised Programme indicators.

Target countries

Albania Bosnia and Herzegovina Croatia Kosovo Macedonia Montenegro Serbia Turkey

Programme operating principles

The Reuniting Europe Programme is one of many programmes of the wider Diplomatic Influence and Values Fund. The programme will:

  • be 100% ODA-able (meeting the OECD criteria on Official Development Assistance)
  • focus on building capacity and efficiency in organisational structures and processes for policy-making, and in establishing the regulatory framework for effective policy implementation
  • focus on fields where the UK has specific expertise to offer or is better equipped than other potential partners to meet the local need
  • pursue harmonisation with other donors through, where appropriate, co-funding arrangements or projects to pump-prime, pilot or underpin the sustainability of larger-scale projects led by other donors which support the UK’s objectives
  • encourage the development of projects using New Member State expertise
  • operate in areas that avoid duplication and maximise complementarity with the work of other UK government funding sources
  • support UK objectives in each country/region and facilitate FCO action, enhancing the UK’s diplomatic effort

Bidding process

Projects should be at a minimum of £20,000 and those spanning up to 3 financial years will be considered. Please note we do not fund academic courses or research, or English language courses.

The Project Officers in the FCO office in the target country are responsible for developing project ideas and managing the projects. When the London Programme Team issue a call for bids, Project Officers at overseas posts will ask implementers to draw up a concept bid.

Full project bids will then be appraised by a selection of functional and geographic experts. This will take form as a country selection committee. Recommendations from the country selection committee will then be examined and approved by members of the Reuniting Europe board, which consists of representatives of the FCO, other relevant government departments and other donor organisations.

Bidding timetable 2011 to 2012

The initial bidding process for funds in 2011 to 2012 has been completed. If you are interested in running a project in future years, get in touch with our Projects Officers at the relevant overseas post to begin discussions regarding your project proposals.

If you have read the website and bidding guidance and would like some further advice or guidance in advance of the next call for bids or about the programme in general, you can contact us by post.
