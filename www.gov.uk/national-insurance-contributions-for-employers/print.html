National Insurance Contributions (NICs) for your employees, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

For all employees earning more than £7,605 a year you have to deduct Class 1 National Insurance contributions (NICs) from their pay. You also pay a direct contribution yourself. The exact amounts depend on:

  • how much an employee earns
  • their National Insurance category (this depends on their circumstances)

You have to keep National Insurance records for all employees earning more than £5,564.

National Insurance on benefits

If you provide any employee benefits (eg health insurance, company cars) you might have to deduct Class 1A National Insurance contributions from these. This depends on the benefit.

Some small or irregular benefits can be brought together in a single annual payment called a PAYE Settlement Agreement. You’ll have to pay Class 1B National Insurance on these.

Workers not on your payroll

Anyone who does work for you but is not on your payroll (eg self employed, volunteers), is responsible for paying their own National Insurance contributions.

Part 2: Calculating and paying National Insurance contributions

Calculating National Insurance Contributions

You calculate your employees’ National Insurance contributions (NICs) using your Pay As You Earn (PAYE) payroll system.

You’ll need each employee’s National Insurance category for this.

If you do your payroll manually and don’t use software, the HMRC National Insurance calculator will help you calculate the amounts you need to enter into your payroll.

HMRC also has information on payroll and NICs calculations.

Making payments

Most employers pay Class 1 NICs by direct debit, which you set up when you register as an employer with HMRC.

If you don’t want to pay by direct debit there are other ways of paying Class 1.

HMRC also has information on paying Class 1A. and on paying Class 1B.

Part 3: Deadlines for NICs payments

National Insurance Contributions (NICs) Class 1 are part of your monthly Pay As You Earn (PAYE) payments.

Class 1A contributions on expenses and benefits are calculated separately at the end of the year. These have to be in HM Revenues & Customs’ (HMRC) account by 22 July if you pay electronically or by 19 July if you send a cheque.

If you pay Class 1B on employee benefits your payments have to be in HMRC’s account by 22 October (19 October if you pay by cheque).

HMRC has detailed information on National Insurance payment deadlines.

Part 4: National Insurance category letters

You need to know an employee’s National Insurance category to work out how much to pay for them. If you use Pay As You Earn (PAYE) payroll software it’ll ask you for the category letter to calculate your contributions.

An employee’s NI category letter depends on whether they have a separate (contracted-out) pension scheme and opted out of paying into the State Pension.

Most employees are category A or D.

When an employee reaches State Pension age

You have to change their National Insurance category to ‘C’ in your PAYE system. You must continue to pay contributions for them.

They need to show you one of the following:

  • proof of date of birth
  • certificate of exemption form CA4140

Part 5: National Insurance Contribution rates

There are 2 types of Class 1 National Insurance Contributions (NICs):

  • primary contributions - what you deduct from your employee’s pay
  • secondary contributions - what you pay towards an employee’s contributions

Both types of contributions depend on:

  • the employee’s National Insurance category
  • the employee’s earnings
