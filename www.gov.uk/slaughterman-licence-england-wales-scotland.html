Licence Slaughterman licence (England, Wales and Scotland)

Apply for this licence

Get started on the Food Standards Agency website

Overview

If you operate as a slaughterman in England, Scotland or Wales, you require a licence from the Food Standards Agency.

You are considered to be operating as a slaughterman if you:

  • restrain any animal for the purpose of stunning, slaughtering or killing that animal
  • stun, slaughter or kill any animal
  • pith any stunned animal
  • assess the effective stunning or killing of any animal
  • shackle or hoist any stunned animal
  • bleed any animal which is not dead

However, you don’t need a licence for the following activities:

  • killing an animal in an emergency, eg if it’s in distress and where it needs to be killed immediately for welfare reasons
  • slaughtering your own animals for your private consumption on premises other than a slaughterhouse or knacker’s yard
  • slaughtering or killing animals for a non-commercial purpose
  • killing animals by certain approved methods as a means of disease control
  • shooting animals in the field eg with a shotgun or rifle
  • dislocating the necks of, or decapitating, poultry on the agricultural premises where they were raised
  • killing farmed foxes or mink by certain approved methods
  • killing surplus chicks or embryos in hatchery waste by certain approved methods
  • shackling birds before stunning or killing
  • operating automatic equipment that stuns, slaughters or kills animals, without requiring you to carry out any of the activities above for which a licence is required
  • slaughtering or killing animals under veterinary supervision

How to apply

To apply for a licence, you must be at least 18 years old and you require a certificate of competence from an authorised veterinary surgeon.

If you work at a licensed slaughterhouse, the assessment for the certificate of competence will usually be carried out by the authorised veterinary surgeon at that slaughterhouse. If you work in any other premises you should apply to the local Animal Health Divisional Office and they will arrange an assessment. A fee may be payable. You can then apply for either a full licence.

If you are in training to become a slaughterman you must apply for a provisional licence. This will be valid for a period of up to three months and will authorise you to carry out the relevant activities only in the presence, and under the direction of, a veterinary surgeon or the holder of a full licence covering the same species, operations and equipment.

You must disclose in your application form if you have been refused a licence to slaughter animals, had such a licence revoked or been convicted under any legislation concerning the welfare of animals.

If applying for a full licence, you must also enclose:

  • your certificate of competence
  • two passport sized photographs (with your name written on the back of each)

No fee is payable for a provisional licence but there a registration charge of £26 for a full licence.

Application forms can be downloaded from the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra). The same form is used to apply for a certificate of competence, provisional licence or full licence.

Conditions

If granted, your licence will state the operations you are licensed to carry out, the relevant species of animal you may carry out those operations on and the equipment or instruments to be used. You must:

  • ensure the welfare of the animals kept on your premises
  • ensure all employees have knowledge of the welfare legislation
  • follow all requirements applicable to slaughterhouses and knackers’ yards

Additional requirements apply to the slaughter and killing of animals other than at a slaughterhouse or knacker’s yard.
