Quick answer Passport services for disabled customers

Not what you're looking for? ↓

If you’re disabled, there are free services and facilities to help you with your passport application.

Wheelchair access

If you need to visit a Passport Customer Service Centre, they all have wheelchair access. Some have toilets for disabled customers as well - in those that don’t, staff will be able to take you to an appropriate toilet in the building.

Problems signing the application form

If your disability means you can’t sign the form (eg you can’t write, have learning or writing difficulties), you can get someone to do this on your behalf (eg a relative, social services officer, doctor etc).

They should:

  • provide an explanation of why you can’t sign in section 8 of the form and in a signed, dated covering letter
  • sign the declaration in section 9 of the form

The wording “The holder is not required to sign” will be shown in the passport where the signature would normally be.

If you need more help or advice, call the Passport Adviceline.

Passport Adviceline

Telephone: 0300 222 0000
Textphone: 0300 222 0222
Text Relay: 18001 0300 222 0000
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, Sunday and public holidays, 9am to 5:30pm

If you’re applying for a child aged 12 to 15 who can’t sign section 6 of the form:

  • leave this section blank
  • explain in section 8 that the child is unable to sign

The Identity and Passport Service (IPS) will endorse the passport to say the holder’s signature isn’t needed.

Problems with passport photos

If your disability means you can’t meet the passport photo requirements, contact the Passport Adviceline.

If you’re deaf or hard of hearing

Passport Customer Service Centres have:

  • staff trained in sign language - they can help you fill in your application form
  • induction loops and quiet rooms with no background noise

If you’d like someone to help with your application, book an appointment to visit a Customer Service Centre. You can do this by calling the Passport Adviceline.

If you’re blind or partially sighted

The guidance booklet to help with your passport application is available in different formats. Call the Passport Adviceline if you’d like it in:

  • Braille
  • audio tape
  • large-print format

Applying by phone

Call 0800 138 8808 to apply by phone. Someone will fill the form in for you using the information you provide. They’ll then post it to you to check, sign and return with the relevant documents, payment and photos.

Braille passport stickers

The IPS can attach a Braille identifying sticker to your new passport. You ask for this on your application form.

To get a sticker for your current passport, contact your nearest Passport Customer Service Centre.
