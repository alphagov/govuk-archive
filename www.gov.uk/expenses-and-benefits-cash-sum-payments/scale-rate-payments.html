Part 4: Scale rate payments

If you provide your employees with a set amount of cash for some common business expenses like travel and meals, these are known as ‘scale rate payments’.

As long as your employee has actually spent the scale rate payment on business expenses, you won’t need to check every single receipt – it’s fine to just check a sample.

You can set up a scale rate payment by either:

  • agreeing a scale rate with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) by providing evidence of typical expenses (eg receipts)
  • using HMRC’s benchmark scale rates for subsistence costs

HMRC also provides benchmark rates for employees travelling outside of the UK.

What to report and pay

You don’t need to report or pay anything if you provide scale rate payments for employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year.

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year:

  • you’ll need to report scale rate payments on form P11D, section N unless you have a dispensation covering them
  • you don’t need to deduct or pay any tax or National Insurance

You can apply for a dispensation to cover scale rate payments. This means you won’t have to include them in your end-of-year reports.
