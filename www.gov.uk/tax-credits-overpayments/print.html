Tax credits overpayments, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overpayments

You have to repay any tax credits you’re not entitled to. Overpayments happen if:

  • you don’t report a change in your circumstance to the Tax Credit Office
  • you make a mistake on your claim form

The Tax Credit Office will tell you what you owe and how to repay it. Overpayments can be repaid:

  • by reducing your tax credits
  • through your tax code - if you owe less than £3,000
  • as a lump sum

If you owe less than £3,000

HM Revenue & Customs can change your PAYE tax code to collect your overpayment. You’ll pay back what you owe by paying more tax in the next tax year (6 April).

This only happens if:

  • your overpayment is less than £3,000
  • you work for an employer, or get a UK-based pension
  • you haven’t contacted the Tax Credit Office about repaying the overpayment

Making extra payments

If you want to make extra payments to clear the debt more quickly - call the Tax Credit Payment Helpline.

Tax Credit Payment Helpline
0845 302 1429

Part 2: Dispute form TC864

If you’re asked to repay an overpayment of tax credits you can dispute the overpayment.

You don’t have to repay any money until you get a decision about the dispute.

How to dispute the overpayment

Send a dispute form to the Tax Credit Office.

Download ‘Form TC846, Dispute a tax credit overpayment decision’ (PDF, 26KB)

Overpayment Disputes Team
Tax Credit Office
Preston
PR1 4AT

What happens next

You’ll get a letter telling you:

  • if you have to repay the tax credits
  • how much you have to repay
  • the reasons for the decision

If you don’t agree with the decision

You can:

  • send them new information - they’ll stop recovering the overpayment
  • ask them to review the information you sent - they won’t stop recovering the overpayment

If you haven’t got any new information and you’re still unhappy, contact an organisation like Citizens Advice.

Part 3: Problems repaying

If you can’t afford the repayments, call the Tax Credit Helpline. They may change the way you pay to make it more affordable. For example, by spreading the payments over a longer period.
