Benefits & credits: Travel grants for medical and dental students (England)

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you’re an English medical or dental student attending a clinical placement in the UK or studying abroad, you may be able to get a grant towards covering some of your travel expenses.

There are rules about eligibility and how much you’ll get.

If you’re not from England, go to:

  • Student Awards Agency for Scotland
  • Student Finance Wales
  • Student Finance Northern Ireland

You don’t have to pay a travel grant back.

Part 2: What you'll get

The amount you get depends on your household income - this is the total income of your parents or guardians, but wouldn’t include any other family members you live with.

You’ll have to pay the ﬁrst £303 of travel costs.

You should keep costs to a minimum, eg travelling standard class and using the train unless it’s cheaper to fly.

If you’re studying abroad

You can claim for:

  • up to 3 return journeys between your home and the overseas institution during a full academic year abroad
  • medical insurance, expenses and travel visas (only if essential)

If you’re a single parent, you may be able to claim for your children’s travel costs.

If you’re doing a clinical placement in the UK

You can claim for travel costs between your home and the hospital or facility where you’re doing your placement.

Part 3: Eligibility

Your home address must be in England.

If you’re studying abroad

You must attend an overseas medical or dental institution for at least 50% of each academic term. This period of study can be compulsory or optional.

If you’re doing a clinical placement in the UK

The placement must be an essential part of your medical or dental course. You won’t get a travel grant if you’re eligible for means-tested bursaries or awards from the Department of Health.

You can apply if you get student ﬁnance that depends on your household income, such as Maintenance Loan or Maintenance Grant.

Part 4: How to claim

To get the forms you need, log in to your Student Finance England (SFE) account and update your details (the forms will then be sent to you automatically).

If you don’t have a student finance account, register to set one up.

Keep all receipts for any expenses you want to apply for; you’ll need to send photocopies when you make your claim.

You can apply for a travel grant every time you travel. The money will be paid direct into your bank account.

If you’re studying abroad

You need:

  • ‘Course abroad’ form, which your university or college must complete
  • ‘Travel abroad expenses’ form

If you’re doing a clinical placement in the UK

You need:

  • ‘Clinical study travel expenses’ form

Order forms by phone
