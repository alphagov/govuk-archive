Collective (group) passports, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

A collective (or group) passport is a way for organised groups of young people to make trips to certain European countries.

The passport isn’t intended for families - it’s for groups like:

  • schools and 6th form colleges
  • scouts
  • guides
  • other recognised youth organisations

Collective passports are for between 5 and 50 children. If there are more than 50 in the group you can split it up and apply for 2 or more passports.

A collective passport costs £39 and applications take about 6 weeks. The rules are:

  • everyone on the passport must be a British national and under 18 by the end of the trip
  • there must be a group leader over 21 with a valid 10-year British passport

You should also have a deputy group leader. The passport will become invalid if the group leader can’t travel, but if a deputy is named on the application, they could take over.

Deputy leaders must be over 21 years old and hold a valid 10-year British passport.

A ‘British national’ is someone who is a:

  • British citizen
  • British overseas territories citizen
  • British overseas citizen
  • British subject
  • British national (overseas)
  • British protected person

There’s no guarantee that collective passports will be accepted or rejected in any country - wherever possible, young people should travel on their own passports.

Part 2: Countries you can visit

The countries you can travel to or through on a collective passport are:

  • Austria
  • Belgium
  • Denmark
  • France
  • Germany
  • Italy
  • Luxembourg
  • Malta
  • Netherlands
  • Norway
  • Poland
  • Romania
  • Slovenia
  • Spain
  • Sweden
  • Switzerland

Visas

The rules about visas and collective passports vary by country - contact the country’s embassy or consulate in the UK for more information.

Part 3: How to apply

Follow these steps to apply for a collective passport:

  1. Download the application form (DOC, 915KB)

  2. Save it to your computer and fill it in (handwritten applications are not accepted).

  3. Email your completed form to durhamcollectives@ips.gsi.gov.uk.

  4. Print a paper copy, signed by the group leader and deputy leader.

  5. Send the paper form with the fee and supporting documents to:

Collective Passport Applications
Millburngate House
PO Box 302
Durham DH1 9ZL

Enclose a cheque for £39, made payable to ‘Identity and Passport Service’, or pay by credit or debit card using the card payment form.

Download ‘Card payment form for collective passports’ (PDF, 49KB)

When you fill in the form, use CAPITAL LETTERS.

Verifying your application

The Identity and Passport Service may contact your organisation to verify the application.

If you’re applying during school holidays, give a contact number on the form that will work during the holidays to prevent your application being delayed.

Part 4: Supporting documents

You’ll need to provide supporting documents with your application for a collective passport.

Nationality questionnaire and parental consent forms

You need to complete these so that the Identity and Passport Service can confirm the nationality of each child.

Include a fully completed form for each child - details of who can give consent are on the forms.

Download ‘Form for children born in the UK’ (PDF, 36KB)

Download ‘Form for children born outside the UK’ (PDF, 31KB)

Photo cards

You need a collective passport photo card for each child who will travel on the collective passport. Order them by emailing durhamcollectives@ips.gsi.gov.uk or calling the Passport Adviceline on 0300 222 0000.

Each card must be filled in with the child’s details exactly as they appear on the application form. You also need to attach a recent passport-sized photo to each card.

The photos:

  • should be of a similar quality to standard passport photos
  • should not have been previously used on another document
  • must not be laminated or photocopies

You can use school photos as long as they don’t have ‘PROOF’ written across them. You can also use digital photos.

The group leader needs to complete their own details and certify the photo cards by signing them before submitting the form.

Each child must sign their card before travel.

Supporting letter

For each application (not each child) there needs to be a supporting letter on headed paper confirming consent to the trip.

The letter must have an original signature (digital or photocopied signatures are not accepted). It can’t be from the group leader or deputy.

The table below shows who should sign and supply the supporting letters.

Type of organisation Who should sign and supply the letter
School The head teacher, a member of the board of governors or the education authority from each school making up the party
Scout or guide group The assistant county/area commissioner or someone from Association Headquarters
Football or rugby club Someone from the local football association or the league chairperson or secretary
Swimming or judo clubs Someone from their national headquarters
Youth orchestras, choirs etc Someone from their local education authority, or vicar for church groups
Army or Airforce cadets Their commanding officer or someone from their services authority
Clubs or youth clubs Someone from the local authority where they’re registered, or their national headquarters
Registered charities The director or a person in a similar position in the organisation

If the group is going abroad to perform for any purpose (including sports competitions) the letter must say:

  • all members of the group are amateurs
  • they won’t get any payment

Download ‘Example supporting letter’ (PDF, 56KB)

If there are children from more than one organisation travelling, you need to send a supporting letter from each organisation.

Part 5: Getting the passport changed

Check your passport when it arrives for mistakes.

Travel may be refused if children are missing from or haven’t been added to the passport. You should send the passport back to the Identity and Passport Service if you need to make changes.

If you need to amend the passport

Return the passport with a letter explaining the circumstances.

If your trip is postponed but you arrange alternative travel within the same season you can usually get your passport amended free of charge.

If several changes need to be made (like adding or deleting lots of names), or if you lose your passport, you’ll have to pay for a new one.

Send the passport back to:

Collective Passport Applications
Millburngate House
PO Box 302
Durham DH1 9ZL
