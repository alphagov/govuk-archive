Part 2: Deduction from earnings order (DEO)

This is a way of collecting child maintenance directly from a paying parent’s earnings or pension.

The ‘paying parent’ is the parent the child doesn’t live with.

The Child Support Agency (CSA), or an authorised debt collection agency, will send you a deduction from earnings order (DEO) if one of your employees is a paying parent who:

  • chooses to pay child maintenance direct from their earnings
  • doesn’t pay the correct amount of child maintenance on time
  • doesn’t pay at all

You must deduct the amount of child maintenance stated on the DEO from your employee’s net earnings or their pension and pay it to the CSA. This will include any arrears that may be due.

If you get more than one DEO for an employee

You could get more than one DEO if your employee is paying maintenance for children by different ‘receiving parents’. You need to make a payment for each DEO.

The ‘receiving parent’ is the parent or carer the child lives with.

Other court orders against your employee

You could get a DEO from the CSA and an attachment of earnings order (AEO) from a court.

AEOs work in a similar way to DEOs, but there are rules about which money you should take first.

You can download a leaflet that tells you more about AEOs.
