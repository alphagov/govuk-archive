Benefits & credits: Jobseeker's Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get up to £56.25, £71 or £111.45 a week Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) to help you while you’re looking for work.

How much you get depends on your circumstances and the type of JSA you qualify for.

To qualify you usually have to be aged 18 or over (but below State Pension age), able and available for work.

You must apply for JSA with Jobcentre Plus and go to an interview to agree what steps you’ll take to find work.

To keep getting JSA you’ll normally have to attend a Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks to show how your job search is going.

Part 2: What you'll get

There are 2 types of Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA). Jobcentre Plus will work out which type you’ll get and how much you’ll be paid.

Contribution-based JSA

Age Weekly amount
16 to 24 Up to £56.25
25 or over Up to £71

You get this if you’ve paid enough National Insurance contributions (NICs) as an employee.

Income-based JSA

Status Weekly amount
Single (under 25) Up to £56.25
Single (25 or over) Up to £71
Couples (both aged 18 or over) Up to £111.45
Lone parent (18 or over) Up to £71
Lone parent (under 18) Up to £56.25

You get this if you’ve not paid enough National Insurance as an employee and you’re on a low income.

Use the benefits adviser to work out how much you can get.

How you’re paid

Usually payments are made every 2 weeks, and are calculated from 3 days after the date you made your claim.

All benefits, pensions and allowances are normally paid into an account, eg a bank account.

To keep getting JSA you’ll normally have to attend a Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks to show how your job search is going.

From April 2013 a limit will be put on the total amount of benefit that most people aged 16 to 64 can get. This is called a benefit cap.

Part 3: Eligibility

To qualify for Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) you must:

  • work on average less than 16 hours a week
  • normally be aged 18 or over but below State Pension age - there are some exceptions for 16 to 17 year olds
  • not be in full-time education
  • live in England, Scotland or Wales

If you live in Northern Ireland, contact your local Social Security or Jobs and Benefits Office.

Extra rules for contribution-based JSA

You can only claim contribution-based JSA for 6 months - after this, you may still be able to get income-based JSA.

The amount you can get might go down if you have:

  • earnings from part-time work
  • an occupational or personal pension

Extra rules for income-based JSA

Usually, to qualify for income-based JSA:

  • your partner (if you have one) must work less than 24 hours a week on average
  • you and your partner must have less than £16,000 in savings

Your household income can affect how much you get too. This includes money from savings (if you have £6,000 or more), pensions and earnings.

Use the benefits adviser to check your eligibility.

16 to 17 years olds

JSA isn’t usually paid to 16 or 17 year olds or people in full-time education, except in certain circumstances. Contact your local Jobcentre Plus for advice.

Your circumstances change

Tell Jobcentre Plus if your circumstances change as this can affect how much money you get. For example, you start working or your income changes.

Volunteering won’t normally affect your JSA but you must report that you’re doing it.

To keep getting JSA you’ll normally have to attend a Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks to show how your job search is going.

Part 4: How to claim

You can claim Jobseeker’s Allowance:

  • online
  • by phone - if you can’t apply online

Jobcentre Plus

Textphone: 0800 023 4888
Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm

If you live in Northern Ireland, contact your local Social Security or Jobs and Benefits Office.

What you need to know

You must attend an interview at your local Jobcentre Plus to complete your claim. If you apply online, you’ll get a text or phone call within 2 working days to arrange this.

Joint claims

You must make a joint claim with your partner if:

  • either or both of you both want income-based JSA
  • at least one of you is aged 18 or over
  • neither of you are responsible for a child

If you’re not sure whether you can get JSA, contact Jobcentre Plus.

Appeal a decision

You can ask for any decision about your JSA to be reviewed at any time, and you can appeal the decision about your JSA if you’re unhappy with it.

Part 5: Further information

Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) interview

To complete your claim you have to go to an interview at your local Jobcentre Plus.

At the interview you have to agree things like:

  • what steps you can to take to find work - eg improving your skills
  • how you can improve your chances of getting a job - eg get help on writing a CV, preparing for interviews, looking for work

What you agree to is recorded in a ‘jobseeker’s agreement’. You’ll normally have a review meeting every 2 weeks to check if you’re doing the things in your agreement.

If you don’t attend your interview or your review meetings this will delay your JSA payment and may mean you won’t get any JSA at all.

How you could lose your benefit

You could lose your Jobseeker’s Allowance for up to 13 weeks if you:

  • don’t attend an adviser interview
  • don’t take compulsory steps that will help you find work, eg by attending a training course when you’re told to
  • don’t apply for or don’t accept a place on an employment programme or  training scheme that you’re told about
  • don’t attend or take part in an employment programme or  training scheme, or leave voluntarily
  • lose  your place on an employment programme or  training scheme because of misconduct

You could lose your Jobseeker’s Allowance for up to 3  years if you:

  • don’t apply for a job that your adviser tells you about
  • don’t accept a suitable job offer
  • leave a job voluntarily, or lose your job because of misconduct
  • don’t take part in a compulsory work activity programme

This is known as a ‘sanction’.

Your benefit will stop and you’ll no longer be eligible for Jobseeker’s  Allowance if you don’t look or you’re not available for work.

If your benefit is stopped

You must still go to your local Jobcentre Plus or  attend any employment programme you’re on - otherwise you may lose your benefit for longer or altogether.

You may be able to claim hardship payments if you’re suffering financial hardship - speak to your adviser at your Jobcentre for more information.
