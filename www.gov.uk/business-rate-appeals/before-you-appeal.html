Part 2: Before you appeal

The first step in appealing your business rates is to ask the Valuation Office Agency (VOA) to check the details. This can change how much you pay for your business rates.

If you think your rateable value is wrong:

  • check the details used to value your property
  • make a valuation enquiry

The VOA will change your rateable value if it was wrong. They may need to visit your property.

You can start an appeal with the Valuation Tribunal at any time.

Material changes

A material change is a physical change to part or all of your property. This can change its rateable value.

Material changes can include:

  • changes to how the property is used
  • new developments in the area
  • changes to access routes or footpaths to the area

If the VOA becomes aware of a material change, they’ll look at how it affects the rental value and whether this means the rateable value needs to be adjusted.

Extreme flooding

You should tell the VOA immediately if extreme flooding is affecting the use of your property. There might be a reduction in your business rates.
