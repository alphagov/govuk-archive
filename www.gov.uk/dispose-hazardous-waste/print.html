Dispose of hazardous waste, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You are responsible for making sure any hazardous waste (also known as ‘special waste’ in Scotland) produced by your business doesn’t cause any harm or damage. This responsibility is known as your ‘duty of care’.

Waste is generally considered hazardous if it (or the material or substances it contains) are harmful to humans or the environment. Examples of hazardous waste include:

  • asbestos
  • chemicals - eg, brake fluid or print toner
  • batteries
  • solvents
  • pesticides
  • oils (except edible ones) - eg, car oil
  • equipment containing ozone depleting substances - eg, fridges
  • hazardous waste containers

Your duty of care lasts from when you produce the hazardous waste until it’s given to an authorised business to deal with.

Checking if your waste is hazardous

England and Wales

The Environment Agency can help you check if your waste is hazardous.

Scotland

The Scottish Environment Protection Agency can help you check if your waste is hazardous.

Part 2: Duty of care

Your responsibilities

You must:

  • consider alternatives to disposal - eg, recycling
  • store your waste safely and securely
  • fill in a consignment note for each load of hazardous waste you transport
  • fill in a consignee return if you receive a delivery of hazardous waste
  • check that any business you use to deal with your hazardous waste is licensed, and keep proof of this - eg, take a copy of their licence
  • usually have a licence to transport, store, treat, recover or dispose of hazardous waste yourself

Find a licensed waste business

  • a business that can recycle or dispose of your waste
  • a collection company to take away your waste

Scotland

There are public registers you can use to find:

  • a business that can recycle or dispose of your waste
  • a collection company to take away your waste

Licences and registration

Usually you have to be licensed to produce, transport, store, treat or dispose of hazardous waste. To get a licence:

  • for a business premises that produces hazardous waste - register the premises (except in Scotland, where they don’t need to be registered)
  • to transport hazardous waste - apply for a waste carrier licence
  • to produce, store, treat or dispose of hazardous waste - get an environmental permit (except in Scotland, where you’ll need either a pollution prevention and control permit and/or a waste management licence

Keep records

You must keep a record (known as a ‘register’) that includes:

  • what hazardous waste you have
  • where it’s stored on your site
  • how it was moved off your site - you can get this from the consignment note
  • how it was ‘treated’ - eg, before it was disposed of
  • if your business disposes of hazardous waste, how you disposed of it - you can get this from your consignee return

Part 3: Storage

You must store hazardous waste safely and securely. To do this:

  • store it in sealed waterproof containers
  • label it correctly and clearly
  • display written instructions for storing and disposing of it
  • keep it separate from any non-hazardous waste
  • keep different types of hazardous waste separate
  • keep liquid hazardous waste in a dedicated area, with a bund or barrier to contain leaks
  • keep records about it and where it’s located
  • regularly check for leaks, deteriorating containers or other potential risks
  • carry out a risk assessment - eg, to identify what the risks are and how to control them

Part 4: Consignment notes

You usually need to fill out a consignment note if you move hazardous waste off your premises. For example, if you move:

  • asbestos from a domestic premises
  • hazardous waste that has been fly-tipped
  • hazardous waste from a premises that is exempt from registering as a hazardous waste producer

Consignment notes must remain with the waste until it reaches its final destination and you must keep copies for 3 years.

Part 5: Consignee returns

Consignees must fill out a consignee return every quarter and send it to the Environment Agency. You’re a consignee if you:

  • receive or reject a delivery of hazardous waste - eg, for disposal, recycling or recovery
  • dispose of hazardous waste

You must keep copies of your consignee returns for 3 years.

Deadlines

Reporting period Deadline
January to March 30 April
April to June 31 July
July to September 31 October
October to December 31 January

Fees

Fees are per consignment of waste and depend on whether the consignment came from a single location or not. The fees are:

  • from a single location - £10 (electronic returns) or £19 (paper returns)
  • from multiple locations - £5 (electronic returns) or £10 (paper returns)
