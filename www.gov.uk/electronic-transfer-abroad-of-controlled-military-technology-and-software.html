Electronic transfer abroad of controlled military technology and software

How to comply with export controls if you transfer military technology overseas electronically

Introduction

UK export controls on technology make specific provisions for electronic transfers of military technology. Electronic transfers can take various different forms including emails, computer file transfers and fax messages.

An example of a controlled electronic transfer of military information would be an email to an overseas customer, with a user manual for an item of military equipment attached to it.

The export controls and licensing requirements that apply to physical technology transfers also apply to electronic transfers. However, there are some specific rules for keeping records of controlled electronic transfers as they can be harder to keep track of.

Guidance on electronic transfers

UK export control legislation makes special provision for electronic transfers of controlled military technology and software. For export control purposes controlled ‘technology’ means specific information that someone would need for developing, producing or using goods whose export is legally controlled. You can read general information on controlled technology transfers in the guide on the export of technology.

Controlled military technology refers to information that relates to items on the UK Military List for export control. You can get details of goods covered by export controls in the guide: UK Strategic Export Control Lists.

An electronic transfer means sending the controlled technology or software by any electronic means from the UK to another country. This could be done using methods such as:

  • email or other computer messaging
  • computer file transfer
  • fax
  • telephone call
  • video, including video conferencing
  • text messaging

If you transfer controlled technology abroad electronically then you must obtain an appropriate licence. You will have various responsibilities under the conditions of your licence including end user documentation and proper record keeping.

Getting detailed information and guidance

The Export Control Organisation (ECO) is part of the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) and is responsible for licensing exports of controlled military goods and technology. They have produced a detailed guide to export controls for exporters who transfer military technology electronically. The guide explains what is controlled, the types of licence available, how to apply for a licence and how to comply with licensing conditions.

Download details on the transfer abroad of controlled military technology and software by electronic means on the BIS website (PDF, 217K).

Licensing and compliance of controlled electronic transfers

If you electronically transfer controlled military technology abroad, then you must obtain a licence. There are four types of export licence that can cover electronic transfers of military technology. The type of licence you need depends on:

  • the nature of the military technology you’re transferring
  • the destination of your electronic transfers

Open General Export Licence

The first step in licensing your electronic technology transfers is to check whether you could use an Open General Export Licence (OGEL). OGELs are general licences that allow any exporter to export specified controlled items to eligible destinations. They remove the need to apply for an individual licence.

If you can meet all the licensing conditions you may be able to register with the ECO to use one of several OGELs available. In particular, there are three OGELs available that apply specifically to electronic military technology transfers:

  • Technology for Military Goods
  • Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK forces deployed in embargoed destinations
  • Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK forces deployed in non-embargoed destinations

You can get more information in the guide on military goods OGELs.

If you transfer controlled military technology using an OGEL you must adhere to the specified conditions and keep certain records. It is your responsibility to check the licence requirements and keep up to date with any changes. For more information on the record-keeping and compliance responsibilities of exporters, see compliance and enforcement of export controls.

Individual licences

If you are not entitled to use an OGEL for electronic transfer of military technology you might be able to apply for one of the other types of licences issued by the ECO. These include:

  • Standard Individual Export Licences (SIELs) - see SIELs
  • Open Individual Export Licences (OIELs) - see OIELs

Applying for a licence

You can use the SPIRE online export licensing system to register to use an OGEL or to apply for an individual licence. See SPIRE - background information.
