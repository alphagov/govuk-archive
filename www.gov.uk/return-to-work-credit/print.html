Benefits & credits: Return to Work Credit

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you’ve been claiming benefit you could get Return to Work Credit if:

  • you’ve been unable to work for at least 13 weeks because of an illness or disability
  • you’re going back to work, starting a new job or self-employment

You have to qualify for Return to Work Credit. Any money you get won’t affect your other benefits and is tax-free.

To get Return to Work Credit you must:

  • tell Jobcentre Plus you’ve started work within 5 weeks of doing so
  • have started claiming benefit before 2 July 2013
  • start work or self-employment before 1 October 2013.

Part 2: What you'll get

You’ll get £40 a week.

You can get Return to Work Credit for up to a year if you carry on working an average of 16 hours or more a week and your gross earnings are less than £288.46 a week (or £1,250 a month)

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

It’s tax-free and doesn’t count as income when working out if you can get:

  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Benefit
  • tax credits

Part 3: Eligibility

To qualify for Return to Work Credit you must:

  • start work within the 5-week period immediately after being entitled to a qualifying benefit
  • have started claiming the benefit before 2 July 2013
  • be starting work that pays at least the National Minimum Wage but not more than £288.46 gross a week (£1,250 gross a month)
  • be getting paid for at least 16 hours work on average per week (this includes being self-employed)
  • expect the work to last at least 5 weeks
  • start work before 1 October 2013

Benefits you must be getting

You must also have been getting at least 1 of these qualifying benefits for at least 13 weeks before starting work:

  • Incapacity Benefit
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Income Support (because you have been ill or have a disability)
  • Severe Disablement Allowance
  • Statutory Sick Pay from your employer

You might not qualify if you claim either of the following benefits in the 5 weeks before you start work:

  • Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Income Support as a lone parent

There are other circumstances that could affect your eligibility. You can check with your local Jobcentre Plus.

There will be no new Return to Work Credit payments from 1 October 2013.

Part 4: How to claim

You must tell your local Jobcentre Plus that you’ve started work within 5 weeks of doing so to get Return to Work Credit.

Contact your local Jobcentre Plus and tell them that you’re going back to work or starting a new job.

If you qualify they should give you a claim form for Return to Work Credit. Ask for one if they don’t.

Or you can download a copy and take it to your local Jobcentre Plus.
