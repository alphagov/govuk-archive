Overview

Check with your council to see if you need permission to distribute free printed material in England and Wales.

Free printed material includes:

  • leaflets or cards advertising your business
  • free newspapers
  • takeaway menus and other promotional material

Some councils also include free samples with anything printed on them.

Restrictions

You must follow all conditions of your licence, including:

  • what is to be distributed
  • when it may be distributed
  • where it may be distributed

Exceptions

You don’t need permission from the council if the printed material is being distributed:

  • for a charity
  • for a political or religious group
  • inside a building, bus or taxi
  • in letterboxes

How to apply

Check with the council if you need permission to distribute printed material.

If you need a licence you can apply in person, by post or on their website. You may also need to pay a fee, which will vary depending on:

  • the council
  • the number of distributors you want to use
  • whether you’re applying for permission for a single event or for an annual permit

Displaying your licence

Some councils issue a badge for each authorised person to wear while distributing printed material.

A police officer may ask to see this or other written evidence of your authorisation at any time.

Fines and penalties

If you distribute free printed material without a valid licence you can be fined up to £2,500 or a fixed penalty notice. The printed material may also be taken away.

If you don’t meet one or more of the conditions, your licence will be cancelled.
