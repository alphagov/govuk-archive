Part 2: Renewing or ending a fixed-term contract

Ending a fixed-term contact

Your fixed-term contract will normally end automatically when it reaches the agreed end date. Your employer doesn’t have to give you notice.

If your contract is not renewed

If your fixed-term contact isn’t renewed, it’s considered to be a dismissal. In this situation your employer needs to show that there’s a ‘fair’ reason for not renewing your contract, eg they’re planning to stop doing the work you were employed to do.

You’ve the right:

  • not to be unfairly dismissed after 2 years’ service (for employees who were in employment before 6 April 2012, it’s 1 year’s service)
  • to a written statement of reasons for not renewing the contract - after 1 year’s service

You may be entitled to statutory redundancy payments after 2 years’ service if your work ends unexpectedly.

If your employer wants to end your contract earlier

If your employer wants to end your fixed-term contract early, you should check the terms of your contract. If:

  • it says nothing about being ended early, your employer may be in breach of contract
  • it says it can be ended early, and your employer has given proper notice, there’s little you can do

The minimum notice period you’re entitled to is:

  • after 1 month’s continuous service, but less than 2 years - 1 week
  • after 2 years’ continuous employment - 1 week for each year you’ve worked

These are the minimum periods - your contract may give you the right to a longer notice period.

If your employer ends your contract without giving you the proper notice, you may be able to claim breach of contract.

Working longer than the contract’s end date

If you work past the end of your contract without it being formally renewed, there’s an ‘implied agreement’ by your employer that the end date has changed.

You would then have the right to be given proper notice if your employer wanted to dismiss you.

Example
If your contract was for 1 month or less but you were actually employed for 3 months, you’d be entitled to the minimum notice period (1 week).

The limit on renewing a fixed-term contract

You can be kept on successive fixed-term contracts for limit of 4 years.

If it’s renewed after that, you’ll become a permanent employee unless your employer can show a good reason why you should stay on a fixed-term contract.

You can work for longer than 4 years on fixed-term contracts if it has been agreed by both the employer and employees agreeing a workforce or ‘collective agreement’.

Your employer wants to renew a fixed-term contract on less favourable terms

If you employer wants to renew your fixed-term contract on less favourable terms you can negotiate with them to reach an agreement.

If the contract ends and you have been unable to agree to the less favourable terms of a new contract, you may be able to claim unfair dismissal.

Ending your contract early

You must give 1 week’s notice if you’ve worked for your employer for a month or more. Your contract may state that you need to give longer notice.
