Quick answer Research your family history using the General Register Office

Order birth, adoption, marriage, civil partnership and death certificates from the General Register Office (GRO) to help you research your family history and family tree.

The GRO has records of every birth, marriage and death registered in England and Wales, starting from July 1837. To trace back further you’ll need to look at parish records.

You can order certificates online from GRO for £9.25 each. If you know the certificate’s GRO index number it’ll make the ordering process faster.

Finding the GRO index number

All births, adoptions, marriages, civil partnerships and deaths registered in England or Wales have a GRO index reference number.

Finding index numbers online

You can view index numbers online for free on the FreeBMD website.

Commercial companies also have the index numbers online, but you will have to pay to search them and prices will vary.

Finding index numbers in person

You can also search for index numbers for free at:

  • Birmingham Central Library
  • Bridgend Reference and Information Library
  • City of Westminster Archives Centre
  • Manchester City Library
  • Newcastle City Library
  • Plymouth Central Library
  • The British Library (you’ll need to register first)

Viewing index numbers locally

Some of the index numbers are available at local libraries, archives and other locations.

Download ‘Where you can view the GRO index numbers in your area’ (PDF, 67KB)

If you don’t have an index number

You can still order certificates from GRO if you don’t have the index number, but they’ll take longer to arrive and you’ll need to provide identifying information to help find the correct record.

Certificates in Scotland and Northern Ireland

In Scotland and Northern Ireland certificates are issued by the General Register Office for Scotland and the General Register Office for Northern Ireland.

Overseas records

GRO also has records with the details of births, marriages and deaths of some British citizens that have taken place abroad since the late 18th century.

These include people registered with British consuls, High Commissions, HM Forces, the Civil Aviation Authority and the Registrar General of Shipping and Seaman.

Download ‘Events recorded in England and Wales and overseas by GRO’ (PDF, 42KB)

Divorce or civil partnership dissolution records

For copies of a decree absolute or final order of the dissolution of a civil partnership, you’ll need to either:

  • contact the court where the divorce or dissolution was granted
  • contact the Principal Registry of the Family Division

Further information

You can also search local archives and the National Archives to help research your family history.

The leaflet ‘Discover your family history’ gives further information about researching your family tree. This includes details of the information you’ll find on certificates and what to do if you can’t find a certificate.
