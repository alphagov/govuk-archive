Duty suspensions and tariff quotas

Temporary duty suspensions and tariff quotas for importing raw materials, components and semi-finished products into the European Community.

Introduction

Imported goods usually carry higher rates of duty than the raw materials from which they are manufactured. Temporary duty suspensions and tariff quotas allow the duty-free importation of raw materials, components and semi-finished products into the European Community (EC) which cannot be supplied from EC sources.

Duty suspensions allow unlimited quantities to be imported into the EU. Tariff quotas allow limited quantities to be imported. This system cannot be used simply to import goods for resale.

Applying for a duty suspension or tariff quota

You can apply for a duty suspension or tariff quota if the product or similar products you are importing cannot be sourced or supplied in sufficient quantity from within the EU.

If the imported product is to be further processed the level of duty saving must be at least €20,000 per year. Duty suspensions allow unlimited quantities to be imported into the EU. Tariff quotas allow the importation of limited quantities.

Applications can be made by:

  • any business established in the EU
  • users of the product in question
  • distributors applying on behalf of customers
  • subsidiaries of a third country (a state that is neither a EU member state or an associated state) manufacturers applying on behalf of customers
  • lawyers, accountants or consultants representing one of the above

Once granted, a suspension or quota is available for any business in the EU to use - it is not specific to the company which applied. If your product is covered by an existing suspension or quota you do not need to make a new request.

Deadline for applications

The deadline for applications is 15 March and 15 September each year. If successful, applications submitted by 15 March will become effective the following 1 January and applications submitted by 15 September will become effective the following 1 July.

Send your completed applications or any questions by email to the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) at phil.walker@bis.gsi.gov.uk

For application forms and guidance, you can:

  • download a duty suspensions and tariff quotas application form from the BIS website 
  • download duty suspensions and tariff quotas application guidance from the BIS website 
  • download duty suspensions and tariff quotas guidance on naming conventions from the BIS website (PDF, 33KB)

You can oppose an application for a suspension or quota. Download a duty suspensions and tariff quotas opposition form from the BIS website and email it to phil.walker@bis.gsi.gov.uk

If your opposition is maintained, the suspension or quota will not be granted.

All applications are considered by the Suspensions and Quotas Working Group in Brussels. Where there is unanimous agreement, the European Commission will prepare proposals for duty suspensions and tariff quotas which will appear in the Official Journal of the European Commission.

Find details of existing suspensions and quotas on the Official Journal of the European Commission website.

Duty suspension and tariff quota import procedures

Once you have made an application for a duty suspension or tariff quota, you must complete the customs entry form C88/Single Administrative Document (SAD). You must then send it to HMRC at the port of entry of your goods before importation.

You can download form C88/SAD from the HMRC website.

You can also find guidance on importing and exporting procedure, including how to complete form C88/SAD, on the HMRC website.

When completing customs entry form C88/SAD for quota entries the statement ‘tariff quota’ must appear in red at the top of the form and the tariff quota serial number (TQSN) must be quoted in box 39 of form C88/SAD. You can call the HMRC Tariff Classification Service enquiry line on 01702 366 077 or email them at classification.tso@hmrc.gsi.gov.uk for the TQSN.

You may also require additional forms such as end-use authorisation. For more information end-use relief.

Your quota request will be sent to the HMRC Central Tariff Quota Unit (CTQU). These are then sent to the relevant part of the European Commission which administers the system. The European Commission usually confirms to the CTQU within three days whether or not your claim has been accepted. A copy of the response will be sent to the port of entry.

You can find current quota balances on the Europa website or by contacting the CTQU on 01702 366 787.

Other EU schemes

There are various EU schemes relating to duty relief and suspension of import duties.

Processing Under Customs Control

The PCC scheme is intended to assist - under certain circumstances - traders who import goods for processing that have a higher rate of duty than would otherwise be payable on their finished product.

You can read about PCC on the HMRC website.

Inward Processing

The Inward Processing (IP) scheme is intended to help traders obtain duty relief on goods imported from outside the EC that are processed and re-exported.

You can read about IP on the HMRC website.

Certificate of Airworthiness

This scheme allows the suspension of import duties for products that are intended for the construction, maintenance and repair of civil aircraft.

Military equipment

This scheme allows the suspension of import duties on certain weapons and military aircraft. You can read about duty relief for military equipment on the page on end-use relief for aircraft and aircraft parts in the guide on end-use relief.
