Introduction

Dual-Use items are those goods and technology that may have both civil and military applications. There are 16 available Dual-Use Open General Export Licences (OGELs) that allow the export of specified controlled items by any exporter (listed left).

Use of these OGELs removes the need to apply for a Standard Individual Export Licence (SIEL). However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

In addition to these OGELs, published by the the UK government, you might be able to use the Community General Export Licence (CGEA) published by the EU.

OGEL (Chemicals)

The OGEL (Chemicals) allows, subject to conditions, certain chemicals to be exported from the UK or any other EU Member State (with the exception of some countries), so long as the exporter is established in the UK. There is a 20kg limit on shipments.

Use of these OGELs removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

Current OGEL

You can download the current OGEL (Chemicals) (PDF, 36KB).

The current licence entered into force on 27 August 2009.

You will need to state the exact name of the licence or the SPIRE reference on any supporting documentation given to HMRC.

Using this licence

You will need to register to use this licence on SPIRE. You can apply for an export licence on SPIRE.

It is your responsibility as the exporter to:

  • use this licence within all of its defined terms and conditions
  • keep up-to-date with any licence amendments - eg changes to permitted or excluded destinations or goods

Details of amendments are published in the (ECO) Notices to Exporters.

To receive these notices directly to your email inbox you can join the ECO mailing list. Follow the links above.

OGEL (Export for Repair/Replacement Under Warranty: Dual-Use Items)

The OGEL (Export for Repair/Replacement Under Warranty: Dual-Use Items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of Dual-Use items which had been previously imported into the UK, or any other EU Member State, to be subsequently returned or exported to the original country for repair or replacement under warranty, provided they are to be returned to the UK after such repair/replacement.

The exporter must have an established presence in the UK. The goods can also be exported if they are being returned as unwanted goods.

Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

OGEL (International Non-Proliferation Regime De-controls: Dual-Use Items)

This OGEL allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of specified dual-use items. 

The international non-proliferation regimes (in which the UK actively participates along with other countries) have agreed that the items specifically listed on the licence should be de-controlled.

This OGEL is therefore intended as a temporary measure placing the specified items under a less restricted form of licensing until such time as the Regulation and the associated UK Strategic Export Control Lists are updated.

Background about controls on dual-use items 

The control on the export of dual-use items is applied via the EU Dual-Use Regulation. This is currently being amended to take into account changes agreed by EU Member States in the international non-proliferation regimes during 2011 and will be reflected in amendments to the UK Strategic Export Control Lists in due course.

To be aware of when the Control Lists are updated you can sign up to the ECO’s Notices to Exporters email service, as detailed below.

Current Open General Licence

 The current licence is dated 13 June 2012 and came into force on 15 June 2012.

You will need to state the exact name of the licence or the SPIRE Reference on any supporting documentation given to HMRC. 

Using this licence

If you export the specified dual-use items previously licensable via a SIEL or OIEL, you should check if you are able to use this particular OGEL instead. To do this you can:

  • check the rating of your goods.
  • use the ECO’s OGEL Checker database to guide you through the terms and conditions and licence requirements. You can use the OGEL Checker website (registration required).

OGEL (Low Value Shipments)

The OGEL (Low Value Shipments) allows, subject to certain conditions, a single export order of certain goods to a named consignee in one or more consignments, the total value of which is not more than £6,000. The shipments can be exported from the UK or any other EU Member State where the exporter is established in the UK.

Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

OGEL (Technology for Dual-Use Items)

The OGEL (Technology for Dual-Use Items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of technology for Dual-Use items from the UK or any other EU Member State where the exporter is established in the UK. Technology is the information needed for the development and production of goods such as blueprints, plans, diagrams, models, formulae, tables, designs, specifications, manuals and instructions.

This OGEL includes the transmission of software or technology by fax, telephone or other electronic media.

Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

OGEL (X)

The OGEL (X) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of specific goods from the UK or any other EU Member State where the exporter is established in the UK. Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

OGEL (Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK Forces deployed in embargoed destinations)

The OGEL (Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK Forces deployed in embargoed destinations) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export or transfer of most military and certain Dual-Use goods, including software and technology, the provision of technical assistance, to UK armed forces or Ministry of Defence civilians operating in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Iraq, Liberia and Sierra Leone.

Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.

OGEL (Exports of non-lethal military and Dual-Use goods: To UK Diplomatic Missions or Consular Posts)

The OGEL (Exports of non-lethal military and Dual-Use goods: To UK Diplomatic Missions or Consular Posts) covers the export of a limited range of non-lethal military and Dual-Use goods, subject to certain conditions and restrictions, to any UK diplomatic mission or consular post overseas where it is intended for the exclusive use of UK government personnel.

Use of this OGEL removes the need to apply for a SIEL. However, exporters must ensure all conditions are met and shipment and destinations are eligible. Exporters will be subject to regular compliance visits.
