Introduction

Learning and training opportunities are important to the farming sector.

Whether you are considering a career in farming - or another land-based industry - or are an established farmer and want to further develop your skills, finding the right education and training in this highly skilled profession is essential.

Qualifications and national standards in the sector are being continuously reviewed and developed to ensure that they meet the needs of the industry.

This guide will help those starting a career understand the range of education and training available to gain the necessary skills and knowledge. It also provides valuable signposts to sourcing further information and support.

It outlines the opportunities available to farmers to update and improve existing skills, and the importance of continuing professional development. This includes business, marketing and management knowledge, and keeping up-to-date with technological advances in an ever-changing industry.

The guide also examines government funding initiatives to sustain and rejuvenate the farming industry.

Benefiting from agricultural skills and training

In addition to the courses offered by the range of specialised private training providers and Fresh Start, there are also graduate opportunities.

Landex is a collection of 34 member colleges in England that offer various land-based and farming courses. Courses offered range from full-time, part-time and flexible learning to pre-entry and postgraduate programmes. The course subjects include:

  • agriculture
  • countryside management
  • environmental conservation
  • horticulture
  • food processing

Find a list of Landex member colleges on the Landex website.

If you are planning to study at any of these colleges, entry to an Higher National Diploma (HND) course usually requires four GCSEs with A to C grades plus one A-level, or an equivalent qualification. Degree courses normally require five GCSEs plus two A-levels or equivalent qualifications. The new Diploma in Environmental and Land-based Studies also gives you the opportunity to go on to study further and higher education.

New entrants into farming and the land-based sector

New entrants into the farming and land-based sector help ensure the future of the industry. Careers range from hands-on practical work through to demanding management opportunities with the chance to manage your own business or work abroad. As new technology advances, the opportunities continue to grow.

Fresh Start

Fresh Start training academies encourage young rural entrepreneurs to consider a career in farming or other rural-related businesses. There are 24 academies across England offering 12-month courses covering different areas of farm business management. You can find out about the Fresh Start Academy on the Career in Farming website.

This website contains careers information for anyone wanting to start and develop a career in the sector. Developed by Lantra, Sector Skills Council, the site provides careers information for all 17 environmental and land-based industries. Lantra CourseFinder

Managed by Lantra, CourseFinder is a free service to help you search over 8,000 courses from over 600 training providers ranging from colleges to private training providers. Find a course on the Lantra CourseFinder website.

Connexions Direct Jobs4U

Jobs4U is a searchable database - part of Connexions Direct website - offering 13-19 year-olds information on a range of farming careers. Find out about the Jobs4U tool on the Connexions Direct website.

Diploma in Environmental and Land-based Studies

The Diploma in Environmental and Land-based Studies has been structured to allow young people in England to study it alongside GCSEs and A Levels. It is available at Foundation, Higher and Advanced Levels in selected schools and colleges from September 2009, with national availability by 2013.

The courses aim to equip young people with the knowledge and understanding relevant to a career in the environmental and land-based sector, and a choice of routes afterwards include farming and land-based apprenticeships, further or higher education opportunities in farming, or directly into work.

Find out about the Diploma in Environmental and Land-based Studies and how employers can support its delivery on the Edexcel website.

Vocational training

There are a number of vocational training programmes designed specifically for workers in the agricultural sector. As an employer, you have the flexibility to train your employees in areas specific to your needs and your employees enjoy continuous employment, learn at their own pace and gain new skills. Vocational - including ‘on-the-job’ - training can work in several ways.

Some vocational training falls within the new Qualifications and Credit Framework (QCF). The introduction of the QCF means that training can be delivered as units - bite-sized chunks of learning - and combined with others to build into full vocational qualifications.

The day release system allows people to study while they are in employment. They usually work full time, but for one day a week for a specified period, their employer allows them to attend a college or training course so they can study for a qualification - such as the Diploma in Work-based Agriculture (formerly known as a National Vocational Qualification (NVQ)). Through this new system, you can follow a unit-based, flexible approach to developing your skills, which can build to full qualifications without having to give up work.

Alternative vocational qualifications include the HND and the Higher National Certificate (HNC) where students attend college or a training course for half a day, then go to work with an employer in their chosen field.

Read information on QCF vocational qualifications in land-based and related industries on the NPTC website.

Block release also involves combining work and study. The student attends a course for a specified number of days over a specified time period. For example, someone who is studying livestock management may attend their course for 44 days over a 12-month period.

Read information on work-based learning and qualifications on the learndirect website.

Farming and land-based apprenticeships

Apprenticeships combine on-the-job training with study at an approved college or training provider.

Employers often find that offering apprenticeships is the best way of ensuring that the workers they employ have exactly the right set of skills for the job.

Apprenticeships are a valuable tool in motivating and retaining your workforce, as they are available to new and existing employees.

There are 15 apprenticeships offered:

  • agricultural crops, livestock and mixed farming
  • amenity horticulture
  • animal care
  • aquaculture
  • dry stone walling
  • environmental conservation
  • equine
  • farriery
  • fencing
  • floristry
  • game and wildlife management
  • land-based service engineering
  • production horticulture
  • trees and timber
  • veterinary nursing

Find out about environmental and land-based apprenticeships on the Lantra website.

Recognising and recording skills

There is a vast amount of training in the farming and land-based sectors which goes unrecognised.

Skills and learning are critical to the success of farming businesses. Farmers need to understand the importance of skills they and their staff already possess and encourage greater participation in learning and development and Continuing Professional Development (CPD). Some industries already have CPD schemes which recognise learning. These include:

  • Pig Industry Professional Register (PIPR) - where those working in the pig sector can register their staff and participate in relevant, demand-led development courses. Find out about the PIPR and how to become a member on the BPEX website.
  • BASIS Professional Register - where members can earn CPD points each year through a range of registered events and learning activities, relating to crop protection and the environment. Join the BASIS Professional Register on the BASIS website.
  • National register of sprayer operators (NROSO) - uses CPD as a means of ensuring ongoing training. Find out about the register and join on the NROSO website.
  • Fertiliser Advisers Certification and Training Scheme (FACTS) provides a recognised standard of competence for UK advisers on crop nutrition and fertiliser use. Find out how to join FACTS on the FACTS website.
  • Lantra’s Skills Manager is an online tool that allows employers, employees and volunteers to assess their own skills and record practical experiences, achievements and qualifications. The system also helps to identify skills gaps, allowing training and development planning and documenting. Find out about Skills Manager on the Lantra website.

Funding opportunities for training and knowledge transfer activities

Lantra - the Sector Skills Council for the environmental and land-based industries - manages a range of programmes across England that offer funding support for training and knowledge transfer activities.

LandSkills is managed by Lantra and focuses on skills and knowledge transfer for farming, forestry and production horticulture businesses. Activities range from targeted training to innovative events to share best practice, such as study trips abroad, all designed to meet industry needs and deliver business benefits. LandSkills is available in the following regions:

  • North East
  • Yorkshire and Humber
  • East Midlands
  • East
  • South East
  • West Midlands

Find out about the LandSkills programme on the Lantra website..

The Women and Work Programme provides funding for training to help women further their skills in areas with low female representation. These areas include:

  • agriculture
  • horticulture
  • land-based engineering
  • environmental conservation
  • fencing
  • game and wildlife management
  • landscaping
  • trees and timber

Find information on Women in Work on the Lantra website.

Organisations that can help

Further information on vehicles, machinery and equipment on farms is available in other guides on this website and from a number of organisations.

Lantra is the Sector Skills Council for environmental and land-based industries. Lantra enables people working in the sector to have the right skills and knowledge to carry out their jobs safely and effectively and for businesses to have a workforce that can enhance productivity and performance.

You can call the Lantra Helpline on Tel 0845 707 8007 or email them at connect@lantra.co.uk.

The Skills Funding Agency is a national agency of the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS) and is responsible for funding and regulating adult skills training in England. It brings together the various skills and employment services available to support employers. Read about employer support on the Skills Funding Agency website. You can contact the Skills Funding Agency Helpline on Tel 0870 900 6800.

One of the major roles of the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) is to help the farming industry operate as efficiently as possible. Defra administers European support policies that provide around £3 billion to UK agriculture. They also oversee a number of agencies that work with arable farmers, imports and exports of crops and implement pest and disease controls. You can call the Defra Helpline on Tel 08459 33 55 77.

In England, the Farm Advisory System advises farmers about cross compliance. For further information, call the Cross Compliance Helpline on Tel 0845 345 1302. Alternatively, find information on cross compliance requirements on the Cross Compliance website.

The National Farmers’ Union (NFU) represents the farmers and growers of England and Wales. It aims to promote successful and socially responsible agriculture and horticulture, while ensuring the long-term viability of rural communities.

Read about the work of the NFU on the NFU website.

Farmers are likely to come into contact with local authorities over a number of farming, land use, food standards and environmental regulations. Your local authority may also be able to provide further information or resources.

Find contact details for your local authority through our Contacts Directory.

Natural England also provides a series of seminars, conferences, walks, farm visits and workshops covering a wide range of land management and environmental advice. For more information, you can contact the Natural England Enquiry Service on Tel 0845 600 3078.
