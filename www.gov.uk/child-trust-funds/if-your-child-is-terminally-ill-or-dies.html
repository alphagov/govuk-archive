Part 8: If your child is terminally ill or dies

You can take money out of your child’s Child Trust Fund (CTF) account if they are terminally ill. If your child has died, the money passes to whoever inherits the child’s estate (their property and possessions).

If your child is terminally ill

‘Terminally ill’ means that they have a disease or illness that is going to get worse and are likely to die within 6 months. Only the registered contact can take money out.

How to take money out

Contact the CTF Helpline to let them know that:

  • your child is terminally ill
  • you want to take the money out of the CTF account

You’ll need to provide evidence that your child is terminally ill. This could be a letter from a doctor.

If your child is already getting Disability Living Allowance (DLA) because of their illness, you won’t need to provide proof. Tell the CTF Helpline when you contact them that your child gets DLA.

CTF Helpline
Telephone: 0845 302 1470
(+44 1355 359 002 from outside the UK)
Textphone: 0845 366 7870

If your child dies

The money in the account will be paid to the person who inherits the child’s estate. This is often one of the child’s parents, but if your child was married, it could be their husband or wife.

If you weren’t able to open the account before your child died, HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) will still pay you what your child was entitled to.

If you get Child Benefit for a child who has died this can still carry on for a short time. It may also help with any extra costs you face at this time.

What you need to do

You need to do the following:

  • if the account has already been opened - tell the account provider
  • if you haven’t opened an account yet - tell the CTF Helpline
  • if you get Child Benefit for the child who has died - call the Child Benefit Helpline:

Child Benefit Helpline
Telephone: 0845 302 1444
(+44 161 210 3086 from outside the UK)
Textphone: 0845 302 1474

The account provider will usually need proof, like the death certificate.
