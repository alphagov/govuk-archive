Determinations under the Fire Safety Order

This guide contains a series of determinations on fire safety risk assessments in specific non-domestic properties

England.

The Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 (the Order) put responsibility for fire safety in non-domestic properties with a designated responsible person. Under the order, the responsible person is required to carry out fire risk assessments and to put in place appropriate fire safety measures, based on the specific circumstances of their premises.

The Order is enforced by periodic inspections or audits by the enforcing authority (usually the Fire and Rescue Authorities).

Where both parties (responsible person and enforcing authority) agree that there is a need for improvements to fire precautions but disagree on the technical solution to be used, they may agree to refer the issue to the Secretary of State for independent determination under article 36 of the Order.

Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005

With the introduction of the Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005 (“the Order”) in October 2006, Fire and Rescue Authorities and other bodies (“enforcing authorities”) now have a duty to enforce fire safety in non-domestic premises. This guidance note sets out the procedures for the enforcing authority and the responsible person under the legislation to ask the Secretary of State to determine disputes. This is the second guidance note in a series intended to assist enforcing authorities in better understanding their duties under the Order and for the public to better understand the intent of specific aspects of the Order.
