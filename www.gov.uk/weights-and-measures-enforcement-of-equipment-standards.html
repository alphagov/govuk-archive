Weights and measures: enforcement of equipment standards

Compliance for manufacturers of measuring instruments, guidance on self verification and conformity assessments.

Introduction

UK manufacturers of measuring instruments need to comply with UK and EU legislation, including the New Approach Directives. These harmonise the trade and use of measuring instruments across the EU by ensuring that instruments are manufactured to the same criteria and standards of quality.

Measuring instruments approved under the directives results in instruments being marked with a CE conformity mark by their manufacturers, meaning that they can be sold and used in any EU country. The National Measurement Office (NMO) weights and measures enforcement team has a duty to establish that any measuring instruments bearing this mark comply with the legislation.

NMO weights and measures enforcement responsibilities

The NMO has a duty to ensure that all UK measuring instruments on the market comply with UK and EU legislation.

They do this by working closely with other professional bodies including:

  • local weights and measures authorities (LWMAs) who deliver the local enforcement of most of the UK’s weights and measures legislation
  • local government regulation - the national coordinating body of local authorities
  • the Trading Standards Institute (TSI) - the professional institute for inspectors of weights and measures
  • regional metrology groups made up from groups of LWMAs
  • approved verifiers - businesses appointed by the secretary of state to conduct weights and measures activity
  • accreditation certification bodies
  • notified bodies
  • the United Kingdom Accreditation Service (UKAS)

The New Approach Directives are a set of European directives which set out essential requirements under which products can be approved for CE conformity marking. Compliance with these standards allows the free movement of goods in the EU. Read about CE marking on the Europa website.

The NMO is responsible for enforcing the following directives relating to weights and measures:

  • Measuring Instruments Directive - which covers standards for water meters, gas meters and volume conversion devices, active electrical energy meters, heat meters, measuring systems for continuous and dynamic measurement of quantities of liquids other then water, automatic weighing instruments, taximeters, material measures, dimensional measuring instruments and exhaust gas analysers
  • Non-Automatic Weighing Instruments Directive

The NMO carries out market surveillance activities to ensure that the aims of the directives are carried out.

WELMEC

The European Cooperation in Legal Metrology (WELMEC) is a European administrative cooperation made up of representative national authorities responsible for consistency in legal metrology amongst the EU member states. They have also produced a guide which helps to identifying the recommended approach to market surveillance, which the NMO follows. You can download a market surveillance guide from the WELMEC website.

Local weights and measures authorities

Local weights and measures authorities are trading standards departments that deliver the local enforcement of most of the UK’s weights and measures legislation. The NMO works closely with them to promote consistency and best practice.

Notified bodies

Notified bodies are third-party organisations that make sure manufacturers of weighing and measuring equipment undergo regular assessment to ensure that they can make declarations to type conformity. They are appointed by EU member states and the UK Secretary of State.

Under the New Approach Directives, instrument types and designs covered by the directive must undergo conformity assessments by the NMO before they are placed on the European market.

To be impartial, it is essential that people who carry out conformity assessments are not the:

  • designer or anyone involved in the design
  • manufacturer
  • supplier
  • installer or involved in the maintenance of the instruments being inspected
  • user or authorised representative of the measuring instruments being inspected

Read about notified bodies on the Societe Generale de Surveillance (SGS) website.

You can find a local notified body to help your business with conformity assessment using the NMO search tool

Approved manufacturers, installers and repairers - self verification

Under section 11A of the Weights and Measures Act 1985 (as amended), manufacturers, installers and repairers of weighing and measuring equipment can test, pass and verify their own work, place their products on the market or return them to the market after a repair.

Approved verifiers must undergo regular inspection by both local weights and measures authorities and the NMO. They are approved for a limited time and this can be taken away if the approved verifier breaks approval conditions.

You can download the approved verifier register from the NMO website (PDF, 204K).

Applying for approved verifier certification

Manufacturers, installers and repairers who want to apply for approved verification status must provide documentation to prove they have an acceptable quality management system.

Apply to become an approved verifier with the NMO.

Weights and measures enforcement

The NMO produces reports and information to help public sector organisations enforce weights and measurement standards in the UK including market surveillance which involves test purchases and product testing.

W&M Bulletins

The W&M Bulletins contain guidance from the NMO for inspectors of weights and measures, approved verifiers and notified bodies on the interpretation of weights and measures legislation. Find up-to-date bulletins on weights and measures in the UK on the NMO website.

National stamp numbers

Stamp identification numbers are allocated to local weights and measures authorities and approved verifiers so that it is possible to identify which party approved a piece of weighing or measuring equipment. You can download a list of national stamp numbers from the NMO website (PDF, 219K).

EEC stamp numbers

EEC stamp numbers are allocated to European weights and measures authorities to identify who has approved a piece of weighing or measuring equipment. They are the European equivalent of national stamp numbers. Download a list of EEC stamp numbers from the NMO website (PDF, 31KB).
