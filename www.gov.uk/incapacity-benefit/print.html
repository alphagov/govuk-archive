Benefits & credits: Incapacity Benefit

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Since 31 January 2011 you can no longer make a claim for Incapacity Benefit. You should claim Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) instead.

You may still be getting Incapacity Benefit if you claimed before 31 January 2011.

Incapacity Benefit is being phased out. If you’re getting Incapacity Benefit, Jobcentre Plus will review your claim. They will check if you’re capable of work or can get ESA instead.

Part 2: What you'll get

Incapacity Benefit is being phased out. This means Jobcentre Plus will review your Incapacity Benefit claim. They will check if you’re capable of work or can get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) instead.

Until your claim is reviewed you will continue to get your current Incapacity Benefit, as long as you still meet the conditions for the benefit.

Incapacity Benefit (IB) is paid at 3 weekly rates:

  • short-term (lower) IB is paid for the first 28 weeks
  • short-term (higher) IB is paid from weeks 29 to 52
  • long-term IB is paid from week 53
Weekly rate Amount Amount if you’re over State Pension age
short-term (lower rate) £74.80 £95.15
short-term (higher rate) £88.55 £99.15
long-term basic rate £99.15 You’re not eligible for long-term basic rate IB

How Incapacity Benefit is paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

Pension income rules

If you have a gross pension income of more than £85 a week, the amount of benefit will be reduced by half of the excess. The excess is the difference between £85 and your actual pension income.

For example, for a pension income of £100, the excess is £15. The amount of Incapacity Benefit payable is reduced by half of that, which is £7.50.

Exceptions to the pension income rules

The pension incomes rules do not apply if:

  • you were getting Incapacity Benefit before 6 April 2001
  • your claim was made before 6 April 2001
  • you get the highest rate of the care component of Disability Living Allowance (DLA)

Part 3: Eligibility

Since 31 January 2011 you can no longer make a claim for Incapacity Benefit. You should claim Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) instead.

You may have been able to get Incapacity Benefit before 31 January 2011 if you couldn’t work because of illness or disability.

If you’re already getting Incapacity Benefit

Incapacity Benefit is being phased out. This means Jobcentre Plus will review your Incapacity Benefit claim. They will check if you’re capable of work or can get ESA instead.

Part 4: How to claim

Since 31 January 2011 you can no longer make a claim for Incapacity Benefit. You should claim Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) instead.

Part 5: Further information

Review of your Incapacity Benefit claim

Incapacity Benefit is being phased out. Jobcentre Plus will review your Incapacity Benefit claim to check if you’re capable of work or can get Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) instead.

Your benefit claim will not be reviewed if:

  • you’re already getting ESA
  • you are due to reach State Pension age before 6 April 2014

How your claim will be reviewed

Jobcentre Plus will write to you when your benefit claim is going to be reviewed. Not everyone will be contacted at the same time. This process started in October 2010 and is expected to be completed in 2014.

Questionnaire

Jobcentre Plus will send you a questionnaire to complete. This questionnaire will ask about how your long term health condition or disability affects your ability to complete everyday tasks.

You must complete this questionnaire with as much detail as possible and return it by the date requested. If you don’t, your benefit claim may be affected.

Include with your questionnaire any medical reports or letters you have which you feel will help to explain your long term health condition or disability. Send the originals - they will be returned to you. If you can’t send the originals, send photo-copies.

Work Capability Assessment

The information you give will be used to decide if you need to attend a face-to-face assessment. This is called a Work Capability Assessment. It will help find out:

  • what you’re able to do
  • whether you might be capable of work

If you’re asked to attend an assessment, you must go and take part fully. If you don’t, your benefit claim may be affected.

If the review finds that you’re capable of working

If this happens, Jobcentre Plus will call you. They will discuss what benefits you might be able to claim, eg Jobseeker’s Allowance. They will also write to you.

If the review finds you’re entitled to ESA

If you’re entitled to ESA, Jobcentre Plus will call you to let you know. They will also write to you.

Your Incapacity Benefit claim will be transferred automatically to ESA. There will be no break in the payments you get.

Depending on how severe your disability or health condition is, you will be placed in one of two groups:

  • Work Related Activity Group
  • Support Group

Work Related Activity Group

If you’re placed in the Work Related Activity Group, you will get support to help you prepare for suitable work.

Your benefit may be affected if you don’t take part in the work-related support.

Support Group

If you are severely disabled or have the most severe health conditions you will be placed in the Support Group. You will not be expected to look for work and will get the extra support you need.

You will not have to take up any work-related support unless you want to.

If your circumstances change

Tell Jobcentre Plus if your circumstances change. Some examples of the changes you need to report include:

  • doing any work, including voluntary work
  • starting training and getting a training allowance
  • changing your address
  • going abroad
  • going into hospital (your Incapacity Benefit won’t usually be affected)
