Nitrate Vulnerable Zones

How to find out if your farm is in a Nitrate Vulnerable Zone (NVZ) and how to comply with NVZ rules

Introduction

If your farm is in a Nitrate Vulnerable Zone (NVZ), an area designated as being at risk from agricultural nitrate pollution, you must comply with NVZ rules.

This guide explains how to find out if your farm is in an NVZ, and what you need to do to comply with the NVZ rules.

If you don’t comply with the NVZ rules, you may be prosecuted and fined. You must also comply with NVZ rules to be entitled to a full subsidy payment under the Single Payment Scheme (SPS).

Is your farm in an NVZ?

Nitrate vulnerable zones (NVZs) are areas designated as being at risk from agricultural nitrate pollution. If your farm is in an NVZ, you must comply with the NVZ rules.

If you have not had a letter from the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) and wish to find out if your land is in an NVZ, you can view field boundary maps of NVZs in England on the Defra website. These detailed regional maps also identify deferred slurry storage areas. For farmers with land in these areas, the date for complying with the slurry storage requirements has been extended by one year to 1 January 2013.

Alternatively, you can call the Defra Helpline on 08459 33 55 77 or email them at helpline@defra.gsi.gov.uk to request a hard copy map of the designated NVZs that affect your farm land.

NVZ and cross compliance

To qualify for full payment under the SPS and other direct payments - eg the Environmental Stewardship schemes - you must meet all relevant cross compliance requirements. These requirements are split into two types:

  • Statutory Management Requirements (SMRs)
  • requirements to keep your land in Good Agricultural and Environmental Condition (GAECs)

If your farm is in a Nitrate Vulnerable Zone and you claim payments under SPS, you must comply with SMR 4 in order to claim your payments. 

Find information on SMR 4 on the Rural Payments Agency (RPA) website.

GAEC 19 - No Spread Zones

These requirements are aimed at protecting water against pollution and run-off from agricultural sources.

This standard came into force from 1 January 2012.

You can find information on the requirements of GAEC 19 - No Spread Zones on the RPA website.

How to comply with NVZ rules

If your farm is in an NVZ, you must comply with the NVZ rules.

Follow the NVZ guidance leaflets

You must follow the guidance that is relevant to your farm activities in the leaflets on the NVZ rules for farmers produced by Defra. The guidance has been divided into nine leaflets and a record keeping checklist. You must work through all the guides that are relevant to you to ensure you comply with the NVZ rules.

Find the NVZ guidance leaflets on the Defra website.

Requirements of the NVZ rules

If your farm is in an NVZ you must:

  • plan your use of livestock manure and manufactured nitrogen fertilisers to ensure that you don’t apply more nitrogen than your crops require
  • produce a risk map  for any land where you intend to spread organic manure
  • comply with the field limit, the Nmax (crop nitrogen requirement) limit, closed periods and spreading controls for spreading manufactured nitrogen fertilisers and organic manures
  • comply with the livestock manure N (nitrogen) farm limit
  • provide adequate storage capacity  for livestock manures - you must have sufficient storage in place by 1 January 2012 at the latest, unless you are in a deferred slurry storage area in which case the compliance date is 1 January 2013
  • keep records of the nitrogen applied to each of your fields, and some records and calculations relating to your farm as a whole - you must keep all records for at least five years

Apply for the derogation for grassland farmers

If you are a grassland farmer and meet certain conditions, you may be able to apply for a derogation from the 170 kilograms of nitrogen per hectare per year (kgN/ha/year) limit on the amount of manure from grazing livestock you can put on your farm. The derogation would allow you to farm up to a limit of 250kgN/ha/year. This roughly equates to a stocking rate of 2.5 cows per hectare rather than 1.7 cows (6000-9000 litre milk yield).

To be eligible for a derogation at least 80% of your farm must be grassland, and you must have sufficient land to comply with the 250kgN/ha/year limit. If your application is approved you will have to comply with additional requirements. You have to apply for a derogation each year.

Find Defra guidance on the derogated livestock manure N farm limit on the Agricultural Document Library (AdLib) website.

Submit an NVZ Derogation Application with Defra.

Alternatively, you can apply by telephone or get a paper application form by calling the Environment Agency’s Derogation Line on 0845 603 3113.

You must apply for 2012 derogations between 1 October and 31 December 2011.

Information and guidance to help with NVZ calculations

You can use software tools to help with some of the calculations required under the NVZ rules.

Planning Land Applications of Nutrients for Efficiency and the Environment (PLANET) helps you calculate field-level nutrients, calculate nitrogen limits and your manure storage capacity. It also helps you to keep records. You can download the PLANET software free of charge from the PLANET website, or by calling the PLANET Helpline on 08456 023 864. Find out about the PLANET software on the PLANET website.

Manure Nitrogen Evaluation Routine (MANNER) is a software tool to help you calculate crop-available manure nitrogen.

Find out about MANNER on the ADAS website.

Order a free copy of MANNER on the ADAS website.

The NVZ Self Assessment Tool helps you store data about your farm, and assess your activities against the requirements of the NVZ rules.

Use the NVZ Self Assessment Tool from Defra.

There are also paper-based tools to help with some aspects of the NVZ rules.

The Tried & Tested nutrient management plan helps you plan how you use manure and fertilisers, and makes recording information easier. Find the Nutrient management plan on the Tried & Tested website.
