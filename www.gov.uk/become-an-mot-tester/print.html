Become an MOT tester, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

A Nominated Tester (NT) is someone who’s approved to carry out MOT tests.

To become an NT, you’ll first need to show that you’re eligible.

 If you meet the requirements you’ll then need to  successfully complete a Nominated Tester Training (NTT) course.

Once you’re approved as an MOT tester you’ll need to take a refresher course every 5 years.

Part 2: Eligibility

To be eligible, you must:

  • be nominated by an Authorised Examiner
  • have a current and full UK driving licence for the vehicle classes you want to test
  • be a skilled mechanic with at least 4 years’ full-time employment in the  service and repair of the vehicle types to be tested
  • have no unspent convictions for criminal offences connected with the MOT testing scheme or the motor trade, or involving acts of violence or intimidation
  • be ‘of good repute’ (eg you have a good reputation in the community, in business etc)

If you want to become a Nominated Tester for class 3, 4, 5 or 7 vehicles you must also either:

  • have an appropriate qualification
  • pass the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) Nominated Tester Training Assessment (NTTA) exam

You don’t have the qualifications

If you don’t have the correct qualifications to become an MOT tester you can take the Nominated Tester Training Assessment (NTTA) exam.

Part 4: Nominated Tester Training Assessment (NTTA) exam

You can take this if you don’t have the correct qualifications to become an MOT tester.

The exam is one-and-a-half hours and takes place at locations across the UK. There are 60 multiple-choice questions.

Booking your exam

To book your exam, fill in a VT78 application form and return it to the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA).

If you fail the exam - appealing

If you fail the NTTA exam and want to appeal, write to your local VOSA office. They’ll review your result for you.

Part 5: Nominated Tester Training (NTT) course

To become an MOT Nominated Tester (NT), you’ll need to complete a 2 or 3 day course, depending on the vehicle class that you want to test.

Before the course

You need to show that you:

  • meet the qualifying criteria to become an NT
  • hold a relevant qualification

Class 1 and 2

The 2 day Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) course for classes 1 and 2 motorbike MOT testing covers the following:

  • relevant acts and regulations
  • classes of vehicle
  • standards and procedures
  • reasons for refusal to start a test
  • recent changes
  • tools for the job
  • documentation and security
  • use of the vehicle testing station (VTS) MOT Computerisation device

Class 3, 4, 5 or 7

The 3 day course for classes 3, 4, 5 and 7 covers:

  • relevant acts and regulations
  • tools for the job
  • when you can refuse to begin a test
  • use of the testing manual
  • standards and procedures
  • documentation and security
  • use of the VTS MOT Computerisation device
  • routine inspection
  • classes of vehicle

Part 7: Refresher course

The Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) constantly monitors the work of Nominated Testers.

You must perform consistently or you could have your status revoked.

To guarantee the standard of MOT testing, you must also attend a refresher course every 5 years. You might have to take a refresher course before this if:

  • you’re involved in disciplinary action
  • you haven’t done any testing for some time

When you’re due to take the refresher course you’ll be sent a message via  the MOT computer system at your test station.

The first message will be issued when your training is due in 180 days,  followed by a second message 30 days later.

If you don’t take the refresher course you’ll be sent a final message  saying your training is overdue - you’ll be suspended from testing until  you complete the course.

Booking your course

  • through the MOT computer system
