ERDF regional guidance: Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly

Guidance about the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) in Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly and how to apply for funding.

Introduction

Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly together form the only area in England to qualify for ERDF convergence funding. This was awarded under European Union funding rules because the region’s gross domestic product was less than 75% of the European Union average.

This means that the area may receive a total of €458.1 million ERDF investment for the 2007 to 2013 programming period.

Call for expressions of interest under the Cornwall & Isles of Scilly Convergence Programme are promoted in the detailed guide about applying for ERDF funding section. There are no current live calls.

Priorities

The Cornwall and Isles of Scilly operational programme document (see ‘Programme documents’ below) outlines 4 main investment priorities for the convergence programme in the Cornwall and Isles of Scilly area:

Priority 1: innovation, research and development

This priority aims to enable Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly to compete as a centre for creativity, innovation and research and development.

Priority 2: enterprise and investment

This priority aims to restructure the economy to one with a higher proportion of high value added business, under-pinned by more productive enterprises across the business base.

Priority 3: transformational infrastructure

The aims are to accelerate the growth of the knowledge-based economy through the development of transformational infrastructure and to secure sustainable economic growth though the use of transformational economic infrastructure.

Priority 4: place-based regeneration

The aim is to develop the capacity of key towns and locations to accommodate new investment critical to the development of a knowledge-based economy.

The cross-cutting themes of equality and environmental sustainability are integral to the successful delivery of the programme.

The programme’s vision is to establish the momentum for transforming the economy of Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly to one where knowledge, environment and quality of life underpin sustainable economic growth. With this in mind, 4 operational objectives have been set:

  1. transforming the economy into one based on professional, highly skilled jobs with a broader range of sectors, and a reduced dependence on low-paid employment
  2. increasing the range and quality of employment opportunities available to the community
  3. managing economic growth in a sustainable manner
  4. take a leading role in investing in what drives a low carbon economy

Further information about the programme’s investments is provided by convergence partners on the Convergence Cornwall website.

Annual implementation report

Regulations set down by the European Commission (EC) governing the management of the ERDF programmes require the programme partners to produce a yearly progress report known as an annual implementation report.

Each report covers a calendar year, and includes progress made during that period in areas such as:

  • financial progress
  • communications
  • achievements
  • monitoring and any programme-related issues arising during the year.

The programme monitoring committee (local management committee after 2011) agrees the content of the report and it is submitted to the EC by 30 June of the following year. Reports are scrutinised to ensure the programme is being effectively managed. Once each report has been accepted by the EC, it will be published below.

Local ERDF guidance

The Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly Programme Delivery Team provide guidance and support to project applicants and grant recipients in relation to ERDF requirements throughout the course of a project’s development.

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the ERDF programmes across England. This includes the introduction of a new suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects approved to receive funding after 1 April 2012 will need to use and follow.

For projects required to use the new documentation see the guide on ERDF: national guidance.

All forms and guidance in existence before 1 April 2012 that ERDF-supported projects have used or are currently using are available from your local programme delivery team.

Please ensure that you are using the correct forms and guidance for your project. If you have any questions, please contact the Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly Programme Delivery Team at SWconv.ERDFenquiries@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Local management committee

The Cornwall and Isles of Scilly convergence joint ERDF and European Social Fund (ESF) local management committee is the partnership body which provides strategic leadership of the Cornwall & Isles of Scilly convergence programme. It plays a key role in driving the programme to achieve its targets and monitoring performance and compliance.

Following a change in the delivery arrangements for European programmes in England, the local management committee replaced the former convergence joint ERDF and ESF programme monitoring committee. The inaugural meeting of the local management committee was on 14 October 2011.

Membership of the local management committee is listed in the terms of reference below. It includes representation from local authorities, the private sector, co-financing organisations, the voluntary sector, trade unions, skills, environment and equalities interests. The group is chaired by DCLG and its deputy chairs are the leader of Cornwall Council and Chairman of the Cornwall and Isles of Scilly local enterprise partnership.

Local management committee sub-groups

The local management committee has 3 sub-groups:

  • the ESF Steering Group steers and monitors delivery of the ESF convergence framework
  • the Convergence Management Group ensures day-to-day delivery of the programme in relation to investment decisions and delivery methods
  • a post-2013 sub-group will ensure that the local management committee is appraised of the future direction of European programmes

Former programme monitoring committee

The final meeting of the convergence joint ERDF and ESF programme monitoring committee took place on 8 June 2011.

The committee was set up in accordance with European regulations, and members were drawn from a range of partners across the public, private, and community and voluntary sectors. Alongside the ERDF ESF managing authorities, the committee had a key strategic role in implementing the convergence programme by monitoring the quality and effectiveness of investments made.
