Part 3: Making changes to your PSV licence

Minor variations

You can apply to make minor variations to your licence for free. These include:

  • adding vehicles to your licence
  • removing vehicles from your licence
  • adding or removing operating centres
  • adding or removing a transport manager (standard national and standard international licences only)

Download ‘PSV430, Vehicles to be used under a PSV operator’s licence’ (PDF, 42KB)

Download ‘PSV430G, Guidance notes to help you complete your application to change the vehicles used under your PSV operator’s licence’ (PDF, 26KB)

Download ‘PSV431AG, Guidance notes to help you complete your application to vary a PSV operator’s licence’ (PDF, 38KB)

How to make minor variations

You can apply to make minor variations to your PSV licence online if you register for the operator licensing self-service system.

You can also fill in PSV431A and send to the address on the form.

Download ‘PSV431A, Application to a Traffic Commissioner for a non-chargeable variation of a PSV operator’s licence’ (PDF, 65KB)

Download ‘PSV431G, Guidance on varying a PSV operators licence’ (PDF, 47KB)

You can also use this form to apply to upgrade from a standard national licence to a standard international licence if you don’t want to increase the number of vehicles you can operate.

Transport managers

You can apply to be a transport manager or add a transport manager to a licence.

Download ‘TM1, Application to add a transport manager to a licence’ (PDF, 49KB)

Download ‘TM1G, Guidance notes’ (PDF, 50KB)

Major variations

You’ll have to pay a fee if you want to:

  • increase the number of vehicles you’re authorised to operate
  • upgrade your licence from standard national to standard international and increase your vehicle authorisation at the same time

How to apply for major variations

You can apply to make major variations to your PSV licence online if you register for the operator licensing self-service system.

You can also fill in PSV431 and send to the address on the form.

Download ‘PSV431, Application to a Traffic Commissioner for a chargeable variation of a Public Service Vehicle Operator’s Licence’ (PDF, 141KB)

Download ‘PSV431G, Guidance on varying a PSV Operators Licence’ (PDF, 47KB)

Changes of circumstance

You must tell the Traffic Commissioner within 28 days if:

  • there’s any change to the correspondence address of the business
  • there’s any change to the establishment address (standard licences only)
  • there’s any change in the address of your operating centres
  • there’s any change in the trading name of the business
  • any of the persons named on the licence have died (in this case you may need a new licence)
  • there’s been a change of partners if it’s a partnership firm (in this case you may need a new licence)
  • there’s been a change of transport managers
  • you, your transport manager, officers employees or agents have any relevant convictions
  • there’s been any other change that the Traffic Commissioner asked you to report as a condition of getting your licence

You’ll also have to tell the Traffic Commissioner if there’s been a change in the ‘legal entity’ of your business, for example if:

  • your company changes from being a sole trader or partnership to a limited company
  • there’s been a change in your registered company number
  • there’s been a change of directors or change in share holding

Send details of any changes to the Office of the Traffic Commissioner’s Central Licensing Office.
