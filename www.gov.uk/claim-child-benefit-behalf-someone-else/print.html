Claim Child Benefit on behalf of someone else, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: If your child has a baby

The Child Benefit Office can only pay Child Benefit into one account.

If a child you’re responsible for has a baby, you can claim Child Benefit for both of them.

If your child is claiming the Child Benefit, you can collect the payment on their behalf by talking to their bank.

The payment can go into a joint account if your child’s name is on the account. Your child must confirm on the Child Benefit claim form that the money is being used in the way they want.

Contact the Child Benefit Office if you have any questions.

You can fill in the Child Benefit claim form for a young parent, but they must check it and sign it themselves.

Part 2: If someone's ill or disabled

You can apply to manage Child Benefit for someone who can’t manage their affairs due to illness or special needs. You’ll need to become an official appointee to:

  • complete the claim form
  • collect the benefit payment
  • deal with any correspondence
  • tell the Child Benefit Office about any changes in circumstances

You can arrange with someone’s bank or the Post Office to collect their payments without becoming an appointee.

Contact the Child Benefit Office to become an appointee.
