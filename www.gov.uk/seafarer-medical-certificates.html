Quick answer Seafarer medical certificates

You must have a seafarer medical fitness certificate to work at sea. The type of certificate you need will depend on the type of vessel you’re working on and where it operates.

ENG1 medical certificate

You need an ENG1 medical certificate if you are either:

  • in charge of a ship, eg a boatmaster
  • serving on a merchant ship
  • a seafarer

An ENG 1 certificate can only be issued by a doctor who is approved by the Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA).

How to apply

You must book an appointment with an MCA-approved doctor.

You’ll need to bring to your examination:

  • photographic ID, eg your passport
  • your previous medical certificate (if you have one)
  • your local doctor’s name, address and contact details
  • any medication you’re taking
  • your glasses or contact lenses (if you wear them)

If you pass, your doctor will immediately issue you with an ENG1 certificate.

Fees

The maximum fee for an ENG 1 medical examination is £80.

ML5 medical certificate

You need an ML5 medical certificate if you are either:

  • in charge of a small commercial vessel
  • serving onboard a seagoing ship
  • the master of a passenger ship that does not go to sea (Class IV or V)
  • the master of a commercial vessel which does not go to sea
  • the master and crew of a small commercial vessel certificated for Area Category 2 to 6 (no more than 60 miles from a safe haven)
  • a crew member or anyone else (e.g. catering staff) who normally works on a domestic passenger ship (Class VI or VIA) which does not proceed to sea

An ML5 certificate can be approved by any doctor who is registered with the General Medical Council and holds a current licence to practice.

You may be allowed to work without a medical certificate for up to 3 months or until the next port of call, as long as you can get a valid certificate when you dock.

Fees

The fee for an ML5 form isn’t set by the MCA, but your doctor will probably charge a fee for completing this.

Equivalent certificates

Some seafarer medical certificates from other countries may be accepted instead of an ENG1 form.

Suspension and cancellation of a medical fitness certificate

You can have your medical certificate suspended or cancelled if:

  • there’s been a major change in your fitness
  • you don’t follow the terms of the certificate you’ve been issued with
  • the issuing doctor didn’t have full details of the your condition at the time of examination

You must hold a valid medical fitness certificate and produce this on demand to the right authorities.

You can find more information on seafarer medical certificates on the Maritime and Coastguard Agency (MCA) website.
