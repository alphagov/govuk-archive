Restore your dissolved company, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Administrative restoration

You can apply to restore your company if it was struck off the register and dissolved by the Registrar of Companies, but only if:

  • you’re a director or shareholder
  • it was trading until it was struck off the register
  • you didn’t voluntarily apply to have your company struck off

If you’re owed money by a dissolved company, or have a legal or personal injury claim, you must apply for a court order to have the company restored.

How to apply

You’ll need to send the following to Companies House:

  • form RT01 - Application for administrative restoration (PDF, 325KB)
  • a cheque for £100, payable to ‘Companies House’
  • any outstanding documents, eg late filing penalties, annual returns etc
  • if your company had assets, a waiver letter from the body representing the Crown

Call Companies House if you need advice.

What happens next

The Registrar of Companies will decide whether to restore the company. If they refuse, you may be able to:

  • apply for a court order to have it restored
  • get a discretionary grant (if you were a shareholder)

Part 2: Discretionary grants

You may be able to get back a dissolved company’s assets if you were one of its shareholders - apply for a discretionary grant.

How to apply

In England and Wales (but not Cornwall or Lancashire) you must apply to the Treasury Solicitor.

Send an application form and a statutory declaration to the Treasury Solicitor’s Office.

If the company can be restored

Download ‘Form DG2 - Application for a discretionary grant’ (DOC, 33KB)

Download ‘Form DG3 - Application for a discretionary grant’ (DOC, 33KB)
