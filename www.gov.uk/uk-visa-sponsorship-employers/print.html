UK visa sponsorship for employers, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Before you can sponsor an employee

Before you can apply for a sponsorship licence you need to know which type of licence you need.

There are different categories (or tiers) of sponsorship for employees:

  • Tier 2 - skilled workers with a job offer
  • Tier 5 - temporary workers

Tier 2 - skilled workers with a job offer

Tier 2 sponsorship can be:

  • general - for people coming to work in the UK with a skilled job offer which is National Qualifications Framework (NQF) level 6 or above.
  • intra-company transfer - for multinational companies sending their employees to the UK for jobs of NQF level 6 or above
  • sportsperson - for elite sportspeople and coaches that will be based in the UK
  • minister of religion - for people coming to work for a recognised religious organisation (for up to 3 years)

Scotland

In Scotland, a skilled job offer has to be Scottish Credit and Qualifications Framework (SCQF) level 9 or above for both general work and intra-company transfers.

Tier 5 - temporary workers

Tier 5 sponsorship can be:

  • creative and sporting - to work as a sportsperson (up to 12 months), entertainer or artist (up to 24 months)
  • charity worker - includes both paid and voluntary workers (up to 12 months)
  • religious worker - for those doing preaching, pastoral and non-pastoral work (24 months)
  • government authorised exchange - for those coming to the UK through an approved scheme (24 months)
  • international agreement - for those coming to the UK for a job that is covered under international law, eg. employees of overseas governments

Part 2: Apply for a sponsorship licence

When you apply for a sponsorship licence, you apply for an annual amount of unrestricted certificates of sponsorship. You can also apply for restricted certificates of sponsorship during the year through the Sponsorship Management System.

You can get a licence if:

  • you aren’t in breach of immigration control
  • you can meet your sponsorship duties
  • you are an organisation or recognised religion operating legally in the UK

You may have to provide documents to prove your organisation is genuine and operating legally in the UK. ‘Appendix A’ has more information about which documents are suitable.

You must send all supporting documents and fees to the UK Border Agency (UKBA).

If you’re successful the UKBA will put you on its list of registered sponsors and give you a set number of certificates of sponsorship to sponsor employees.

Sponsorship ratings

If you’re successful you’re given an A or B licence rating.

An A rating means there isn’t any evidence of abuse of the immigration system and you have support in place to meet your sponsorship duties.

A B rating means there isn’t enough support in place meet your duties or there is previous evidence of abuse of the immigration system. The UKBA will help you improve your rating.

Part 3: What sponsors must do

Sponsors must:

  • follow the code of practice for sponsoring skilled workers
  • if needed complete a resident labour market test for Tier 2 general workers
  • keep records of your employee
  • report changes in your employee’s working status to the UK Border Agency (UKBA), eg if they stop showing up for work or they resign
  • follow immigration laws

If a sponsor doesn’t follow these duties, their licence can be downgraded (from A to B status), suspended or taken away.

Employers must give their employee a certificate of sponsorship so they can apply for a visa or extension of their stay.

Reporting

If a sponsored employee thinks that their employer has given false information or isn’t carrying out their duties, they can get in touch with the Sponsor Licensing Unit.

Part 4: Certificate of sponsorship

The certificate is a virtual document with a unique reference number that lets employees apply for a visa or extension of their stay.

You must pay for each certificate of sponsorship you give but don’t get a refund if your employee does not use it.

Deadline to apply

Once a certificate of sponsorship is assigned to someone, they have 3 months to use it to apply for a visa or an extension of their stay. If they don’t apply within that time the certificate will run out and they’ll need to get a new one from their sponsor.

If a sponsored employee changes their employment

If an employee wants to change their employment (with the same or a new employer) they must reapply under the points based system.

The only exception is if they work with the same sponsor and the new job will be the same standard occupational classification as their original certificate of sponsorship.

If a sponsored person changes their mind about taking a job

If a prospective employee has a certificate of sponsorship assigned to them but decides not to take the job because they want to work for another sponsor, they should:

  • contact the original sponsor and ask them to withdraw their certificate of sponsorship
  • get a certificate from the other sponsor once the first certificate has been withdrawn

An applicant can only have one certificate assigned to them at a time.
