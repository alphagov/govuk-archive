Benefits & credits: The Warm Home Discount scheme

Notes

Part 1: Overview

For winter 2012 to 2013, you could get a £130 discount on your electricity bill through the Warm Home Discount Scheme.

The discount won’t affect your Cold Weather Payment or Winter Fuel Payment.

Not everyone gets the discount, you have to qualify.

Part 2: What you'll get

For Winter 2012 to 2013, you can get a £130 discount on your electricity bill.

The money is not paid to you, it’s a one-off discount on your electricity bill, usually between October and March.

Pre-pay or pay as you go customers

People using pre-pay or pay as you go electricity meters can also qualify for the discount.

If eligible, your electricity supplier can tell you how you’ll get the discount. For example, a voucher you can use to top up your meter.

Part 3: Eligibility

Usually, you qualify for the discount if on 21 July 2012 your supplier was part of the scheme, your name (or your partner’s) was on the bill and you were either:

  • 80 or over and getting the Guarantee Credit element of Pension Credit
  • under 80 and only getting the Guarantee Credit element of Pension Credit

You might still get the discount even if you don’t qualify.

Some suppliers offer the discount to people who wouldn’t normally qualify. They have their own rules about who else can get the discount – contact them to check their rules.

The following suppliers are part of the scheme:

  • Atlantic

Part 4: How to claim

You’ll get a letter from the Department for Work and Pensions telling you one of the following:

  • you don’t have to apply for the discount – you’ll get it automatically
  • you must apply for the discount by 31 March 2013 – the letter will tell you why and how

Letters are sent between October 2012 and January 2013. Contact Warm Front if your letter doesn’t arrive.
