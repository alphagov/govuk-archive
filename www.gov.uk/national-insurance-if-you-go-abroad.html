Quick answer National Insurance if you go abroad

Contact HMRC to find out if you need to pay National Insurance when you work abroad.

HMRC Residency helpline
Telephone: 0845 915 4811
Fax: 0845 915 7800

From overseas
Telephone: +44 (0)191 203 7010
Fax: +44 (0)191 225 7800

Working in Europe

If you go to another EEA country or Switzerland and start working for an employer there, you’ll normally pay social security contributions in that country rather than UK National Insurance contributions.

Workers temporarily posted by their UK employer to one of these states may be able to continue paying contributions to the UK instead of the state they’re posted to. Your employer will be able to tell you if you qualify and provide the necessary forms.

Working in Social Security agreement countries

If you go to a country with a Reciprocal Agreement or Double Contribution Convention (known as Social Security agreements) and start working for an employer there, you’ll usually pay social security contributions in that country rather than UK National Insurance contributions.

These countries are:

Barbados, Bermuda, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Canada, Croatia, Guernsey, Israel, Jamaica, Japan, Jersey, the former Yugoslav republic of Macedonia, Mauritius, Montenegro, New Zealand, Philippines, Republic of Korea, Serbia, Turkey, USA.

Workers temporarily posted by their UK employer to one of these states may be able to continue paying contributions to the UK instead of the state they’re posted to. Your employer will be able to tell you if you qualify and provide the necessary forms.

Voluntary contributions

If you’re eligible you can pay voluntary contributions to your National Insurance. These go towards your state pension and certain benefits and allowances if you return to the UK.

Your circumstances change

Contact HMRC if your circumstances change when you are abroad, eg if you move house or your marital status changes. You’ll need your National Insurance number.
