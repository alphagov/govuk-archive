A list of army HIVES in western England and Wales, with contact details (including phone numbers) and opening times. HIVES are information centres for forces personnel and their families.

Birmingham HIVE

Serving Birmingham, Hampstead, Selly Oak, Harborne, Edgbaston, Moseley, Dawberry Close, Bournville, King’s Heath. Information on Lichfield is also available from Birmingham HIVE.

Royal Centre for Defence Medicine (RCDM) is situated just outside the centre of Birmingham which is the second largest city in the UK and offers a diverse range of shopping, entertainment, and culture. The HIVE can provide information to permanent staff and trainees from all three services living and working in the Birmingham area.

When you arrive, RCDM will issue a staff information booklet detailing information relating to your posting, useful telephone numbers, staff training and development, equality and diversity, safety, health and safety, security, and much more together with a variety of documentation to be completed. In addition the HIVE can provide further details on unit facilities.

  • Ash House, Hamstead Campus, Friary Road, Handsworth Wood, Birmingham, B20 1AP
  • Email: birminghamhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Telephone: 0121 551 8775
  • Please see the HIVE blog for opening hours

An outreach HIVE service is provided to Lichfield HIVE.

  • JSMC Whittington, Whittington Barracks, Lichfield, WS14 9PY
  • Telephone: 01543 433 658

Chepstow HIVE

Serving Beachley Barracks, Old Coach Road, Wyvern, St Ewan’s Estates Beachley and Brecon.

The name Chepstow is derived from the Olde English words “Chepe” and “Stowe” meaning market place. The historic walled border town and ancient Port of Chepstow commands the entrance to the Lower Wye Valley in an area of outstanding natural beauty. The ruins of Chepstow Castle sit on the cliffs over the River Wye where building started the year after the Battle of Hastings in 1067 by the Normans.

Nearby Chepstow Racecourse is a well-known venue attracting events such as the Welsh Grand National. On Sundays the racecourse is the venue for a general market and monthly antique fairs. Within the local area you can find a full range of amenities including shopping, sport and places of interest. Full details are available from the HIVE.

Unit facilities

Beachley Barracks is well equipped with a large education centre, a gymnasium and a library. At the centre of the camp is the former cookhouse, now re-built and providing a multiuse facility including dining hall, NAAFI shop, Junior Ranks and NCO Mess, hairdressers, plus an IT Suite. The Wyvern Estate is home to the Little Scamps Pre-school and nursery and the Coffee Pot cafe.

  • UWO Beachley Barracks, Chepstow, Monmouthshire, NP16 7YG
  • Email: chepstowhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Telephone/fax main HIVE: 01291 645441
  • Telephone/fax serving personnel (SP) HIVE: 01291 645310
  • Please see the HIVE blog for opening hours

Hereford HIVE

Serving Hereford.

Hereford is located on the A49 between Ross-on-Wye and Shrewsbury, although a lot of people think it is located in Wales we are on the English side of the Welsh boarder and we are surrounded by beautiful countryside and farmlands. Hereford town is a bustling, up-and-coming city centre with plenty of shops and restaurants to suit everyone’s taste, and with major shopping centres such as Birmingham and Cardiff only an hour away it is an excellent location.

Hereford Garrison is split into two main areas, Credenhill and Stirling Estate, approximately 6 miles apart. Please contact the HIVE once you have an address so that the right information can be given to you prior your move.

The Unit has excellent facilities, some of which are available for use by dependants, such as the gym and the Aquatic Centre. Timetables for these are available from the HIVE. For military personnel there is the Officer’s Mess and the WOs’/SGTs’ mess, an all ranks bar open daily 9.30am to 2pm and evenings Monday to Thursday 7am to 11pm, an Army Learning Centre, Library, and hairdressers.

  • HQ Hereford Garrison, Hereford, HR4 7DD

Lichfield HIVE

Serving Lichfield, Whittington, Sutton Coldfield.

Note: Lichfield receives an outreach service from Birmingham HIVE (above.) Please use the Birmingham HIVE contact details.

Whittington Barracks is amongst the oldest military camps still in use today and is ideally situated between Lichfield city and Tamworth. Birmingham city centre is also within easy reach. The Service Family Accommodation (SFA) is all within a 10 minute walk from camp, there is also a community centre, pre-school and post office within easy reach of the married quarters. Whittington Village is also within walking distance from the estate.

Stafford HIVE

Serving MOD Stafford.

MOD Stafford is in the West Midlands to the north of Stafford in the heart of surrounding countryside. Stafford has a variety of high street stores, an indoor market, leisure centre, Ten Pin Bowling, cinema, theatre, library, Job Centre and various pubs and places to eat. Staffordshire boasts more miles of canals than any other county. There is a wide range of places to visit and events that take place in Staffordshire. The HIVE can provide you with further information.

Unit facilities

On camp, MOD Stafford has a Londis shop, a gym and sauna open to Service personnel and their dependants. Off camp the Dixon Community Centre holds regular coffee mornings and a parent and toddlers group. The Beacon Amenties Centre is off camp and is available to hire for private functions.

  • Dixon Community Centre, Tedder Road, Stafford, ST16 3RA
  • Email: staffordhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Telephone: 01785 258843

St Athan HIVE

Serving St Athan and West Camp. Also provides information cover for Haverford West.

St Athan is situated in a quiet and beautiful countryside area, with its own beach in the Vale of Glamorgan about 3 miles from Cardiff International Airport.

Llantwit Major (Llanilltud Fawr) (under 2 miles) is the closest town, it has a good range of shops, banks, doctors, dentists, schools, leisure centre and also a bus and train station and its own beach. It would be approximately a few minutes drive but a good 20 minute walk.

Barry is approximately 11 miles away and is easily accessible by road or bus and has a larger range of shops, stores, cinema and nightlife and is always developing its amenities to make it a bigger and better town to visit and there is also Barry Island to visit if you fancy a fun-packed seaside day out.

Bridgend is approximately 11 miles away and is easily accessible by road, bus or train and has a better range again compared to Barry, this is where you will find the closest shopping centre and designer outlet.

Cardiff is approximately 18 miles away and is easily accessible by road, bus or train. Bearing in mind this is a city sometimes it works out far quicker and cheaper to travel on the bus or train. There is everything in Cardiff, you can spend a day sight-seeing or a day of shopping, and there is something here for people of every age.

All the facilities on St Athan station are available to service personnel and their dependants. Facilities include a swimming pool, gym, golf club, cinema, learning centres, medical centre, hairdressers, Post Office and more.

  • WCCC, MOD St Athan, Barry, South Glamorgan, CF62 4WA
  • Email: stathanhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Telephone/fax: 01446 798 381
  • Mil: 95421 8381

Tern Hill HIVE

Serving Tern Hill, Shrewsbury and MOD Donnington.

Clive Barracks in Tern Hill is currently the home of the 1st Battalion, The Royal Irish Regiment. Facilities on camp include a Unit gym, medical and dental centres, and an Army Learning & LearnDirect Centre with free internet access. The Service families accommodation estates are within walking distance of the barracks, and include a primary school, nursery, Army and Air Cadets’ building, Unit Welfare Office.

The HIVE is located within the Unit Welfare Office. The R IRISH Community Centre is located centrally on Hedley Way, and incorporates the Coffee Pot, bar (open Friday evenings), parents and toddlersdefe groups, health visitor clinics, a children’s soft play area, youth clubs and free internet access.

  • c/o Unit Welfare Office, 3 Boscobel Road, Buntingsdale Estate, Market Drayton, Shropshire, TF9 2HG
  • Email: ternhillhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Telephone: 01630 639 806
