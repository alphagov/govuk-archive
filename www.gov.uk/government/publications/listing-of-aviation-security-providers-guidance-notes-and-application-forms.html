Policies:

Detail

The purpose of these notes is to help aviation security providers submit an application to become listed under the Aviation Security (Aviation Security Service Providers) Regulations 2006. The DfT intends, in due course, that directed parties will only be able to contract with a company/partnership providing aviation security functions where that company/partnership is listed by the DfT.

‘Part A’ - general company/partnership information

1. (i) Name of company/partnership

Companies must provide their full registered address, registered number, VAT number and telephone/fax details. Email and website addresses should also be given if available. Companies must provide details of the company directors and confirmation that directors do not have convictions for terrorist-related offences. Please name senior managers and provide information about the structure of the company (organisational charts, names of any parent companies or other names used by the company).

Partnerships must provide their full headquarters address, VAT number (if applicable) and telephone/fax details. Email and website addresses should also be given if available. Partnerships must provide the name of all partners who have a role in the management of the partnership and confirm that none have convictions for terrorist offences.

(ii) State the location of records for recruitment, vetting and training. This is usually the head office, but may vary with each company/partnership.

(iii) It is a requirement for being listed that you transfer, on request, mandatory training, recruitment and vetting records to another company/partnership should any of your staff transfer to that company/partnership.

2. Informing the DfT of changes

It will be the responsible person’s responsibility to ensure that the DfT is notified of any major changes in the operation. The DfT must be notified within 14 days if:

a) The company/partnership enters into a new contract with a directed party

b) A contract ceases

c) The company or partnership changes name or location

d) The overall responsibility is assigned to anyone other than the person named at point 3 (i)

e) There are any other changes or procedures likely to significantly impact on your listed status (eg conviction for a terrorist-related offence)

3. (i) Responsible person (E.g. senior security manager/senior partner)

This should be the person who is applying on behalf of the company/partnership and who will be assuming responsibility for making sure that the company/partnership remains compliant with the criteria for an approved listing. If the responsible person has security clearance, please state the level of security clearance and any expiry date eg

Basic Check (BC) = verified references and criminal record check;

Counter-Terrorist Check (CTC) = a BC plus a check of security service records;

Security Check (SC) = a CTC plus a credit check;

Developed Vetting (DV) = SC plus additional checks.

The responsible person must provide their name, position in the company/partnership, what security clearance they hold (if any) and full registered business address. They must also sign and date this section in the presence of a witness. NB - It is a DfT requirement that we are informed in writing within 14 days of a new responsible person taking over.

3. (ii) Witness (eg company secretary/partner)

A witness is required to countersign the application. This should be the most senior person in the structure of the organisation who has the authority to bind the application. They must sign and date at the same time and in the presence of the responsible person.

The witness must provide their name, position in the company/partnership, what security clearance they hold (if any) and their business address.

‘Part B’ - security functions by location

Complete a separate ‘Part B’ for each location.

1. Location

Please submit a separate ‘Part B’ for each location. Note that providers operating at major airports with multiple terminals must complete a separate ‘Part B’ for each terminal eg one ‘Part B’ for all functions at Gatwick North and another for Gatwick South. A separate ‘Part B’ is required for each of the following terminals:

Heathrow Terminal 1 Manchester Terminal 1

Heathrow Terminal 2 Manchester Terminal 2

Heathrow Terminal 3 Manchester Terminal 4

Heathrow Terminal 4

2. Security functions

Please list all security functions (from the list below) delivered by your company/partnership at the location, including the approximate number of staff deployed for each function. Include details of whom you are contracted to, provide the functions for and any additional information you may feel is relevant. Do not list any function that does not appear below:

a) The checking or searching of aircraft, and the controlling of access to aircraft

b) The searching and/or patrolling of the restricted zone

c) The screening or searching by hand, of passengers, other persons or vehicles entering the restricted zone

d) The screening by x-ray or other equipment or searching by hand, of hand baggage

e) The screening by x-ray or other equipment or searching by hand, of hold baggage

f) Secondary screening of passenger and cabin baggage

g) The Accounting and Authorising of Hold Baggage for Carriage by Air (Triple A);

h) Passenger questioning

i) The issuing of passes to persons, or in respect of vehicles, to access the restricted zone

Overall assessment

DfT will list the functions provided by your company/partnership at the locations quoted on this form, unless the department is satisfied that the function is not being delivered to the required standard. The required standard is that determined by the DfT in relevant directions made under Part II of the Aviation Security Act 1982 (as amended) and the National Aviation Security Programme (NASP) and is the same for both directed parties and their agents. If the function is listed once the Regulations come into effect, you will be required to maintain the required standards. Failure to do so may result in being de-listed and or the directed party being served an enforcement notice.

Notice of decision

The DfT may wish to undertake an inspection of the function as part of the assessment of this application.

Thereafter, the DfT will notify the company/partnership of whether or not, and on what day, the company/partnership was placed on the list of approved providers. Listing will expire after a period of 5 years from that day. The company/partnership can apply for a renewal listing no sooner than 2 months before the date of expiry.

If the company/partnership is not successful, the DfT will notify the applicant and state the reasons why listing was refused. The applicant will be notified of the appeals procedure and entitled to appeal and or reapply. A re-application will be considered, provided that it is submitted with an action plan stating the measures to be taken and the targets for achieving compliance.
