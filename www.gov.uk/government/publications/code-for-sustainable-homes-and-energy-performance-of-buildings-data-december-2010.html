Detail

The latest official statistics on the Code for sustainable homes and Average Energy Efficiency (SAP ratings) were released on 22 February 2011.

Statistics in this release relating to the Code for sustainable homes show the number of dwellings that have been certified to the standards set out in the Code Technical Guide.

Key points from the latest release are:

  • There were 18,339 post construction stage certificates and 46,519 design stage certificates issued up to and including December 2010.
  • 11 per cent of homes with post construction certificates and 21 per cent of those with design stage certificates have been built for the private sector. 89 per cent of homes with post construction certificates and 79 per cent of those with design stage certificates have been built for the public sector.
  • Between April 2007 and December 2010, 40,017 dwellings at the design stage received a three star rating and 289 dwellings received a six star rating.
  • Between April 2007 and December 2010, 16,513 dwellings at post-construction stage received a three star rating and 30 dwellings received a six star rating.
  • The majority of the certificates issued since April 2007 at design stage (86 per cent) and at post construction stage (90 per cent) have been awarded at Code level 3.
  • The average energy efficiency, SAP rating of new homes in England was 81.7 and in Wales 80.6 for the quarter October - December 2010. This is an increase on the same quarter 2009 from 80.2 in England and 79.3 in Wales.

Revision

A number of errors were contained within these published statistics.

These errors related to the number of Code Certificates (at both Design and Post-Construction stage) issued by 22 of the 25 Welsh Planning Authorities. Although these errors affected the number of cases for individual Planning Authorities, it did not affect the overall total of cases in Wales. The statistics for England and Northern Ireland remain unchanged. The Cumulative and Quarterly Data commentary (.PDF and Word documents) are therefore unchanged as these relate to national statistics which remain the same.

We apologise for the error which was caused by an anomaly in the transfer of data which has now been rectified. The document ‘Table of Welsh statistics changes’ below sets out the data as originally published and as amended.

The published quarterly statistics have now been amended with the changes being denoted by an ‘R’ in the appropriate column. These were published 26 August 2011.
