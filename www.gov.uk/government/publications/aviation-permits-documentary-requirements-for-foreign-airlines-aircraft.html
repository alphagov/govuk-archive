Guidance Aviation permits - documentary requirements for foreign airlines/aircraft

Organisations:
Department
for Transport
Published:
8 September 2005
Policy:
Making sure UK airports and airlines are safe, secure and competitive while reducing their impacts on the environment and communities

The documentary evidence foreign registered airlines/aircraft need to provide when requesting an operating permit to operate to/from the UK.

Detail

Documentary requirements for foreign registered airlines and aircraft applying to operate to/from the United Kingdom:

  • Certificate of competency and/or Air Operators Certificate and /or an Operating Licence issued by the aeronautical authority of the State of Operator.
  • Certificates of Airworthiness and Registration issued by the aeronautical authority of the State of Registry for all aircraft to be used on services to the UK.
  • Noise Certificates issued by the aeronautical authority of the State of Registry for all aircraft to be used on services to the UK. Or a copy of a noise exemption obtained from the Economic Regulation Group of the UK Civil Aviation Authority (CAA). (All aircraft operating scheduled/charter passenger/cargo services must be Chapter 3 compliant).
  • Certificate of Liability Insurance for passenger and third party risks for aircraft to be used. The level of insurance must meet the minimum requirements of insurance for passenger and third party liability set out in Articles 6 and 7 of EC Regulation No.785/2004.
  • Confirmation that aircraft is fitted with an Annex 6 compliant Enhanced Ground Proximity Warning System (EGPWS).
  • Confirmation that no dangerous goods or munitions of war will be carried (if so, provide letter of exemption issued by UK CAA dangerous Goods Office).
  • Confirmation that an Airborne Collision Avoidance System (ACAS II or TCAS Version 7) is fitted (if not, provide exemption statement by the airline’s State aeronautical authorities).
  • A statement that the airline’s crews are aware of the UK Approach Ban requirements as set out in Articles 108(6) of the UK Air Navigation Order 2009, and that flight crews will be issued with written instructions with regard to Articles 108(6) prior to operating the flights into the UK. (See Note 1 below.)
  • A statement that the airline’s flight crew’s Non Precision and Category 1 Aerodrome Operating Minima comply with Article 108(3) of the Air Navigation Order 2009. (See Note 2 below).
  • Advance Passenger Information (API). An e-Borders Certificate issued by the UK Border Force confirming that arrangements are in place for the transmission of API data in accordance with paragraphs 27 & 27B of schedule 2 to the Immigration Act 1971 and the Immigration and Police (Passenger, Crew and Service Information) Order 2008 (SI 2008/5). Further information about the requirements for certification can be obtained from e-borderscarriers@homeoffice.gsi.gov.uk.

Leased aircraft

The department will also require the above documents in regard to any foreign registered aircraft leased from another carrier, either with or without crew, which the applicant carrier proposes to use on services to the UK. The department will also require the following additional information:

(a) Written confirmation that the lease has been approved by the lessee’s aeronautical authorities;

(b) A copy of the lease agreement;

(c) Confirmation of which airline’s operations and flight manuals will be used.

Note 1

Article 108(3) states that a foreign carrier’s minima must be no less restrictive than either:

(a) minima calculated in accordance with the notified method, or

(b) minima which comply with the law of the country in which the aircraft is registered, whichever are the more restrictive.

The notified method for calculating UK minima is set out in the UK Aeronautical Information Publication (UKAIP), Section AD 1-1.

Note 2

Article 108(6) of the Air Navigation Order 2009, states that an aircraft making a descent to an aerodrome shall not descend from a height of 1000 feet above the aerodrome if the relevant runway visual range at the aerodrome is at the time less than the specified minimum for landing. In addition the aircraft referred to above when making a descent to an aerodrome shall not:

(a) continue an approach to landing at any aerodrome by flying below the relevant specified height, or

(b) descend below the relevant specified minimum descent height, unless in either case from such height the specified visual reference for landing is established and is maintained.

“Specified” in this context means specified by the operator in the aerodrome operating minima made available to the flight crew in pursuant to paragraph 2 of Article 108 of the Air Navigation Order 2009.
