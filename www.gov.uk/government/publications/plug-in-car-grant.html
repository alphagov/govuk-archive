Guidance Plug-in Car Grant

Organisations:
  • Department
    for Transport
Published:
25 February 2010
Policy:
Reducing greenhouse gases and other emissions from transport

Grant funding to support private and business buyers looking to purchase a qualifying ultra-low emission vehicle.

Detail

Since January 2011, motorists purchasing a qualifying ultra-low emission car have been able to receive a grant of 25% towards the cost of the vehicle, up to a maximum of £5,000. The 2010 Spending Review confirmed that government has made provision to support the Plug-in Car Grant for the life of this Parliament.

Both private consumers and businesses can benefit from the Plug-in Car Grant when purchasing a qualifying ultra-low emission car and registering it in the UK. The grant is only available on first registrations, with registration defined as falling under the Vehicle Excise and Registration Act 1994. In addition, the grant can only be administered for a specific vehicle once.

The government is adopting a technology neutral approach to reducing emissions from transport. This means that all cars that meet the criteria set out below are potentially eligible for the Grant. This includes, but is not limited to, electric, plug-in hybrid and hydrogen-fuelled cars. However, consumers will find that hydrogen vehicles are, as yet, less available on the open market than electric and plug-in hybrid options.

The Plug-in Car Grant has been designed to help make the whole-life costs of a qualifying car more comparable with petrol or diesel equivalents. Over time, as manufacturers begin to make these cars in greater volumes, the costs of production should begin to fall. This will help to make an ultra-low carbon car a realistic option for anyone looking to buy a car.

The Grant is not designed to support research and development projects: any funding for this would be delivered by the Technology Strategy Board.

The cars eligible for the Plug-in Car Grant are:

  • Chevrolet Volt
  • Citroen CZero
  • Mia
  • Mitsubishi i-MiEV
  • Nissan Leaf
  • Peugeot iOn
  • Renault Fluence ZE
  • Smart fortwo electric drive
  • Toyota Prius Plug-in Hybrid
  • Vauxhall Ampera

As of 30 September 2012, 2,311 claims have been made through the Plug-in Car Grant scheme.

1. Car eligibility

To be eligible for the Grant, applicants need to demonstrate that their completed vehicle (chassis and bodywork) has type approval or Individual Vehicle Approval and meets the Grant criteria. These criteria have been set to ensure that vehicles meet an agreed level of performance and safety.

2. Multi-stage builds

To qualify for the Plug-In Car Grant any multi-stage built M1 vehicle will need to have been approved as a completed vehicle (e.g. comprising both chassis and bodywork): the accepted evidence listed above must cover the vehicle in its finished condition. An approval for a vehicle that has not been completed will not be accepted. This is to ensure vehicles driven off the forecourt when bought by the public meet the Plug-In Car Grant criteria.

Manufacturers will need to consult the Vehicle Certification Agency (VCA) to obtain completed vehicle approval

  • Type approval for goods vehicles
  • European Community whole vehicle type approval (ECWVTA)

To make an enquiry about applying for type approval:

  • email enquiries@vca.gov.uk
  • telephone 0117 952 4164

3. Criteria

3.1. Vehicle type: Only new cars are eligible (vehicle category ‘M1′). This includes pre-registration conversions (normal, internal combustion engine cars that were converted to battery or hybrid versions by specialist convertors before the car’s first registration). Motorbikes and quadricycles are not covered.

3.2. Crash Test: These vehicles must either have EC whole vehicle type approval (EC WVTA) Or if Small series approval is granted, evidence must be supplied to show that the car has appropriate levels of safety, as judged by international standards.

3.3. Carbon dioxide exhaust emissions: Vehicles must emit less than 75 grams of carbon dioxide (CO2) per kilometre driven.

3.4. Range: Electric vehicles (EVs) must be able to travel a minimum of 70 miles between charges. Plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) must have a minimum electric range of 10 miles.

3.5. Minimum top speed: Vehicles must be able to reach a speed of 60 miles per hour or more.

3.6. Warranty: Vehicles must have a 3-year or 60,000-miles vehicle warranty (guarantee) in addition to a 3-year battery and electric drive train warranty, with the option of extending the battery warranty for an extra 2 years. Where, ‘Drive train’ means the parts that send power from the engine to the wheels. These include the clutch, transmission (gear box), drive shafts, U-joints and differential.

3.7. Battery performance: Vehicles must have either a minimum 5-year warranty on the battery and electric drive train as standard or extra evidence of battery performance to show reasonable performance after 3 years of use

3.8. Electrical safety: Vehicles must comply with certain regulations (UN-ECE Reg 100.00) that show that they are electrically safe.

4. Frequently asked questions

i. I am thinking about buying a qualifying car - how do I apply for the Grant?

If you are looking to use the Plug-in Car Grant to purchase an eligible car, the good news is that there are no application forms to fill in. The dealership or vendor you are buying your car from will complete all the paperwork on your behalf as you buy the car; and the amount of your Grant will be automatically deducted from the price of your car at the point of purchase.

Before you purchase your car, the dealer will ask you a few questions about your decision to purchase an ultra-low emission vehicle. This data will help inform policy decisions about ultra-low emission cars and supporting infrastructure.

ii. How do I know which vehicles are eligible for the Grant?

The list above shows which vehicles are eligible and this will be updated when more cars enter the scheme. These vehicles meet all the criteria.

iii. I am a vehicle manufacturer looking to see whether a model is eligible for subsidy under the terms of the scheme. How do I apply for Plug-in Car Grant eligibility?

Vehicle manufacturers who want to apply for their vehicle model to be eligible for subsidy under the scheme will need to make a formal application. To do this, you will require an application pack and associated guidance. You can request this from olev.enquiries@olev.gsi.gov.uk.

The guidance document will give you more detail about how to provide evidence that you meet each criterion, including examples of acceptable evidence.

iv. I am a vehicle importer looking to import and sell eligible vehicles in the UK. How do I apply for Plug-in Car Grant eligibility?

Importers also need to demonstrate the vehicles they will import and sell meet the Grant criteria. To do this, you will require an application pack and associated guidance. You can request this from olev.enquiries@olev.gsi.gov.uk. For example, you will need to submit information on the measures you have taken to mitigate electrical safety risks and the warranty that you will provide with the vehicles that you intend to sell.

v. What about vehicles that have been converted?

The Grant is for new cars and vans at first registration in the UK. Pre-registration conversions are eligible to be submitted for the scheme however, post-registration conversions are not.
