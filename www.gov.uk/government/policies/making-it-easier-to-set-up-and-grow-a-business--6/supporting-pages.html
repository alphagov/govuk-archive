Encouraging people and giving them the skills to set up their own business

Engaging with young people

Evidence shows we need to engage with young people early on through the education system and to give them hands-on business experiences to encourage them to see starting their own business as a career option in later life.

What we are doing

We’re recruiting young entrepreneurs to volunteer to go into schools to talk about running their own businesses and how they got there. This is part of our ‘Inspiring the future’ initiative. We’re aiming to get 2,500 enterprise champions. Find out more about Inspiring the future.

We’re supporting every school to develop and run its own business through the ‘Enterprise Village’. The Village supports teachers to set up and develop a school-based business.

We’re providing funding to develop the ‘Premier League Enterprise Academy’ model, enabling football clubs to develop enterprise in young people, mainly in deprived areas.

We’re supporting the development of ‘Tenner’ (a programme providing schoolchildren with £10 to fund a business idea and earn money) to ensure all schoolchildren have an opportunity to take part in Tenner at some point in their school life. This programme has now been transferred and will be run by Young Enterprise.

We’re funding the development of student-led enterprise societies and ensuring their support is accessible to all students in universities and most colleges by 2015. Societies encourage students to become more business minded. They work with local enterprise to get loans for student enterprises and give support to students launching their start-ups whilst still at university / college.

Promoting enterprise

We are raising the profile of enterprise and entrepreneurship in the UK by funding awards and special events, including:

  • the Queen’s Awards for Enterprise - these are the most prestigious awards for businesses and individuals in the UK, with three Awards for Business in the categories of Innovation, International Trade and Sustainable Development, and an award for Individuals called ‘The Queen’s Award for Enterprise Promotion’
  • Enterprising Britain, an annual competition run and funded by BIS to find the most enterprising place in the UK; the organisation that best promotes enterprise; and the organisation that best encourages busineses to export
  • Global Entrepreneurship Week (GEW), an annual event run by the Prince’s Trust and Youth Business International to help people to learn more about the range of support available to entrepreneurs in the UK

Encouraging entrepreneurship in specific areas

We’ve introduced 24 Enterprise Zones across England to generate businesses and jobs, helping to drive local and national growth. Working with other government departments, we’ve put in place a range of measures to support the development of Enterprise Zones and provide them with the incentives they need to make them attractive places to do business.

Helping unemployed people to set up a business

We offer help for unemployed people who want to start a business through the New Enterprise Allowance (NEA), administered by the Department for Work and Pensions.

The NEA is available to people aged 18 and over and claiming Jobseeker’s Allowance. It provides access to business mentoring and offers financial support of around £2,000. Our goal is for the NEA to help up to 40,000 unemployed people start up a business by 2013. [Find out more about the New Enterprise Allowance].

Helping ex-service personnel to set up a business

We support ‘Be the Boss’, a £5 million scheme to help ex-Service personnel to set up and grow businesses.

Be the Boss is funded by a grant from the Department of Business, Innovation and Skills and administered by the Royal British Legion and their partners. It provides funding, training and mentoring support to people leaving the services on how to run a successful small business.
