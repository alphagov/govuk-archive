Issue

There is a threat to the UK, British nationals and our interests abroad from terrorism.

The vast majority of these threats come from outside the UK and cannot be resolved by concentrating efforts in the UK alone. The government is addressing these threats at their source overseas before they reach the UK.

Actions

The government’s Counter Terrorism Strategy, CONTEST, reduces the risk from international terrorism so that people can go about their daily lives freely and with confidence.

It is based on 4 main actions:

  • prevent: to stop people from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism
  • pursue: to stop terrorist attacks
  • protect: to strengthen our protection against terrorist attack
  • prepare: where an attack cannot be stopped, to mitigate its impact

Prevent

We aim to stop people becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism in the UK and abroad.

We do this by:

  • responding to the ideological challenge of terrorism and the threat we face from those who promote it
  • preventing people from being drawn into terrorism and ensuring that they are given appropriate advice and support
  • working with sectors and institutions where there are risks of radicalisation which we need to address

You can find the latest information on the Prevent strategy on the Home Office website. This includes the full text of the strategy and the outcomes of the Prevent review.

Pursue

The Pursue strand of the strategy means working to detect and investigate terrorist networks and disrupt their activities.

We do this by:

  • improving our ability to identify and understand the terrorist threat
  • disrupting terrorist activity and taking action to frustrate terrorist attacks
  • bringing terrorists to justice through prosecution – this includes strengthening the legal framework against terrorism
  • developing international co-operation with partners and allies to strengthen our ability to work together to disrupt terrorist networks, for instance by using Deportation with Assurances

Protect

The Protect strand means seeking to reduce the vulnerability of the UK and its interests overseas from terrorist attack.

We do this by:

  • providing accurate FCO travel advice on the threat from domestic and international terrorism, with advice for British companies abroad
  • supporting key foreign governments to develop their ability to protect areas such as aviation and maritime security, energy infrastructure, crowded places and ‘soft’ targets

Prepare

The Prepare strand means working to mitigate the impact of a terrorist attack where it cannot be stopped. This includes work to manage an ongoing attack and recover from its aftermath.

We do this by:

  • preparing the FCO network to respond to terrorist attacks
  • supporting foreign governments to develop their own ability to respond to terrorism

Advances in technology by both terrorists and organised crime groups create challenges to our national security. The UK’s Science and Technology Strategy for Countering International Terrorism uses innovation, science and technology to reduce the risk to the UK and its interests overseas from international terrorism.

Background

Al Qaeda is weaker than at any time since 9/11. It has played no role in recent political change in North Africa and the Middle East. Its ideology has been widely discredited and it has failed in all its objectives.

Continued international pressure has further reduced its capability and the UK is working with other countries to weaken terrorist threats.

However, al Qaeda continues to be a significant threat and other terrorist groups, some affiliated to al Qaeda, have become stronger.

The Home Office is responsible for keeping the UK safe from the threat posed by terrorism.

The determination of the government and everyone involved in the 2012 Olympic and Paralympic Games was to create a safe and secure event that the UK and the world could enjoy. We thoroughly tested our plans to see how government departments, police and emergency services could respond to a range of incidents.

The games’ safety and security strategy has helped communities become more resilient to crime and the threat from violent extremism, and relationships between police and other security agencies have improved.

In the event of a terrorist incident, the Office for Security and Counter-Terrorism is responsible for activating and co-ordinating the Home Office response. We provide a crisis response 24 hours a day, 365 days of the year. The government’s response to all kinds of emergencies, including terrorism, is set out in the Central government arrangements for responding to an emergency: concept of operations.
