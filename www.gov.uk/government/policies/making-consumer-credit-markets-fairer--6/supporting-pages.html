Consumer credit markets

High cost credit (sometimes known as short-term credit)

The government is making high cost credit (sometimes known as short-term credit) easier for consumers to understand.

We’ve negotiated with the four trade associations representing 90% of payday loan companies in the UK. They agreed that by 26 November 2012 they would:

  • publish how their loans work and the costs involved
  • freeze charges and interest for customers in financial difficulty
  • run credit checks on customers to make sure they can afford a loan

The trade associations have also agreed to monitor their members to identify and deal with bad practice in the industry.

Additionally, ministers have confirmed the Financial Conduct Authority’s power to cap the total cost of credit or length of any loan if necessary.

We’ve also asked the University of Bristol Personal Finance Research Centre to carry out research into the impact on consumers and business of introducing a cap on the total cost of credit. They published a progress report in March 2012, with a full report expected early in 2013.

Mainstream credit

We’ve agreed measures with the banks to make it easier for consumers to manage their overdrafts and avoid going overdrawn if they don’t want to. These measures mean customers can get:

  • an email, text or other alert when their balance falls to a certain level (already in place)
  • accounts that don’t charge for going overdrawn by a small amount (in place by April 2014)
  • a text or other alert telling them when they should pay money into their account to avoid charges (in place by April 2014)

We’ve agreed some new rules on store cards with credit companies. These new rules should mean consumers are better protected. They include:

  • stopping sales staff getting commission when a customer takes out a store card
  • better training for staff
  • a 7-day ban on in-store discounts or other incentives for taking out a store card - this should stop people being encouraged to take out credit by an instant discount or free gift

Debt problems

We’re not able to offer individual consumers or businesses debt advice.

If you’re in debt, please contact the Money Advice Service. The website contains useful information about managing debt. It also has a list of organisations that provide free debt advice.

For advice about bankruptcy or the threat of bankruptcy, please speak to the Insolvency Service.

Illegal money lending

Our illegal money lending teams operate locally and nationally. Their main job is to defend consumers against illegal money lenders, often known as loan sharks.

The teams are funded and co-ordinated by the National Trading Standards Board.

To report a loan shark, call 0300 555 222 or text LOAN SHARK to 60003 or email the Stop Loan Sharks Team on reportaloanshark@stoploansharks.gov.uk
