The issue

Certain parts of the country are too dependent on the public sector for employment. The government wants to increase the number of lasting, private sector jobs - by supporting the private sector to expand and ensuring that government cash delivers jobs.

Actions

We’re funding large projects that have the potential to create or protect long-term jobs in the private sector, and programmes where partner organisations choose smaller projects to support, which will deliver sustainable jobs.

Regional Growth Fund

We’re doing this through the Regional Growth Fund (RGF). We have a total of £2.46 billion to give to businesses by 2016 to spend on projects such as capital investment, research and development, or training. (Please note: businesses cannot currently apply to the fund.)

The projects we’ve chosen must attract private funding to match the public funding we provide, and the projects that programmes choose to support must also include investment of private money. We provide grants, loans and loan guarantees.

RGF is a competitive fund – this means that in order to get our support, businesses must apply during a bidding round explaining how much money they need and what they will use it for.

Bids are then considered by an expert panel who recommend to ministers which bids to support. Ministers then choose the bids that they believe will give the best value for money to the taxpayer.

This is a cross-government fund and is jointly administered by the Department for Business, Innovation and Skills and the Department for Communities and Local Government.

For more detail, see Understanding the Regional Growth Fund

Background

The money was allocated in 3 rounds.

Round 1, April 2011

£450 million was allocated to 50 bids, attracting £2.5 billion in private sector investment. The projects will create or safeguard over 27,000 direct jobs, with around 100,000 more in companies that are suppliers.

Round 2, October 2011

£950 million was invested in 126 bids, attracting nearly £6 billion of private investment. Around 200,000 jobs will be created or protected: around 37,000 direct jobs, with more than 164,000 in companies that are suppliers.

Round 3: October 2012

£1.055 billion was allocated to 130 bids, attracting £6 billion of private investment. The projects will create or safeguard 240,000 jobs.

Process

Organisations had to apply for a minimum of £1 million funding. Projects were assessed on:

  • location
  • value for money for taxpayers
  • ability to create jobs in the private sector
  • project management expertise
  • state aid rules

Projects had to comply with the EU’s rules on state aid to make sure any funding did not give a company an unfair competitive advantage.
