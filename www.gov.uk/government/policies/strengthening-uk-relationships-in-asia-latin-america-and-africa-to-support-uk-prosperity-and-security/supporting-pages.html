Prosperity Fund programme

The Prosperity Fund was launched in April 2011 and in its first year supported 248 projects across a network of 14 countries and regions at a cost of £19.4 million. Its remit is to stop dangerous climate change, strengthen energy security and promote an open global economy in the main emerging economies.

For the financial year 2012 to 2013, the programme has an allocation of £19 million devolved to 15 countries and regions. Countries with dedicated programmes are: Brazil, China, Colombia, India, Indonesia, Japan, Mexico, Russia, South Africa, South Korea and Turkey. We also operate regional funds in the Gulf, Latin America and South East Asia to widen our global reach, and work with the International Energy Agency and the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development through our delegation in Paris.

It is likely that funds will also be available for 2013 to 2014 and 2014 to 2015, although the exact amount of money allocated to each region will not be known until nearer the time. As your first point of contact, please contact your nearest British Embassy or High Commission.
