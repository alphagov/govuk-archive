Supporting detail:

Tenant empowerment programme

We’re giving all social housing tenants the power to ensure their landlord provides the services they want and support and advice for tenants.

We’re spending £2 million every year between 2011 and 2015 on the tenant empowerment programme. This will support tenants to:

  • set up tenant panels
  • challenge their landlords to provide a better service
  • take over the management of local housing services such as repairs and estate management, through the Right to Manage

Following the abolition of the Tenant Services Authority, the Department for Communities and Local Government took responsibility for running the tenant empowerment programme on 1 April 2012. We’re considering longer-term options for the programme.

Tenant panels

We’re supporting the formation of tenant panels as a way to give tenants a bigger say over how local services are run, for instance by scrutinising landlord performance and pressing for improvement. The Localism Act 2011 gives tenant panels new powers to solve disputes at a local level.

On 28 August 2012, we announced a £1.3 million competition for charities, benevolent societies and philanthropic institutions to fund a national programme giving social tenants the skills and confidence to hold landlords to account. The closing date for applications was 8 October 2012.

We have supported the National Tenant Organisations to produce a guide to setting up tenant panels.

We’re also supporting the National Communities Resource Centre at Trafford Hall to provide residential training to tenants to take part in panels.

Right to Manage

Local council tenants have the right to take over the management of local housing services. The Housing (Right to Manage) (England) Regulations 2008, together with statutory guidance, show the procedures for a tenant management organisation to enter into a management agreement with a local housing authority.

We’re making it easier for tenants to take up their Right to Manage. So far, around 200 tenant management organisations have successfully taken responsibility for providing services such as repairs and estate management for 70,000 homes.

Following consultation, on 6 April 2012 we streamlined the Right to Manage regulations to make it even easier for tenants to take up their Right to Manage.

The Right to Manage grant and assessment processes are also being streamlined. For more information on the process, including copies of the application forms you need to get started, please email: tenantempowerment@communities.gsi.gov.uk

Community cashback

The £1.3 million competition we launched on 28 August 2012 included a new community cashback grant programme. This encourages tenants to take control of small-scale local services, like cutting the grass or decorating in communal areas. Tenants can reinvest any savings they make from running these services into other community priorities.

Tenant cashback

The tenant cashback scheme allows social tenants to take control of the repairs budgets for their homes, for example to carry out their own DIY, or commission it locally and pocket any savings made.

The scheme is supported through a provision in the Tenant Involvement and Empowerment Standard.

Pilot schemes, such as those run by Bromford Group and Home Group have trialled different tenant cashback models.
