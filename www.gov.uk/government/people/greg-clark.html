Biography

Greg Clark was appointed Financial Secretary to the Treasury on 4 September 2012. He has been the Conservative MP for Royal Tunbridge Wells since 2005.

Education

Born in Middlesbrough, Greg studied economics at Cambridge before earning his PhD from the London School of Economics.

Political career

Greg was Director of Policy for the Conservative Party from March 2001 for three successive Leaders; William Hague, Iain Duncan Smith and Michael Howard, before being elected as the Member of Parliament for Tunbridge Wells in 2005.

Greg’s previous position was Minister of State at the Department for Communities and Local Government (CLG), with responsibilities for decentralisation and planning policy. He was appointed as Shadow Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change in October 2008, having previously been Shadow Minister for Charities, Social Enterprises and Volunteering.

On 9 June 2010 Greg Clark was appointed to the Privy Council. He is also Minister for Cities, a position he has held since July 2011.

Career outside politics

First working as a business consultant, Greg was a special advisor to the Secretary of State for Trade and Industry Ian Lang between 1996 and 1997. After that he was the BBC’s Controller for Commercial Policy. Between 2002 and 2005 he was also a councillor on Westminster City Council, serving as Cabinet Member for Leisure and Lifelong Learning.

Personal life

Greg and his wife have 3 children and live in Royal Tunbridge Wells.

Financial Secretary to the Treasury

Responsibilities include financial services policy and overall strategic relationship with UK financial services, including:

  • banking and financial services reform and regulation (at home and in the EU)
  • financial stability
  • City competitiveness
  • bank lending and access to finance
  • the Financial Services Authority (FSA)

The Secretary also oversees the EU budget, the Cities policy (supported by the Department for Communities and Local Government (CLG), The Department for Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS) and the Cabinet office) and provides support to the Chancellor on EU and wider international finance issues.
