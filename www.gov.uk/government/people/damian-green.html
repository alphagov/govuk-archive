Biography

Damian Green is Minister of State for Police and Criminal Justice. He is the Conservative MP for Ashford.

Education

Damian was educated at Reading School and Balliol College, Oxford. He was President of the Oxford Union in 1977.

Career

From 1978 to 1992 he worked for the BBC and The Times, as well as Channel 4, where he was a producer and presenter specialising in business programmes. In 1992 he joined John Major’s policy unit, where he worked for 2 years.

Political career

Damian held a number of positions in the Shadow Cabinet, including Shadow Secretary of State for Education and Skills from 2001 to 2003, Shadow Secretary of State for Transport between 2003 and 2004 and Shadow Minister for Immigration from 2005 to 2010.

He was Minister of State for Immigration from May 2010 to September 2012.

Personal life

Damian is married with 2 children.

Home Office

Minister of State for Policing and Criminal Justice

The minister is responsible for:

  • crime and justice policy
  • police reform (includes police and crime commissioners and the strategic policing requirement)
  • police funding
  • policy pay and pensions
  • police productivity
  • police bureaucracy
  • neighbourhood policing, police community support officers, special constables; and community safety partnerships
  • police transparency and accountability
  • Independent Police Complaints Commission and HM Inspectorate of Constabulary
  • National Policing Improvement Agency phase-out (including Police ICT company)
  • police professionalism; equality and diversity
  • College of Policing
  • public order (including firearms and football policing)
  • roads policing
  • international policing
  • business partnering and value for money (including emergency services mobile communications programme)
  • shadow organised crime
  • shadow National Crime Agency
  • police senior appointments and leadership
  • police stakeholders: Association of Police Authorities, Association of Chief Police Officers, Police Superintendents’ Association of England and Wales, Police Federation of England and Wales, police chief officers, Metropolitan Police Commissioner, police staff support groups, Unison, Public and Commercial Services Union
