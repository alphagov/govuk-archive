Biography

Cheryl Gillan was appointed Secretary of State for Wales in May 2010. She is the MP for Chesham and Amersham.

She was educated at Cheltenham Ladies College and the College of Law. After leaving university she joined International Management Group before working as Director of British Film Year, a Senior Marketing Consultant at Ernst and Young and Marketing Director for Kidsons Impey.

She was Parliamentary Under-Secretary of State for the Department of Education and Employment in the last Conservative government and held a number of positions in the Shadow Cabinet including Shadow Minister for Home Affairs and Shadow Secretary of State for Wales.

Secretary of State for Wales

The Secretary of State is responsible for overall strategic direction of the Wales Office.

Responsibilities include:

  • assembly liaison
  • constitutional issues
  • major acts of Parliament (such as Government of Wales Act)
  • elections (assembly, parliamentary and local)
  • referendum
  • economy, finance and business
  • foreign affairs including interaction with the EU
  • public appointments
  • environment and energy
  • Welsh language and royal matters
  • defence
