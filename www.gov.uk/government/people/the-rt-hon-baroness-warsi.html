Biography

Rt. Hon Baroness Warsi PC was appointed Senior Minister of State at the Foreign and Commonwealth Office and Minister for Faith and Communities at the Department for Communities and Local Government in September 2012. She was previously Chairman of the Conservative Party and Minister without Portfolio.

Education

Baroness Warsi was educated at Birkdale High School and Dewsbury College before reading law (LLB) at the University of Leeds.

Political career

Baroness Warsi has been politically active since her college days. She was instrumental in the launch of Operation Black Vote in West Yorkshire in 1996 and stood as a candidate in the 2005 General Election. She became Conservative Party Vice Chairman for Cities and Diversity and was appointed to the House of Lords in 2007 becoming Shadow Minister for Communities and Social Action in the Shadow Cabinet.

Baroness Warsi is a leading voice on faith, foreign affairs, racial justice, human rights, integration, and social cohesion.

In September 2010 she made a speech at the Anglican Bishops’ Conference pledging that the Coalition Government would ‘do God’. In June 2011 she represented the United Kingdom and spoke at the OIC Foreign Ministers’ Conference in Astana, Kazakhstan. In February 2012 she led the UK’s largest ministerial delegation to the Holy See, speaking at the Pontifical Ecclesiastical Academy. She has also made keynote speeches on anti-Muslim hatred and anti-Semitism.

Baroness Warsi has been involved in voluntary, charitable and community work all her life and pioneered the Conservative Party’s Social Action agenda, including launching Project Maja in Bosnia and Bangladesh.

Career outside politics

Baroness Warsi trained with the Crown Prosecution Service and the Home Office Immigration Department, before setting up her own specialist legal practice.
