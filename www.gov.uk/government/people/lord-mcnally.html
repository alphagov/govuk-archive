Lord McNally

  • Deputy Leader of the House of Lords

Biography

Lord McNally was appointed Minister of State for Justice in May 2010. He is also Deputy Leader of the House of Lords. Lord McNally is a Liberal Democrat member of the House of Lords.

Education

He was educated at St. Joseph’s College, Blackpool and University College London where he studied economics and social history.

Career

He worked for the Labour Party for a number of years and was appointed Head of the Political Office in 10 Downing Street in 1976. He supported the successful merger of the SDP with the Liberal Party to form the Liberal Democrats and served on the federal executive of the new party.

He was elected as the Liberal Democrat Leader of the House of Lords in October 2004 and was the Liberal Democrat Spokesperson for Constitutional Affairs between 2006 and 2010.

Personal life

He is married with 3 children.

Minister of State

The minister is responsible for the business of the Ministry of Justice in the House of Lords. Other responsibilities include:

  • civil liberties
  • Freedom of Information, data protection and data sharing
  • legislation and law reform
  • public law and public legal issues
  • Crown dependencies
  • delegated royal, church and hereditary issues and Lord Lieutenants issues
  • Land Registry, National Archives and Law Commission

Deputy Leader of the House of Lords

The Deputy Leader of the House of Lords supports the House of Lords in it’s job of questioning Government ministers, improving legislation and debating topics of national significance.
