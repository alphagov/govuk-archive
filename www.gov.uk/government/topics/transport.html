Decision letter - Greater Manchester (Light Rapid Transit System) published 21 November 2012 10:14 Publication

Safe and dependable transport is essential to UK society and the economy. The government is working to make rail, road, air and water transport more efficient and effective, keep them safe and secure, and reduce greenhouse gas and other emissions.

Policies

  • Developing a new high speed rail network

    The government will develop a new high speed rail network to provide the capacity that Britain needs to compete and grow.

  • Expanding and improving the rail network

    The government is expanding and improving the rail network to improve passengers' journeys and support economic growth

  • Improving local transport

    The government is giving councils more freedom to improve their local transport systems to make them more efficient and sustainable

  • Reducing greenhouse gases and other emissions from transport

    The government is taking steps to reduce carbon emissions from all forms of transport in the UK

  • Sustaining a thriving maritime sector

    Enabling the maritime sector to grow efficiently to support economic growth.

  • Making roads safer

    Reducing road collisions through better driver training, tougher drink/drug driving charges, road safety campaigns and lowering speed limits

  • Providing effective regulation of freight transport

    Fair and effective regulation will help shift freight from road to rail and water, moving it safely and securely across the UK and abroad

  • Managing, improving and investing in the road network

    We're making roads clearer by giving local councils the power to remove road signage clutter, enforce parking controls and manage street works

  • Making sure UK airports and airlines are safe, secure and competitive while reducing their impacts on the environment and communities

    Creating economic, sustainable and socially responsible growth through competitive airlines and airports

  • Making transport more accessible to all

    To make sure the transport network lets people get around independently

  • Managing the risk to transport networks from terrorism and other crimes

    Preventing terrorist attacks and managing emergencies on our road, rail, air and sea transport systems by having the correct security measures and well organised response plans in place
