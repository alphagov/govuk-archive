Part 2: Register the death

If the death has been reported to a coroner you can’t register the death until the coroner gives permission.

Who can register the death

You can register the death if you’re:

  • a relative
  • someone who was present at the death
  • the person making the funeral arrangements
  • the person who found the body

Where to register the death

You should register the death at a register office in the area where the person died. You can find the correct register office online.

You need to take the medical certificate with you.

You need to tell the registrar the person’s:

  • full name (including any maiden name)
  • date and place of birth (town and county if born in the UK and country if born abroad)
  • last address
  • occupation

You also need the full name, date of birth and occupation of a surviving spouse.

Tell the registrar if the person was getting a State Pension or any benefits.

What documents you get from the registrar

You’ll get a:

  • Certificate for Burial or Cremation (the ‘green form’) - give this to the funeral director
  • Certificate of Registration of Death (form BD8) - you complete this if the person was claiming benefits or had a state pension

Getting a death certificate

You need a death certificate to sort out the person’s money, property and possessions.

You can buy one or more death certificates at this time (the cost varies between register offices).
