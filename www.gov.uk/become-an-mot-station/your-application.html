Part 2: Your application

Authorised Examiner (AE status)

You must have AE status to set up an MOT test station.

An AE can be an individual, partnership or company authorised by the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA).

The AE is responsible for making sure that:

  • the test facilities and equipment are checked and well-maintained
  • the Nominated Testers are assessed correctly and complete training and quality checks
  • VOSA staff have access to the premises for checks on staff and equipment
  • MOT documents are correctly stored and access to electronic MOT test systems is only given to eligible users
  • MOT tests are properly conducted
  • VOSA is informed about significant changes to the business within 7 working days

There’s more information about the AE’s responsibilities in VOSA’s guide to becoming an AE.

Download ‘So you want to become an Authorised Examiner?’ (PDF, 361KB)

To apply for AE status, send form VT01 to VOSA. Use the same form if you already have AE status and want to open a test station. The guide to requirements for authorisation of AEs tells you more about the application process.

Download ‘Form VT01 - application for AE status / opening an MOT test station’ (PDF, 274KB)

Download ‘Requirements for authorisation of AEs’ (PDF, 114KB)

Send the form to your local VOSA office.

If your application is refused, VOSA will write to you - you can appeal the decision and ask for a hearing by writing to VOSA within 14 days.

AE status doesn’t transfer with a business. If you’re buying an existing MOT station, you need to apply for AE status in your own right.

Changes to your company

You may need to apply for AE status again if:

  • your company is reconstructed in a way that means it’s given a new company registration number
  • a partner leaves or joins a partnership

Premises and equipment

Different classes of vehicle require different test equipment and testing bay sizes. You’ll need to make sure you have at least the minimum level of acceptable equipment for each vehicle class you’re approved to test.

Download ‘Requirements for authorisation for a vehicle testing station’ (PDF, 47KB)

You can ask for your premises to be approved in principle when applying for AE status. This can help you avoid committing to expensive work or alterations before your premises are approved.

If you’ve got AE status but want to make changes to the test facilities, write to VOSA before any changes are made, with supporting drawings, to show that the changes won’t affect the testing station’s approval.
