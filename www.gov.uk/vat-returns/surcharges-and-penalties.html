Part 6: Surcharges and penalties

If your VAT Return or payment is late it’s known as a ‘default’.

If you default more than once in 12 months you may enter a ‘surcharge period’. Defaulting in a surcharge period means:

  • the surcharge period is extended by 12 months
  • you may have to pay a penalty (known as a ‘surcharge’)

If you default, HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) will write explaining any penalties and what happens if you default again.

Penalties

Penalties are usually a percentage of the unpaid tax. HMRC estimate this if they don’t have your return - this is known as an ‘assessment’.

Default Penalty if turnover is less than £150,000 Penalty if turnover is £150,000 or more
1st No penalty but if you default again within 12 months you enter a surcharge period No penalty but you enter a surcharge period
2nd No penalty 2% (or no penalty if you owe less than £400)
3rd 2% (or no penalty if you owe less than £400) 5% (or no penalty if you owe less than £400)
4th 5% (or no penalty if you owe less than £400) 10%
5th 10% 15%
6 or more 15% 15%

Exceptions

There’s no penalty if you send a late VAT Return and you have no tax to pay or you’re due a VAT repayment.

Penalties for incorrect VAT Returns

If your VAT Return is inaccurate HMRC can charge you a penalty (between 15% and 100% of the unpaid owed).

Download ‘Penalties for inaccurate tax returns’ (PDF, 200KB)

Interest

You may be charged interest on any tax you owe.

Penalties for sending a paper VAT Return

If you have to send your return online, you’ll be charged a penalty if you send a paper return.
