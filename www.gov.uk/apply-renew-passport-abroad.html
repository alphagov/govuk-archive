Quick answer Apply for or renew your passport if you're abroad or live outside the UK

Not what you're looking for? ↓

How you apply for or renew a British passport when you’re overseas depends on which country you’re in.

You must apply to the Identity and Passport Service (IPS) in the UK if you’re in any of the following countries:

  • Austria
  • Bosnia and Herzegovina
  • Bulgaria
  • Croatia
  • Denmark
  • Estonia
  • Finland
  • Iceland
  • Latvia
  • Lithuania
  • Macedonia
  • Montenegro
  • Norway
  • Serbia
  • Slovakia
  • Slovenia
  • Sweden

If you’re in any other country, you must apply to the Foreign and Commonwealth Office - choose the country where you’re applying from to find out what to do.

How long it takes

Renewing a British passport takes at least 4 weeks from when the IPS gets your application.

It will take at least 6 weeks if you’re:

  • applying for a first British adult or child passport
  • replacing a lost or stolen passport

Applications may take longer if the IPS needs to ask you for more information or documents or the photographs you send are rejected.

You may have to attend an interview for a first passport or if you’re replacing a lost or stolen passport. The IPS will tell you if you do.

Cost

You’ll have to pay the fee for your passport plus a courier fee of £19.86 - this pays for your supporting documents to be sent back to you securely.

Passport type Passport fee Total to pay (including courier fee)
Adult standard 32-page passport £128.00 £147.86
Adult jumbo 48-page passport £154.50 £174.36
Child passport £81.50 £101.36

Fill in a ‘Paying by credit card or debit card’ form for each passport you’re applying for and send it with your application. Your credit or debit card will be charged in sterling.

You can use most major credit or debit cards - but not American Express.

Download ‘Paying by credit card or debit card’ (PDF, 803KB)

How to apply

  1. Download the application form.

  2. Use the ‘Applying for a passport if you’re outside the UK’ guidance notes to help you fill in the application form.

  3. Include 2 identical new photos of you (or your child, if it’s a child passport application). Follow the rules about passport photos or your application may be delayed.

  4. Get your application form and 1 photo countersigned - unless you’re renewing your passport and your appearance hasn’t changed since the last one was issued.

  5. Check which supporting documents you must send with your application (see table under ‘Supporting documents’ below).

Download ‘British passport overseas application form’ (PDF, 355KB) Download ‘Applying for a passport if you’re outside the UK’ (PDF, 214KB)

Send your application

The IPS recommends you send your application using a secure delivery service or courier.


Identity and Passport Service
OVS-L
101 Old Hall Street
Liverpool
L3 9BP

Getting your passport

Your passport will be delivered by courier. Your supporting documents will be sent back separately.

Supporting documents

If you’re renewing a British passport, download the supporting documents guidance for the country you’re in.

If you’re applying for a first British passport, download the supporting documents guidance for the country where you were born.

For example, if you’re living in France but were born in Algeria, download the supporting documents for Algeria - not France.
