Licence Licence to manufacture, assemble or import medicines

Apply for this licence

Get started on the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) website

Overview

You require a manufacturer’s licence to manufacture or assemble licensed medicinal products for human use in the UK, to export products or to import licensed medicinal products for human use from countries outside the European Economic Area.

Licences are obtained from the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA).

Applications must be made in the format required by MHRA and include any required samples, documents and information. They must include a description of the medicinal products that are to be licensed. This description should either be an appropriate general classification or by a description of the actual product.

The MHRA will take into account the operations to be carried out under the licence and the premises where these will take place, the equipment to be used, the qualifications of the supervisor of operations and arrangements for maintaining and keeping records or products to be manufactured or assembled.

The MHRA will decide your application within 90 days.

Conditions

A licence may be granted subject to provisions. You will be served a notice if your application is refused or if your licence is granted subject to provisions that were not in your application. You may make representations in relation to any such notice within the time frame allowed and subject to conditions, eg you must provide a written summary of any oral representations you will make and provide copies of supporting information.

If the MHRA wish to reject your application in relation to questions on the safety, quality or efficiency of a product they must refer your application to a committee, known as an appropriate committee. If the committee can’t advise they will notify you of this. You can make representations to the committee if this occurs within a time allowed by them. If you intend to give oral representations you must provide a written summary of these representations and any documents you intend to use to support you within six months of the date you were notified.

After any representations have been made and the committee has told the MHRA of its decision the MHRA will advise you of the result.

You must hold a product licence to be able to hold a manufacturer’s licence or the products to be manufactured must be by order of a product licence holder, or by the sponsor of a clinical trial where the products in question are to be used.

Licences will apply for either five years from the date it was granted or until it is either revoked by the MHRA or surrendered by you for products which are governed by EC Directive 2001/83/EC relating to medicinal products for human use.

You may be served with a notice from the MHRA requesting further information. You must provide this information. You must also notify the MHRA when a product is placed on the market within two years from the date of your licence being granted.

Your licence may be revoked, suspended or varied if it is found you provided false or incomplete information in your application, you have breached any provisions in the licence, there has been a change of circumstances in any of the provisions of your licence or you have failed to comply with a notice to provide further information.

Fines and penalties

If you manufacture, assemble, export or import products without a licence you may commit a criminal offence and be fined, imprisoned for up to two years or both. If you fail to provide information when requested by notice you may commit an offence and be fined up to £1,000.

You may apply to have your licence varied. Applications must be in writing, specify the required variation include any information in support. You must also include any required fee.
