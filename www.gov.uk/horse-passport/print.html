Getting and using a horse passport, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

All horses, ponies and donkeys must have a horse passport.

You must have it with you at all times when you’re with your horse, unless it’s in a stable or grazing in a field.

You (or the horse’s main keeper) may have to show the passport to a Trading Standards inspector or an animal health officer.

You can be fined up to £5,000 if you don’t have a valid horse passport.

Part 2: Apply for a horse passport

To apply for a passport you must be the owner of the horse.

Get an application form

You can get an application form from an authorised Passport Issuing Organisation (PIO).

Download a list of Passport Issuing Organisations that manage studbooks (PDF, 480KB)

Download a list of Passport Issuing Organisations that do not manage studbooks (PDF, 280KB)

Identify your horse

Get a vet to microchip your horse - you may also need a diagram of the horse (‘silhouette’). The PIO will let you know if it’s needed.

A foal must be microchipped and have a passport before it’s 6 months old or by 31 December in the year it’s born, whichever is later.

The passport is issued

Your horse gets a ‘Unique Equine Life Number’ which is recorded in the passport and added to the National Equine Database.

You need a passport for each horse you own.

Part 3: Using your horse passport

Medication your horse is given - Section IX of the passport

Horses can be declared as ‘intended’ or ‘not intended’ for the human food chain in Section IX of its passport.

Your vet must record any medication or injections your horse is given in Section IX. This affects what happens to the horse when it dies.

Selling or buying a horse

If you sell your horse, give the passport to the new owner - if you don’t, you could be prosecuted.

If you buy a horse, let the PIO know you’re the new owner within 30 days.

If someone tries to sell you a horse without a passport, contact your local Trading Standards office.

Importing or exporting a horse

If you import a horse from a European Union (EU) country, it needs a valid passport issued by an authorised PIO in the EU.

If the PIO is unauthorised, or there’s no passport, you must get a passport from an authorised PIO in the UK.

If you’re importing or exporting a horse from outside the EU, contact the Horse Passports office.
