Expenses and benefits: tips and service charges, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer, you might have to pay tax and National Insurance on tips or service charges that employees get from customers. You don’t have to report them to HMRC.

The tax you have to pay depends on:

  • whether the customer chooses to leave money or pays a compulsory service charge
  • how the money is then given to staff

Money can either be given to staff directly or it can go into a central fund called a ‘tronc’. Someone is appointed to be the ‘troncmaster’ and they divide up the tronc between employees.

You must tell HMRC who the troncmaster is so that they can set up a PAYE scheme for the tronc.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to pay tax and National Insurance if your employees receive tips directly from your customers and they’re allowed to keep them.

Part 3: What to report and pay

If tips aren’t exempt, you may have to pay tax and National Insurance on them.

What you need to pay depends on the type of payment (compulsory or voluntary) and how it’s distributed.

If employees receive money from compulsory service charges

This counts as earnings, so:

  • add it to your employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

Tips and voluntary service charges you distribute to employees

How money is distributed What to pay
You decide how the money is divided and pay employees Add the cost to the employee’s other earnings and deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Another person decides how the money is divided, but you pay employees Add the cost to the employee’s other earnings and deduct and pay PAYE tax (but not Class 1 National Insurance) through payroll

Tips and voluntary service charges from a tronc

You must tell HMRC who the troncmaster is so that they can set up a PAYE scheme for the tronc.

How money is divided What to pay
You decide how the tronc money is divided Troncmaster pays PAYE tax and you pay National Insurance
The troncmaster decides how the money is divided Troncmaster pays PAYE tax
