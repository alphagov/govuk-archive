Quick answer Correct a birth, marriage or death certificate

Contact the register office or religious building where the event was registered to correct errors on a certificate or record.

You can search for a register office by postcode or location.

Information on certificates and records

Certificates show the information held on the official record of a birth, marriage or death.

A certificate or record can only be changed when the information on it is wrong.

Changes can’t be made to show new information if your circumstances have changed after the event. For example, if you have changed your name after your child was born.

When a mistake is corrected, the original information will always be shown on the record but a note will be added saying why the change was made.

Application forms and guidance

You may be told by your local register office that you need to contact the General Register Office (GRO) to correct the mistake.

Fees for corrections

If a copying or administration error was made when the certificate was issued, whoever issued the certificate will normally replace it free of charge.

For other changes you might need to pay a fee if you want a new certificate to be issued.
