Entry Level Stewardship

How to apply for the ELS scheme or the Uplands ELS scheme, to get funding for good environmental management of farmland in England.

Introduction

Entry Level Stewardship (ELS) is one element of the Environmental Stewardship scheme delivered by Natural England on behalf of the Department for Food, Environment and Rural Affairs (Defra) and is open to all farmers, land managers and tenants. It is a voluntary scheme, designed to deliver significant environmental benefits in high priority areas.

ELS requires a basic level of environmental management, and participants can choose from a wide range of more than 80 management options. These cover all farming types including uplands - for example, hedgerow management, stone wall maintenance, low input grassland, buffer strips and various arable options.

Environmental Stewardship is a part of RDPE - the Rural Development Programme for England (2007-2013).

This guide explains the main aims of Environmental Stewardship, how ELS works, how to apply, where to get application forms and further guidance.

Aims of Environmental Stewardship

ELS is the basic level of the Environmental Stewardship scheme delivered by Natural England. The scheme encourages effective environmental management across a range of farmland, providing funding to farmers and other land managers in England who wish to join the scheme.

The main objectives of Environmental Stewardship are:

  • conserving wildlife and maintaining biodiversity
  • maintaining and enhancing the quality and character of the natural landscape
  • protecting the historic environment and natural resources
  • promoting public access and understanding of the countryside
  • protecting natural resources

The secondary objectives of Environmental Stewardship are genetic conservation - including traditional breeds of cattle and varieties of fruit trees - and flood management. Environmental Stewardship also has a general aim of helping the natural environment to adapt to climate change by, for example reducing greenhouse gas emissions and providing and protecting carbon storage.

For more information, see the guides on Environmental Stewardship: the basics, Higher Level Stewardship (HLS) and Organic Entry Level Stewardship (OELS).

How ELS works

ELS is voluntary and requires farmers and land managers to manage land according to specified environmental standards in return for a set payment per hectare.

You must deliver 30 points-worth of options per hectare of land covered by the agreement. There are more than 80 options, including:

  • hedgerow management
  • stone wall maintenance
  • low input grassland
  • buffer strips
  • arable options
  • uplands options

Points are awarded for the different options, and these are used to calculate ELS payments.

ELS payments

Natural England makes a payment of £30 per hectare per year for all land entered into the scheme, provided participants deliver 30 points worth of options per hectare. An exception is for parcels of 15 hectares or moreabove the Moorland Line - the line used to distinguish payment levels for farming support schemes - for which a payment of £8 per hectare per year is made.

If you apply for ELS, you must complete a Farm Environmental Record (FER) of your land’s environmental features. Completing a FER earns three points per hectare.

ELS agreements

You are guaranteed an ELS agreement as long as you deliver enough points from your land management options and meet all the conditions of the scheme.

ELS agreements last five years and run from the monthly start date of the month after the application is processed. Payments are sent out automatically, with a partial payment in the autumn and an annual payment between January to June (depending on your agreement start date).

The third edition of the ELS handbook, published in 2010, governs agreements that started on or after 1 February 2010. You can access the third edition ELS Handbook on the Natural England website.

Agreements that started before October 2008 are governed by the first edition of the ELS handbook, published in 2005. Agreements starting from October 2008 onwards are covered by the second edition, published in 2008.

Download the first edition of the ELS handbook from the Natural England website (PDF, 1.23MB).

You can access the second edition of the ELS handbook on the Natural England website.

Some ELS management options were revised and new ones introduced in May 2009. These are covered in the Addendum to the second edition handbook.

Download Natural England’s new and revised ELS management options from the Agricultural Document Library website (PDF, 123KB).

Uplands ELS

In 2010, Uplands ELS replaced the compensatory Hill Farm Allowance (HFA) with a scheme that rewards farmers for maintaining and improving the upland landscape and environment in England’s Severely Disadvantaged Areas.

To be part of the Uplands ELS scheme you will need to use simple and effective environmental management to maintain and improve the biodiversity, natural resources, landscape and historical value of the uplands and contribute to climate change mitigation and adaptation. For more information about how you can apply, see the guide on Uplands ELS](http://whitehall-admin.production.alphagov.co.uk/government/admin/specialist-guides/4073).

If your land is under a Countryside Stewardship Scheme or Environmentally Sensitive Area agreement, you are not eligible for Uplands ELS, instead you are eligible for the Uplands Transitional Payment (UTP), provided you received HFA payments in 2010. For information on how to apply for UTP, see the UTP section in the guide on Uplands ELS.

Cross Compliance

The Cross Compliance requirements of the Single Payment Scheme also apply to ES agreements, whether or not you are also claiming the Single Farm Payment.

You can find out more about Cross Compliance in the guides on Cross Compliance: the basics, statutory management requirements (SMR) and standards of good agricultural and environmental conditions (GAEC).

Nitrate Vulnerable Zones

If your land is in an Nitrate Vulnerable Zones (NVZ), you must make sure that you meet the rules of both the NVZ Action Programme and ELS. Where the rules overlap, you must meet those that are more demanding. For more information, see the guide on NVZs.

Applying for ELS

You must satisfy certain criteria before you apply for an ELS agreement.

ELS is open to farmers and land managers who are:

  • freehold owners
  • tenants
  • contractual licensees
  • common land or shared grazing rights holders, on behalf of whom an appropriate individual is willing to sign an Environmental Stewardship agreement and be responsible for maintaining it

Any land entered into an ELS agreement must first be registered on the Rural Land Register (RLR) - a digital record of all farmed land in England - and must be part of the farmed environment. You can register land on the RLR by contacting the Rural Payments Agency (RPA).

You also need a RPA vendor number and a County Parish Holding number. You will have these if you have already claimed payments under other programmes - such as the Single Payment Scheme (SPS). If you do not have these, you will need to apply to the RPA.

When you complete your ELS and SPS forms, you must make sure that the ELS options and SPS codes you use are compatible.

ELS application forms

To get an ELS application form, you will need to contact your local Natural England office. You can also apply online.

However, you can find many of the supplementary forms via the index to Environmental Stewardship forms on the Natural England website.

Find contact details for Natural England offices on the Natural England website.

FER preparation

When applying, you must include a FER map of the environmental features on your land. These features must be maintained throughout the agreement.

You can find the detailed advice on preparing your FER and an example FER map in Annex 1 to section 2 of the Entry Level Stewardship Handbook 2010. You find the different sections of the handbook on the Natural England website.

Applying for ELS online

ELS online is a secure software package that enables you to prepare and submit your ELS application electronically. It can be used by individual applicants and agents submitting applications on behalf of their clients.You can also use ELS Online to renew an existing agreement.

You can find information on ELS Online on the Whole Farm Approach section of the Defra website.

You can download ELS online software guidance from the Defra website (PDF, 1.03MB).

Applying for ELS online has several benefits, including:

  • less time required to complete applications
  • faster processing of your application
  • customer support from a dedicated online team
  • user friendly and time-saving software
  • multiple online applications possible
  • you can alter your applications before submission

To find out more, you can contact the ELS online team. Find contact details for the ELS online team on the Defra website.

Further ELS guidance

Detailed guidance on ELS is available to download from the Natural England website.

ELS management options

You can also find a list of environmental management options in the edition of the ELS handbook applicable to the year of your agreement.

You can access sections of the third edition of the ES handbook on the Natural England website.

Changes to your agreement

You should be able to carry out the options and management required under your agreement without difficulty. However, you may need to change the choice or location of your management options due to an unforeseen and serious situation - for example, flooding or a severe and prolonged weed infestation.

In these cases, you can make an amendment to your agreement. However, it is important that there is continuity of management so that the environmental benefits are achieved. Amendments are therefore only to be used for changes that are absolutely necessary.

Find out how to make changes to your ELS agreement in the ELS handbook. You can read the ELS handbook on the Natural England website.

Breaches and appeals

You will be in breach of your ELS agreement if you fail to comply with any agreement conditions or make false or misleading statements in your application or other correspondence.

You can find out more about ELS breaches and appeals procedures in the ELS handbook. You can read the ELS handbook on the Natural England website.

Additional ELS forms

ELS applicants or agreement holders may need to use additional forms as part of their ELS application or agreement management.

The agent authorisation form allows applicants or agreement holders to appoint an agent to act on their behalf. Download an ELS agent authorisation form from the Natural England website (PDF, 198KB).

If your ELS application includes common land, you should fill in a common land and shared grazing supplementary application form. Download a common land supplementary application form from the Natural England website (PDF, 70KB).

You may need to ask Natural England for a derogation - this is a minor and temporary change to an aspect of your ELS management agreement, needed on a single occasion. To do so, you must fill in a derogation notice. Download a derogation notice form from the Natural England website (PDF, 96KB).

Registration with other schemes

To find out more about transferring land to ELS from existing agreements, you can see the guide on Environmental Stewardship: the basics.
