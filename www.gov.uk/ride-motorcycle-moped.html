Part 1: Overview

If you want to ride a motorcycle on the road you first need to get a provisional driving licence and complete compulsory basic training (CBT).

If you want to ride a moped you first need to complete CBT unless you passed your full car driving test before 1 February 2001. In this case you can ride a moped, but still need to complete CBT to ride a motorcycle.

Once you’ve completed CBT you have 2 years to pass your full motorcycle theory and practical tests or you’ll have to complete CBT again.

Rules for learner riders

When you’re learning you’ll need to have the right provisional driving licence. This depends on whether you want to ride a moped or motorcycle.

If you’re using your own vehicle you’ll need to make sure it:

  • has a valid V5C registration certificate (log book)
  • is taxed
  • has an MOT (if needed)

You’ll also need adequate motor insurance.

Taking the full motorcycle tests

All riders have to pass the theory test before taking the motorcycle practical test.

You can take the practical test on a motorcycle of between 75cc and 125cc.

The licence you’ll get depends on the type of bike you take the test on:

  • a light motorcycle licence (A1), which restricts you to ride bikes of up to 125cc and with a power output of 11kW
  • a standard motorcycle licence (A), if you take the test on a bike of over 120cc but not more than 125cc and capable of traveling at least 62mph

If you get the standard motorcycle licence you’ll be restricted for 2 years to riding bikes of up to 25kW power output and a power/weight ratio not exceeding 0.16kW/kg.

After the 2 years you can then ride any size of bike.

If you’re 21 or over, or will reach 21 before this 2 year restriction ends, you have other options under the ‘direct access’ and ‘accelerated access’ rules.

More information

For further details download the DVLA’s flowchart, which gives step-by-step instructions on how to get a moped or motorcycle licence.
