Licence Art therapist registration

Apply for this licence

Start now on the Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC) website

Overview

To practise as an art therapist in the UK, you need to register with the Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC).

This includes using any of the following protected titles:

  • art psychotherapist
  • art therapist
  • drama therapist
  • music therapist

To be eligible to apply for registration, you must:

  • hold an approved qualification
  • be in good physical and mental health
  • be of good character

You must enclose a fee and any necessary supporting documents with your application (eg qualifications and a character reference.)

The registration fee for the 2 year registration cycle is £152 (£76 per year) and there is a one off non-refundable scrutiny fee of £53. The registration fee is reduced by 50% if you successfully completed a UK approved programme within the last 2 years.

If your application is successful, your name will be added to the register and you’ll receive a registration certificate and authentication card.

Conditions

You must undertake continuing professional development (CPD) activities as a condition of your registration. You must keep a written record of these activities, including any supporting evidence, and submit this for inspection by the HCPC on request.

If the HCPC isn’t satisfied that you’re meeting your CPD obligations, your name may be removed from the register.

Fines and penalties

It’s a criminal offence to call yourself an art psychotherapist, art therapist, drama therapist or a music therapist if you’re not registered with the HCPC. If you do so, you may be fined up to £5,000.
