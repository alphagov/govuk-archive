The Days at Sea Scheme: Cod Recovery Zone

How the Days at Sea Scheme affects cod fishing vessels operating in the Cod Recovery Zone, potential exemptions and penalties.

Introduction

The Days at Sea Scheme is a UK initiative restricting the number of days per year that vessels can fish in the Cod Recovery Zone (CRZ). The aim of the scheme is to allow cod and sole stocks to recover in this area, improving long-term sustainability, and allows the UK to comply with EU Regulation 1342/2008.

Under the scheme, if you wish to operate a fishing vessel in the CRZ you will need to apply for an annual allocation of days you may fish, according to the type of fishing gear you use. The scheme is administered in the UK by the Marine Management Organisation (MMO).

This guide explains how the Days at Sea Scheme may affect your cod fishing vessel, potential exemptions and penalties.

Notification of intention to carry regulated gears

As the master of a vessel (or their representative), in order to receive your days at sea allocation you must notify the local MMO coastal office as to which categories of regulated gear the vessel will carry in the forthcoming period. You should make an application before the first day of each management period. The port office will provide you with the application form.

An application to transfer in days for a vessel which does not have eligibility in its own right will also count as notification.

A management period can be a period of one or more months.

If you apply for a new management period that ends the previous management period with a deficit (that is, where the allocated days have been used before the end of the management period), no allocation will be made for the new period until more days are transferred in to eliminate the deficit.

For example, if a fisherman is allocated 135 days for TR1 gears (whitefish gear) for 12 months, but uses them in 10 months and then opts to begin a new management period using a different gear type, the previous management period will have ended with a deficit of 22.5 days. Therefore, no allocation can be made for the remaining two months of the original 12-month period until more days are transferred in.

Any days remaining at the end of a management period will not be transferred into the new management period. Days not used by the end of a management period are therefore lost.

Vessels are allowed to carry only one type of regulated gear at any time.

Regulated gears

The regulated gears are listed in Annex 1 to EC Regulation 1342/2008. You can read the regulation on the Eur-LEX website.

To be eligible to use them, you must also have a record of fishing activity with those gears at some point during the years 2001-05 or inherited eligibility by a single licence transfer or aggregation from a vessel or vessels with a track record, where this has been agreed.

Vessels eligible to notify gear category TR1 may carry any category of regulated gear.

For advice, you can call the Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team - Days at Sea Scheme on 0191 376 2596 or email them at daysatsea.transferrequests@marinemanagement.org.uk

Days at sea allocation

The MMO Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team will send letters to eligible vessels giving their allocation of days for the management period. You should carry this letter on board at all times when fishing in the CRZ with regulated gears. The letter will also specify areas where your vessel may fish.

You should apply for an allocation for a single grouping of regulated gears, such as TR1 and TR2, or a combination. If you notify for more than one category of gear, your allocation of days will be the average of the rates for the categories notified.

Vessels will receive an allocation of days in proportion to the duration of the management period notified.

Vessels catching less than 5% cod

If you agree to catch less than 5% cod when notifying any regulated gear, you will be allocated additional days at sea. If you make any trip that involves activity within the CRZ for all or part of the trip, the 5% limit on cod catches will need to be observed.

You will be allocated 200 days and can choose to use any combination you wish if notifying multiple gears. For example 150 TR2 days and 50 TR1 days or 100 TR2 days and 100 TR1 days, or any other variation.

Fishermen wanting to transit through the CRZ to fish outside it must lash and stow their gear whilst in the CRZ (both there and back).

A vessel will revert to the standard allocation for the rest of the scheme year if:

  • on one occasion it lands more than 5% cod by live weight
  • an inspection at sea records a catch of cod that is 5% or more of the total catch

You could be required to transfer days if your vessel has already exceeded the standard allocation.

Allocation of additional days at sea (buy backs)

You can also apply to receive extra allocated days if you agree to fish exclusively using specified gear during the management period. The options are:

  • the Eliminator trawl – available to TR1 vessels and vessels using this receive an extra 24 days allocation
  • the Orkney trawl – available to TR1 vessels and vessels using this receive an extra 20 days allocation
  • 130 millimetres cod end – available to TR1 vessels carrying gear with a cod end of mesh size 130 millimetres or greater, and vessels using this receive an extra 8 days allocation
  • nephrops square mesh panel (SMP) – the insertion of a 120 mm SMP of minimum length 3 metres in the straight extension of the net or a 130 mm SMP in the taper where the rearmost row of the meshes of the SMP shall be no more that 12 metres from the cod line, available to TR2 vessels and vessels adopting this will receive an extra 8 days allocation

You can call the Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team - Days at Sea Scheme on 0191 376 2596 or email them at daysatsea.transferrequests@marinemanagement.org.uk

Definitions of the Eliminator trawl and the Orkney trawl are in the Days at Sea Scheme 2012/13: Cod Recovery Zone scheme rules (PDF 234KB)

Transferring days at sea

Transfers of days at sea are subject to controls. They are also limited to a set percentage of a vessel’s allocation - 20% for TR1 and TR2 gears and 30% for other gears. This means that:

  • any vessel that benefits from the extra 5% cod entitlement can transfer days in but not transfer days out
  • a vessel donating days to another can only donate up to that percentage of its allocation of days to other vessels
  • once days are transferred to a vessel they may not be transferred out (this is to prevent the accumulation of days onto individual vessels as a form of banking – however, the MMO will consider allowing such transfers out in some circumstances)
  • recipient vessels are not restricted in terms of how many days they can receive
  • transfers of effort will only be allowed where the donor and recipient vessels are both administered by the MMO
  • for vessels receiving extra days at sea by complying with special conditions (such as the 5% limit on cod catches), they can only transfer days from vessels that are operating under the same conditions
  • vessels that are ineligible to carry regulated gears may transfer in days from eligible vessels to allow them to fish in the CRZ

Where a transfer takes place, the number of days received by the recipient will be adjusted in proportion to its fishing capacity (measured in engine power) compared to the other vessel.

You can call the Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team - Days at Sea Scheme on 0191 376 2596 or email them at daysatsea.transferrequests@marinemanagement.org.uk

Penalties for breaching days at sea rules

Penalties for breaching the days at sea scheme rules are as follows:

  • fishing in an area of seasonal closure, when in effect – a deduction of 7 days
  • fishing in an area of real time closure, when in effect – a deduction of 5 days
  • carrying more than one category of regulated gear onboard – a deduction of 5 days for each occasion
  • fishing in an area of advised cod avoidance after notifying the MMO that you will not fish in these areas – on each occasion a deduction of days awarded in relation to avoidance of area in the management period, including any days used from that additional allocation
  • failing to fish exclusively with specified gear after notifying the MMO that you will – on each occasion a deduction of days awarded in relation to use of gear in the management period, including any days used from that additional allocation
  • failing to accept an observer on board in response to a reasonable request – on the first occasion there is no penalty, on each subsequent occasion, a deduction of 5 days

The Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team will give consideration to any exceptional circumstances.

If the team is considering applying a penalty, it will notify you in writing of the intention to impose the penalty, and the reasons for this.

On receipt of this notification, you may appeal. Such appeals should in the first instance be lodged with the team. However, any appeal will be considered by a senior officer within the MMO outside the team.

You can call the Fisheries Management and Effort Control Team - Days at Sea Scheme on 0191 376 2596 or email them at daysatsea.transferrequests@marinemanagement.org.uk
