Part 1: Your rights

Starting a family

As a disabled person, you have the right to make your own mind up about becoming a parent.

You shouldn’t be denied fertility treatment because you’re disabled.

Your health and well-being are factors in adoption assessments but you mustn’t be automatically disqualified because you are disabled.

Your child(ren)

Your child shouldn’t be identified as ‘in need’ because you’re disabled (although this could be a factor). A child-in-need assessment should only be carried out if there are concerns about your child’s well-being.

A ‘child in need’ is a child under 17 whose health and development is likely to be made significantly worse without support.

The Human Rights Act 1998 is relevant to disabled parents, eg:

  • Article 12: The right to marry and to found a family - social care services and health providers should take all reasonable steps to help you have a family including giving advice and support before and after your child is born
