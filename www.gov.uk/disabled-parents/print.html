Disabled parents, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Your rights

Starting a family

As a disabled person, you have the right to make your own mind up about becoming a parent.

You shouldn’t be denied fertility treatment because you’re disabled.

Your health and well-being are factors in adoption assessments but you mustn’t be automatically disqualified because you are disabled.

Your child(ren)

Your child shouldn’t be identified as ‘in need’ because you’re disabled (although this could be a factor). A child-in-need assessment should only be carried out if there are concerns about your child’s well-being.

A ‘child in need’ is a child under 17 whose health and development is likely to be made significantly worse without support.

The Human Rights Act 1998 is relevant to disabled parents, eg:

  • Article 12: The right to marry and to found a family - social care services and health providers should take all reasonable steps to help you have a family including giving advice and support before and after your child is born

Part 2: Benefits and help

You could get extra help at home as a disabled parent. Ask for a health and social care assessment to work out what help you need. You can get the assessment before you have your child.

If you get Disability Living Allowance (DLA), the amount of benefit you’re entitled to may change if you have a child. Contact the Disability Living Allowance helpline.

Disability Living Allowance helpline
Telephone: 08457 123 456
Textphone: 08457 22 44 33

Your local council must make sure disabled people are supported in their family and parental roles. They can support you in several areas, eg visiting your child’s school.

Equipment

The Disabled Living Foundation has information on equipment for disabled parents.

Parenting classes

Parenting UK holds classes on the skills you’ll need as a disabled parent. Classes must be accessible.
