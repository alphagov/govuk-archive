Part 3: Residential Property Tribunal: council homes and park homes

The Residential Property Tribunal can help solve disagreements about:

  • council homes
  • park homes (static caravans or mobile homes)

Council homes

You can appeal to the tribunal if you disagree with a decision about your council home, eg to:

  • reuse an empty building
  • improve parts of a building
  • demolish or close a building
  • make a property safe
  • restrict how many people live in a property

You can also appeal if you’re refused the ‘Right to Buy’ your council or housing association home because it’s suitable for elderly people.

Park homes

You can appeal to the tribunal if you disagree with a decision about your park home - eg to:

  • change or end a residence agreement
  • change the pitch fee
  • move a park home
  • repair a park home
  • transfer a park home to someone else

Appealing to the tribunal

To make an appeal to the tribunal, you have to fill in an application form.

The forms are listed under the headings:

  • ‘Housing Act appeals and applications’
  • ‘Right to Buy appeals’
  • ‘Park homes’

Send the form to your local rent assessment panel.

Cost of appealing to the tribunal

You may have to pay a £150 fee for some cases. The application form will tell you if you have to pay a fee.

You can pay by postal order or cheque (made payable to ‘HM Courts & Tribunals Service’).

Help paying costs

If you’re on benefits, like Income Support, you can ask for help with your fees, using the form below:

What happens after you send your appeal

The tribunal may ask you for permission to inspect the property or park home.

You may have to go to a hearing with the council or site owner.

If there isn’t a hearing, the tribunal will ask you to send written evidence.

The tribunal will send you a letter giving its decision, usually shortly after any hearing.

If you disagree with the tribunal’s decision

You may be able to appeal against the decision to a higher tribunal, called the Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber).

To do this, you have to ask the Residential Property Tribunal for permission to appeal, using the form below:

Download ‘Application to the Residential Property Tribunal for permission to appeal a decision to the Upper Tribunal (Lands Chamber)’ (DOC, 123KB)

You must send this form to your local rent assessment panel within 21 days of getting its decision.
