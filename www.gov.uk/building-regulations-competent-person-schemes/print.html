Competent person scheme, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you work in the building trade as an installer, you can join a competent person scheme to self-certify certain types of building work. This is instead of getting building regulations approval.

This means:

  • you don’t have to use a local council or approved building inspector to check your work
  • your customers benefit from lower prices as there are no building control fees

Membership is voluntary - you can still use local councils and building inspectors if you prefer.

Competent person scheme operators and installers must follow certain rules to make sure that their work complies with building regulations.

Download ‘Competent person schemes - conditions of authorisation’ (PDF, 60KB)

How to join a scheme

Contact one of the relevant schemes. They each have different application processes, rules and fees.

Before you’re approved, you’ll be assessed to make sure you meet the level of competence required. If you become a member, there will be ongoing inspections of your work to make sure standards are met.

Types of building work

Air pressure testing of buildings

The authorised scheme is BINDT. It has 2 parts and you can join either - one is for dwellings only and one is for any type of building.

To test any type of building you need to be a member of the Air Tightness testing and Measurement Association.

Cavity wall insulation in an existing building

The authorised scheme is CIGA.

Part 3: How schemes are authorised

The Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) is in charge of authorising competent person schemes.

To make sure that the standards are consistent, there are rules that scheme operators must follow and conditions they must meet.

Download ‘Competent person schemes - conditions of authorisation’ (PDF, 60KB)

DCLG is currently considering 13 applications to run new schemes or extend existing ones. Approved schemes will begin in 2013.
